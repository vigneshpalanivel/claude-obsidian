# Research: Running a Software Dev Company Post-Vibe-Coding

**Research date**: 2026-04-15 | **Rounds**: 2 | **Searches**: 7 | **Sources fetched**: 6

---

## Overview

The "vibe coding" era — [[Andrej Karpathy]]'s term for LLM-driven development where developers describe and accept AI-generated code — has matured into [[Agentic Engineering]], where engineers orchestrate autonomous agent workflows. This reshapes every layer of running a software development company: team structure, hiring criteria, delivery processes, client relationships, and pricing models.

The core shift: **a smaller team with better judgment now out-delivers a larger team of traditional coders.** The competitive advantage moved from headcount to leverage.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. The Paradigm Has Already Shifted

- 90% of engineering teams use AI coding tools as of end-2025 (Source: [[Jellyfish-2025-Engineering-Management]])
- 25% of Y Combinator Winter 2025 startups had 95%+ AI-generated codebases
- Claude Code became the #1 AI coding tool within 8 months of release (May 2025)
- "Vibe coding" is passé; Karpathy's current term is "agentic engineering" — orchestrating multi-step agent workflows (Source: [[Karpathy-2025-Year-in-Review]])
- By mid-2026, agentic AI handles more than 50% of routine coding tasks

### 2. Hire for Judgment, Not Code Speed

Raw coding ability is no longer the primary differentiator. The six capabilities that matter for AI-native engineers (Source: [[Augment-Code-AI-Native-Hiring]]):

1. Product & outcome taste (defining the right problem)
2. System & architectural judgment (evaluating long-term tradeoffs)
3. Agent leverage (delegating effectively to AI)
4. Communication & collaboration (reaching clarity fast)
5. Ownership & leadership (end-to-end accountability)
6. Learning velocity & experimental mindset (adapting as tools evolve)

Practical implication: interview for these six, not algorithmic coding speed. Use take-home projects that involve building with AI, reviewing AI-generated output, and making architectural decisions under ambiguity.

### 3. Team Structure: Smaller, More Leveraged

- Gartner: 80% of large engineering teams will reorganize into smaller AI-augmented units by 2030
- Junior hiring declining (54% of leaders predict long-term drop) — but don't thin junior pipelines without building mentorship structures first
- Best hiring move: hybrid profiles (software engineering + applied AI) — most sought-after and hardest to find
- Average AI engineer salary: $206K in 2025 (up $50K from prior year) — small teams get expensive fast
- Alternative to full-time: pre-vetted staffing firms, Toptal/Upwork for specialists, offshore partners for execution-heavy work

### 4. Process: Quality Beats Speed in 2026

2025 was the year of AI speed. 2026 is the year of quality. Key workflow patterns:

**Agent-driven test loop** (best practice):
1. Agent writes code
2. Agent generates tests
3. Agent runs tests, identifies failures
4. Agent fixes and iterates
5. Human reviews final diff before PR merge

**Governance non-negotiables**:
- Mandatory code review for AI-generated PRs (AI code has 1.7x more bugs)
- Audit trails for all AI-assisted development (regulators arriving: EU AI Act, Cyber Resilience Act)
- 90% of security leaders report shadow AI (unapproved tools) — enforce tooling standards
- "Best engineers know when not to use AI" — cultivate this judgment

**Multi-agent workflows** are normalizing: one agent writes, another critiques, another validates security. Treat this as the default architecture for complex features.

### 5. Pricing: Value Over Hours

Time-based billing is broken in the AI era. When AI compresses delivery to 30-50% of previous time, hourly models destroy revenue. Shift to:

**Hybrid pricing** (most practical for agencies):
- Base retainer: covers architecture, oversight, project management, planning
- Variable layer: AI compute costs, agent execution, additional deliverables
- Anchor client conversations on **outcomes delivered**, not hours worked

**Outcome-based pricing** (for mature client relationships):
- Define measurable success metrics upfront
- Charge per resolved issue, feature shipped, conversion rate improved
- Example: Intercom's Fin charges $0.99 per resolved support ticket
- 30%+ of enterprise SaaS incorporated outcome pricing by 2025

**Positioning advice**: Re-anchor value on expertise and judgment, not execution time. Speed is a benefit you're delivering to the client, not a reason for them to pay less.

### 6. Leadership: The Measurement Gap Is the Risk

- 61% of companies increased engineering budgets in 2025 — only 20% measure AI's impact
- Without metrics, you can't justify investment, demonstrate ROI, or identify where AI is creating technical debt
- AI high performers are 3x more likely to have strong senior leadership ownership (Source: [[Jellyfish-2025-Engineering-Management]])
- Treat AI adoption as organizational transformation, not a tool upgrade

## Contradictions

- **"10x productivity" hype vs. actual data**: Some claim AI enables 10x developer productivity. [[Jellyfish-2025-Engineering-Management]] shows realistic gains are 30-50% for deeply engaged users. The 10x claim is observed mainly in edge cases (greenfield, AI-native workflows). Trust the survey data over the anecdotes.
- **Junior hiring decline vs. mentorship need**: Junior hiring is declining, but thinning junior pipelines creates a knowledge transfer problem. Companies eliminating junior roles entirely are storing up a future leadership gap.
- **Outcome pricing vs. client readiness**: Outcome-based billing is theoretically optimal but requires client trust and clear success metrics. Most clients aren't ready for pure outcome models — hybrid is the safer entry point.
