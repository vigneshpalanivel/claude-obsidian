# Gastric Issue — Solutions & Remedies

## Root Cause

Trapped gas in stomach/intestines → vagus nerve stimulation → **forehead headache** in the afternoon. Confirmed pattern: lunch → 1–2 hours later → headache onset.

Likely triggers in Buddha bowl (vendor lunch): raw chickpeas, raw broccoli/cauliflower, mixed legumes, excess raw onion, heavy dressings with vinegar.

---

## Daily Routine (Prevention)

### Before Lunch
- Drink **warm water** 20–30 min before eating — prepares stomach lining, stimulates digestive enzymes
- Do NOT drink large amounts during or immediately after meal — dilutes stomach acid

### After Lunch (most important step)
- Drink **warm cumin (jeera) water immediately after eating** — within 5–10 minutes
- 150–200ml warm cup — small, not large
- Acts as carminative — reduces gas formation during digestion
- **Cumin water is after eating, not before** — no benefit before food arrives

### At Office (no boiling available)
- Steep cumin seeds in hot water from pantry for 3–5 minutes — works fine
- OR **chew a small pinch of raw omam (ajwain/carom seeds)** after lunch — strongest carminative, zero preparation needed

---

## Omam vs Cumin

| | Omam (Ajwain) | Cumin (Jeera) |
|-|--------------|---------------|
| Tamil name | ஓமம் | சீரகம் |
| Effect | Stronger | Moderate |
| Preparation | Chew raw pinch | Steep in hot water |
| Best for | Immediate relief | Prevention |
| Office use | Easiest — no water needed | Needs hot water |

**Recommendation:** Keep omam at office desk. Chew small pinch after every lunch.

---

## If Headache Already Started

1. **Warm water with omam** — drink slowly
2. **Lie on left side** for 10 minutes — helps gas move through colon
3. **Asafoetida (perungayam/hing)** — tiny pinch in warm water — strongest fast relief
4. **Gentle clockwise abdominal massage** — follow colon path, moves trapped gas
5. **Walk 10 minutes** — movement pushes gas through digestive tract

## Foods to Avoid at Lunch

| Food | Why |
|------|-----|
| Raw chickpeas (unsoaked) | Ferments in gut → gas |
| Raw broccoli / cauliflower | High fermentable fibre |
| Excess raw onion | Gas-forming |
| Carbonated drinks | Direct gas introduction |
| Vinegar-heavy dressings | Fermentation trigger |
| Maida-based items | Ferments → gas + bloating |

## Foods That Help Digestion

| Food | Benefit |
|------|---------|
| Omam (ajwain) | Strongest carminative |
| Cumin (jeera) | Gas prevention |
| Ginger | Speeds gastric emptying |
| Buttermilk (moru) | Coolant + salt replacement + digestion |
| Curd | Probiotic, improves gut flora |
| Warm water | Thins mucus, aids motility |

---

## Water Rules for Digestion

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 20–30 min before meal | Drink warm water normally |
| During meal | Small sips only |
| Immediately after meal | Avoid large amounts |
| 30–45 min after meal | Normal drinking resumes |

> High water volume (3L+) with insufficient salt can cause electrolyte imbalance → headache. Add pinch of salt + lemon to one glass of water daily.

---

## Connection to Sinusitis

Both gastric gas AND sinusitis cause forehead headache — they often combine in the afternoon. Treating only one gives partial relief. Full resolution requires:

1. ENT treatment for sinusitis (primary cause)
2. Omam after lunch (gastric cause)
3. 20-20-20 eye rule (eye strain cause)
4. Neck stretch midday (tension cause)
