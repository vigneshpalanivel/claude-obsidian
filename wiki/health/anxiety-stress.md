## How Anxiety Presents in Vignesh (Physical, Not Mental)

Anxiety doesn't always feel like worry or panic. In this case it shows up predominantly as **physical symptoms**:

| Symptom | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| Body heat sensation | Cortisol → histamine → blood vessel dilation |
| Sneezing / allergic flares | Stress lowers mast cell activation threshold (CRH pathway) |
| Sleep impairment | Elevated cortisol at night delays sleep onset |
| Appetite suppression | Stress hormone directly suppresses hunger signals |
| Headaches (tension-type) | Cortisol tightens temporalis muscle → bilateral temple pain |
| Low BP episodes (90/60, 100/60) | Autonomic dysregulation from chronic sympathetic activation |
| Premature ejaculation | Anxiety activates sympathetic nervous system → shortens ejaculatory latency |

> The anxiety has been present long enough to become the **baseline state** — it no longer feels like anxiety, it just feels like how things are. The physical symptoms are the signal.

## Why Work You Enjoy Still Causes Stress

Liking work ≠ no stress. They're independent.

Stress = **load vs. recovery mismatch.** When output (decisions, responsibilities, screen time, deadlines) consistently exceeds recovery (sleep, food, movement, downtime), the nervous system stays in low-grade activation regardless of how meaningful the work feels.

**Current load:**
- Founder-level responsibility across two companies (Trioangle + InnBlockchain) simultaneously
- **12-hour office days for the past month (Jun–Jul 2026)** — sustained overload with no recovery window
- Chronic undereating — body running on insufficient fuel amplifies cortisol
- Sleep impairment — inadequate recovery overnight
- Multiple active health conditions managed in parallel
- Zero exercise in any recorded visit or self-report — no cortisol disposal mechanism

> 12 hours/day × 30 days = chronic cortisol elevation with no physical outlet. This single factor explains the worsening of every symptom in Jul 2026: meal skipping (appetite suppression), sleep quality drop, headache frequency, allergic flares, PE. Not coincidental timing.

Enjoying the work makes it harder to notice — there's no "I hate this" signal prompting you to stop.

## Ashwagandha Caution

You're on **Escitalopram (SSRI)**. Ashwagandha has mild serotonergic activity and can affect thyroid hormones.

- **At tea-bag quantities:** likely fine — below interaction threshold
- **At supplement doses (capsules/powder):** check with Dr. Sabrin first
- **Signal to watch for:** increased anxiety, jitteriness, or worsened sleep after taking it → stop and flag to doctor
