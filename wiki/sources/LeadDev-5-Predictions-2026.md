# LeadDev: 5 Uncomfortable Predictions for Engineering Leaders in 2026

**Source**: [leaddev.com](https://leaddev.com/leadership/5-uncomfortable-predictions-for-engineering-leaders-in-2026) | LeadDev | Late 2025

---

## Summary

Five contrarian predictions for how AI will create new leadership problems, not just new productivity gains. Useful counterweight to optimistic adoption narratives.

---

## The 5 Predictions

**1. Bosses will demand AI impact evidence**
70% of developers report time savings, but only 17% see improved team collaboration. Leaders need concrete measurement frameworks. Investment without accountability is a leadership failure. (confidence: medium)

**2. Major incidents will trace back to AI-generated code**
Research: AI code causes ~1 in 5 security breaches; ships code "10 times riskier than doing it by hand." Mandatory security education, code reviews, and audit trails are no longer optional. (confidence: high)

**3. Regulators will scrutinize AI usage**
EU's AI Act and Cyber Resilience Act signal tightening oversight, especially in regulated industries. Teams need documented audit trails and governance frameworks before regulators come calling. (confidence: medium — timing uncertain)

**4. Junior developer hiring will decline**
54% of leaders predict long-term drops in junior hiring. Implication: retention and internal upskilling matter more than recruitment pipelines. Emotional intelligence remains uniquely human — invest in it. (confidence: high)

**5. Best engineers know when not to use AI**
Sometimes manual coding is faster than fixing AI errors. Hire and promote for judgment about automation, not tool fluency. (confidence: high)
