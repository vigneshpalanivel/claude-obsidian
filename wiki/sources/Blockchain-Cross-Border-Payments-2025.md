# Blockchain in Cross-Border Payments: 2025 Guide

## Source Summary

Research across BVNK, Global Trade Magazine, Rapyd, and Deloitte on blockchain-driven cross-border payment solutions, adoption trends, and technical patterns as of 2025.

## How It Works

Blockchain cross-border payments use **decentralized ledgers** and **stablecoins** (crypto pegged to fiat) to transfer value directly between parties without correspondent banks. The "stablecoin sandwich" pattern:

1. Convert domestic currency → stablecoin (at source)
2. Transfer stablecoin globally (seconds, any time)
3. Convert stablecoin → destination currency

## Adoption Leaders

- **JPMorgan Kinexys Digital Payments**: 24/7 cross-border transfers, near real-time, $2B+ daily transactions
- **Stripe**: Building proprietary blockchain for global settlement (2026)
- **Deel**: Contractor payments in stablecoins, eliminating settlement delays
- **Visa**: Stablecoin settlement integrations
- **Tesla**: Enterprise stablecoin payments

## Benefits vs. Traditional Correspondent Banking

| Dimension | Traditional | Blockchain |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Settlement time | 3–5 days | Minutes |
| Cost | 3–8% | 0.5–2% |
| Hours | Business hours only | 24/7/365 |
| Chargebacks | Possible | No chargebacks |
| Transparency | Limited | Full audit trail |

## Challenges

- **Regulatory fragmentation**: No unified global framework; compliance varies by jurisdiction
- **Interoperability**: Limited cross-chain compatibility slows enterprise adoption
- **Fiat off-ramp gaps**: Last-mile conversion to local currency in developing markets remains friction point
- **Adoption rate**: Only ~4% of global population actively uses blockchain; stablecoins still 3% of cross-border flows
