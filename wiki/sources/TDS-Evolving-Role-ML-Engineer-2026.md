# Towards Data Science: The Evolving Role of the ML Engineer

**Source**: [towardsdatascience.com](https://towardsdatascience.com/the-evolving-role-of-the-ml-engineer/) | Stephanie Kirmer, Staff ML Engineer | 2026

---

## Summary

Practitioner perspective on how the ML engineer role is changing in the post-LLM era. Key reassurance: the role has shifted, not disappeared — experienced MLEs remain essential for non-standard problems.

---

## Key Claims

**Role is shifting, not eliminated** — Despite LLM advances, "there's still a lot for people in ML to do, especially applying our skills to unusual or unique problems." (confidence: medium, practitioner opinion)

**MLEs use AI tools strategically** — Kirmer uses LLMs to "bounce ideas off, to get critiques of my approaches" and for boilerplate (unit tests). The tools are collaborators, not replacements. (confidence: high)

**What hasn't changed**
- Critical problem-solving on non-standard issues
- Strategic tool evaluation and customization
- Navigating the gap between theoretical capability and real-world constraints

**Focus shifted** — From routine implementation toward higher-level engineering judgment, strategy, and unconventional problem-solving.

---

## What This Contributes

Counterbalance to the "AI is eating ML engineering" narrative. Useful context for [[Trioangle]] pivot planning: deep ML skill is still valuable, but for specialized/custom work rather than standard pipeline plumbing.
