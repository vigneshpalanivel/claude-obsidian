# EU Fintech Compliance Landscape: 2025-2026 Overview

## Source Summary

Aggregated research across Powens, Nortal, LegalNodes, InnReg, Unnax, and Coredo covering the full landscape of EU fintech regulatory compliance requirements active or entering force in 2025-2026.

## The Nine Key EU Fintech Regulations (2026)

| # | Regulation | Deadline | Scope |
|---|-----------|----------|-------|
| 1 | **PSD3 / PSR** | Q1-Q2 2026 in force; 21-month transition | All PSPs |
| 2 | **Instant Payments Regulation (IPR)** | Sending: Oct 2025; VoP: Oct 2025 | All PSPs offering euro credit transfers |
| 3 | **FiDA** (Financial Data Access) | Estimated 2027 (trilogue paused) | All financial data holders |
| 4 | **CCD2** (Consumer Credit Directive 2) | November 20, 2026 | Consumer credit providers, BNPL lenders |
| 5 | **DORA** | January 2025 (enforcement ramps 2026) | All financial entities + ICT providers |
| 6 | **EU AML / AMLA** | AMLA operational July 2025; rulebook July 2027 | All FIs, fintechs, crypto platforms |
| 7 | **EU AI Act** | High-risk: August 2, 2026 | Fintechs using AI in regulated processes |
| 8 | **MiCA** | Transitional end: June 2026 | All CASPs |
| 9 | **National e-invoicing** (France, Spain) | France: Sep 2026; Spain: Jan/Jul 2027 | B2B businesses in FR/ES |

## Enforcement Climate (2025)

- Regulatory fines in H1 2025: **$1.23 billion** — a **417% increase** vs H1 2024
- DORA already in force since January 2025; supervisors reviewing frameworks before stricter enforcement in 2026
- MiCA enforcement: >€540M in fines since implementation; 50+ crypto firms' licenses revoked by Feb 2025
- EU AI Act prohibited AI practices enforceable from February 2025

## Upcoming Regime (2027+)

- **AMLA** full supervision begins 2028
- **FiDA** compliance expected 2027 (30-32 months post-adoption)
- **DLT Pilot Regime** review by ESMA due March 2026; Commission decision on permanent regime pending
