# Morrison & Foerster — Frequently Asked Questions About Regulation S

## Source Summary

Mofo's FAQ format guide aimed at issuer-side counsel preparing a first Reg S offering. Mofo is an established capital markets firm; this FAQ has been cited across the industry as a practical primer for over a decade.

## What It Contributes

### Practical Use Cases
- Foreign issuers raising in their home country without U.S. registration
- U.S. issuers tapping the Eurobond market or Asian institutional debt
- Combined offerings with Rule 144A (for U.S. QIBs) or with Reg D (for U.S. accredited investors)
- Convertible and exchangeable bonds with global tranches

### Pitfalls Highlighted
- U.S. roadshow meetings inadvertently constituting directed selling efforts
- Press releases via wire services with U.S. circulation
- "Offshore" closes that are pre-arranged with U.S. buyers — the SEC will look through the form to substance
- Improper transfer legends or missing buyer certifications, which give the SEC grounds to challenge the safe harbor

### Reg D / Reg S Integration
The two safe harbors can be used in parallel for the same capital raise (U.S. accredited via Reg D, offshore via Reg S). Each must independently satisfy its own conditions. Modern Reg D integration safe harbors (post-2020 amendments) generally protect concurrent Reg S offerings from being collapsed into a single U.S. offering for registration purposes — but only if marketing channels are kept separate.
