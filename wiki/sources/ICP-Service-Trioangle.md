## What This Document Is

Trioangle's Ideal Customer Profile for **custom development** (greenfield, platform rebuilds) and **AI engineering** (AI features added to existing platforms). Distinct from [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]] (clone scripts, $500-$25k) and [[ICP-Channel-Partner-Trioangle]] (agency intermediaries).

**Track-aware framing**: This is **Track 2** in the [[Trioangle Growth Plan]] two-track model — the recurring-revenue growth engine. Track 1 is [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]] (cash engine).

**Routing rule**: If buyer needs a proven-model platform launched fast with standard features, route to [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]]. If they need proprietary architecture, unique business logic, or a rebuild, they belong here. Buyers with $10k-$25k budget — assess use case (clone fit → Product; custom requirements → Service).

## Existing-Operator Filter (Track 2 Qualifier)

The qualification gate that makes Track 2's recurring-revenue thesis work — without it, Service ICP recreates Product ICP mortality risk.

✅ Required: platform operational 12+ months OR established offline business with revenue traction · revenue traction (not pre-revenue) · funded round / established business / family-group backing · for P5 specifically: existing platform with active users (no greenfield/pre-launch).

❌ Disqualifying: pre-launch / pre-revenue (route to Product ICP or decline) · strong in-house engineering teams (no recurring demand) · "just exploring AI" without specific business problem (P5) · equity-only / deferred-payment requests.

## Situational Triggers

**Custom Dev (P1-P4):** funding announcement (Seed / Series A / bridge) in last 90 days · platform outage mentioned publicly · CTO/VP Eng job posting · key engineering lead departure (LinkedIn) · expansion to new geography or vertical.

**AI Engineering (P5):** competitor in same vertical announces an AI feature · ML/AI Engineer job posting · investor deck mentions AI roadmap · public AI underperformance signal (dispatch latency complaints, fraud false-positive reviews) · CTO/CPO publicly engages with AI engineering content · funding round where AI was named as a moat.

**Pause signals:** mid-fundraise · post-failed-agency (lead with empathy, free architecture review) · internal engineering hiring spree · post-acquisition integration · enterprise Q1/Q3 budget freeze · price-shopping posture · "we're trying Copilot/Cursor internally first."

## Competitive Positioning

**Primary differentiator**: 800+ domain-specific platform builds since 2015 across all 9 verticals. Domain edge cases (dispatch logic, commission flows, multi-tenant auth, real-time tracking, rental availability) are solved patterns, not learned on the client's budget.

> [!warning] AI delivery count caveat
> 800+ counts cumulative *platform* deliveries (since 2015), NOT AI feature deliveries (service line launched 2026). AI positioning copy should anchor on **domain knowledge of the verticals** (legitimately demonstrated by 800+) rather than AI delivery volume specifically. Avoid "we've built AI features in 800+ platforms" — conflates the two.

**Against large consultancies (Accenture, Infosys)**: Up to 80% lower cost; 16-week average delivery vs. 18-24 months.
**Against generic AI agencies**: Domain-specific AI implementation vs. strategy decks. We know the vertical's edge cases before the first call.
**Against AI coding tools (Copilot/Cursor)**: We deliver AI features (outcomes), not AI tools (accelerators). Engineers using Copilot still need to know what to build.
**Against hyperscaler AI SaaS (AWS Personalize, GCP Vertex, Pinecone)** — *new for 2026*: Honest about when SaaS fits; win on custom integration, vertical-specific tuning, data ownership.
