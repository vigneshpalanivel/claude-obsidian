## What This Document Is

Trioangle's Ideal Customer Profile for **AI Engineering** (primary — AI features embedded into existing platforms) and **custom development** (secondary — platform rebuilds, modernization, expansion for established operators). Distinct from [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]] (clone scripts, $500-$25k) and [[ICP-Channel-Partner-Trioangle]] (agency intermediaries).

**Track-aware framing**: This is **Track 2** in the [[Trioangle Growth Plan]] two-track model — the recurring-revenue growth engine. Track 1 is [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]] (cash engine).

**Routing rule**: If buyer needs a proven-model platform launched fast with standard features, route to [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]]. If they need proprietary architecture, unique business logic, AI features embedded into a running platform, or a rebuild, they belong here. Buyers with $10k-$25k budget — assess use case (clone fit → Product; custom requirements → Service).

## Existing-Operator Filter (Track 2 Qualifier)

The qualification gate that makes Track 2's recurring-revenue thesis work — without it, Service ICP recreates Product ICP mortality risk.

✅ Required: platform operational 12+ months OR established offline business with revenue traction · $1M+ ARR (revenue traction, not pre-revenue) · funded round / established business / family-group backing · for P5 specifically: existing platform with active users (no greenfield/pre-launch).

❌ Disqualifying: pre-launch / pre-revenue (route to Product ICP or decline) · strong in-house engineering teams (no recurring demand) · "just exploring AI" without specific business problem (P5) · equity-only / deferred-payment requests · solo non-technical founders with <$10k.

## Two Pre-Sales Products

Both 2-week paid engagements, credited toward full engagement. Pricing tiers in Sales Playbook §8.

| Product | For | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Audit Sprint** (canonical name) 🥇 | **P5** (default entry) · also offerable to **P4** (relationship-demonstration) and **P3 in Phase 2** (after platform exists, ~6-12 months post-launch) | Data audit · AI feature shortlist (ranked) · reference architecture (isolation, fallback, rollout) · build-vs-buy-vs-SaaS recommendation per feature · fixed-price quote · AI engineer team intro |
| **Discovery Sprint** 🥈 | **P1** and **P2** arriving with rebuild brief · **P3 in Phase 1** (net-new digital platform build, inside or alongside RFP) | ERD + data model · system architecture + tech stack · wireframes (P2) · critical path map · fixed-price delivery estimate · team intro |

**Default rule:** Lead with AI Audit Sprint unless prospect arrives with a clear non-AI rebuild brief. When Discovery Sprint is signed (P1/P2 rebuild path), **always name AI Audit Sprint as the planned follow-on**.

**Don't offer to:** P4 (MENA — trust is relationship-built); P3 in Phase 1 (no platform exists yet — no data to audit).

## Situational Triggers

**Custom Dev (P1-P4):** public expansion announcement · 10× user-growth milestone · CTO/VP Eng job posting · platform outage publicly mentioned · key engineering lead departure · acquirer/late-stage technical DD · Series C+ round in established platform.

**AI Engineering (P5 additional):** direct vertical competitor announces AI feature · ML/AI Engineer / Applied Scientist job posting · prospect's investor deck/board update mentions AI roadmap · public AI underperformance signal (dispatch latency, pricing volatility, fraud false-positive reviews) · CTO/CPO publicly engages with AI engineering content · Series C+ growth round where pitch deck named AI as a moat.

**Pause signals:** post-failed-agency (lead with empathy, free architecture review) · mid-fundraise pre-close · internal engineering hiring spree · post-acquisition integration · enterprise Q1/Q3 budget freeze.

## Competitive Positioning

**Primary differentiator**: 800+ domain-specific platform builds since 2015 across 9 verticals (historical). Domain edge cases (dispatch logic, commission flows, multi-tenant auth, real-time tracking, rental availability, marketplace fraud patterns) are solved patterns, not learned on the client's budget.

> [!warning] AI delivery count caveat
> 800+ counts cumulative *platform* deliveries (since 2015), NOT AI feature deliveries (service line launched 2026). AI positioning copy should anchor on **domain knowledge of the verticals** (legitimately demonstrated by 800+) rather than AI delivery volume specifically. Avoid "we've built AI features in 800+ platforms" — conflates the two.

**Against large consultancies (Accenture, Infosys)**: Up to 80% lower cost; 16-week average delivery vs. 18-24 months.
**Against AI-native mid-market shops (Markovate, InData Labs, etc.) — most acute 2026 threat**: 9-vertical platform-delivery moat is the wedge; they ship AI features into systems they didn't build and don't deeply understand.
**Against generic AI agencies**: Domain-specific AI implementation vs. strategy decks. We know the vertical's edge cases before the first call.
**Against AI coding tools (Copilot/Cursor)**: We deliver AI features (outcomes), not AI tools (accelerators). Engineers using Copilot still need to know what to build.
**Against hyperscaler AI SaaS (AWS Personalize, GCP Vertex, Pinecone)**: Honest about when SaaS fits; win on custom integration, vertical-specific tuning, data ownership.
