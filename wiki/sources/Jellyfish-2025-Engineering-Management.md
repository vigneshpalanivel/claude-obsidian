# Jellyfish 2025 State of Engineering Management Report

**Source**: [jellyfish.co](https://jellyfish.co/blog/2025-software-engineering-management-trends/) | Jellyfish | 2025

---

## Summary

Survey-based industry report on engineering management practices during AI adoption. Covers: AI tool adoption rates, productivity measurement, leadership engagement, and team structure evolution. Strong primary data on what separates high-performing teams from the rest.

---

## Key Data Points

**AI adoption**: 90% of engineering teams now use AI coding tools, up from 61% one year prior. 95% use them at least weekly; 75% use AI for at least half their engineering work. (confidence: high)

**Claude Code's rise**: Released May 2025, became the #1 AI coding tool within 8 months, overtaking GitHub Copilot and Cursor. (confidence: high)

**The measurement gap**: 61% of companies increased engineering budgets in 2025. Only 20% measure AI's impact with engineering metrics. Investment is racing ahead of accountability. (confidence: high)

**Productivity reality check**: 67% predict at least 25% velocity gains in 2026, but the "10x engineer" narrative remains aspirational — observed mainly in edge cases (greenfield projects, AI-native workflows). Realistic gains: 30-50% for deeply-engaged users. (confidence: medium)

**Leadership differentiator**: AI high performers are 3x more likely to report strong senior leadership ownership and engagement. Cultural investment matters more than tool selection. (confidence: high)

**Multi-tool behavior**: 48% of high-performing teams use two or more AI platforms — not tool loyalty but deliberate experimentation. (confidence: high)
