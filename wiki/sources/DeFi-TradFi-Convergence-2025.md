# DeFi and TradFi Convergence: 2025 Research

## Source Summary

Research across Equinix, Finextra, ResearchGate, iLink.dev, and academic papers (SSRN) tracking how DeFi protocols and traditional banks are converging through stablecoins, regulatory frameworks, and hybrid models.

## Regulatory Bridge: The GENIUS Act

The US Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins (**GENIUS Act**, 2025) is the first federal framework for stablecoin regulation in the United States. By legitimizing stablecoins for bank use, it acts as the key bridge between TradFi and DeFi — institutions can now offer blockchain-powered financial products without full regulatory ambiguity.

## Hybrid Model Architecture

DeFi projects are adopting hybrid architectures combining:
- Decentralized protocol execution on-chain
- Off-chain **compliance layers** (AML/KYC) to satisfy regulations
- Smart contract automation with human oversight for high-value transactions

Traditional banks are reciprocating by:
- Exploring stablecoin issuance and custodianship
- Integrating DeFi yield strategies into structured products
- Running permissioned DeFi protocols (e.g., Onyx, Kinexys)

## AI-DeFi Integration

By early 2025, AI-powered DeFi agents began integrating with:
- **Yearn Finance**: automated yield optimization strategies
- **Aave**: risk assessments and portfolio rebalancing
- AI agents execute cross-protocol arbitrage autonomously

## Key Institutional Moves

- JPMorgan extended JPM Coin to public blockchains (November 2025)
- Kinexys integrating with Canton Network for deposit token cross-chain flows
- Multiple major banks launching stablecoin pilot programs post-GENIUS Act
