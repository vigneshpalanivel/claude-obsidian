# Pillsbury — Offerings and Resales of Restricted Securities Under Regulation S

## Source Summary

Pillsbury practitioner guide on the intersection of Reg S resales and Rule 144 restricted-security treatment. The authoritative practice note on Rule 905 — which is the rule most often misunderstood by U.S. private issuers using Reg S.

## What It Contributes

### The Wash-Off Problem (Pre-1998)
Originally, U.S. domestic issuers placed equity offshore under Reg S, waited out the (then) 40-day restricted period, and resold the now-"unrestricted" securities back into U.S. markets. The SEC viewed this as a sham — equity that began life as restricted should remain restricted.

### Rule 905's Fix
Added in 1998. Equity securities of a U.S. domestic issuer placed offshore under Rules 901, 903, or 904 are **classified as restricted securities under Rule 144**. Multiple offshore resales do not strip that status. The Rule 144 holding period applies regardless of how many times the security changes hands offshore.

### Practical Effect
- U.S. domestic non-reporting issuer placing equity offshore: 1-year Rule 144 holding period before the buyer can resell into U.S. public markets
- U.S. domestic reporting issuer: 6 months
- During the period, offshore resales must be among non-U.S. persons or to QIBs under 144A

### Why This Catches Issuers Out
A U.S. founder raising offshore through Reg S often assumes their European or Asian buyer can freely resell on day 41. They cannot — the Rule 144 clock is the binding constraint, not the Reg S distribution compliance period (which controls only the issuer-side restrictions).
