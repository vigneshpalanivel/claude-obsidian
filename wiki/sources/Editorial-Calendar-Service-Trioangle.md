## Why This Source Matters (Beyond Restating the Content Strategy)

The [[Content-Strategy-Service]] source covers 81 articles, 6 verticals, persona-gated CTAs, and keyword strategy. It answers *what* to write and *for whom*. The editorial calendar answers *when* — and more importantly, *in what order* and *with what operational constraints*.

The non-obvious decisions baked into this calendar:

**1. Cadence change from 3x/week to 2x/week (applied in this ingest session).** The original 3x/week schedule produced 72 articles over 6 months. The 2x/week schedule produces 48. The tradeoff: better quality on Pillar 7 AI engineering deep-dives (2,500-4,000 words, technically reviewable) at the cost of 24 lower-priority Pillar 1/3 architecture articles moved to Month 7+. All 26 Pillar 7 articles are preserved in Months 1-5.

**2. Pillar 7 front-loading is the primary structural decision.** Month 3 is 100% Pillar 7 — no trust or decision-stage content. This is an intentional trade-off: topical authority on the primary service line (AI Engineering) before competing on custom-dev keywords. The calendar includes a Month 2 monitoring trigger (pillar hub click-through rate) that can pull a decision-stage article forward if the funnel shows friction.

**3. Trigger-based outbound distribution is non-negotiable.** Articles targeting late-stage growth, acquisition signals, engineering departures, and competitive AI launches are published to the blog on schedule for SEO — but withheld from Channel 4 outbound sequences until a matching established-operator trigger is confirmed. This discipline protects reply rates.

**4. The Channel 4 conditional gate (Month 2) is the highest-risk operational dependency.** If trigger-detection tooling (LinkedIn Sales Navigator + Google Alerts/Mention + AppFollow/Sensor Tower + Crunchbase Pro) is not operational with a named owner, Channel 4 must be deferred to Month 3 — not fired without monitoring.

**5. P5 implementation articles (#114, #115, #116) were promoted from Month 7+ to Month 5.** These close the retention gap for AI Audit Sprint clients 3-6 months post-build. Article #116 (model retraining) doubles as the primary upsell trigger for the second AI Audit Sprint engagement in the 2-4/year repeat-buy cadence.

## Key Claims and Scheduling Logic

### Scoring System
Articles are prioritized using a score (1-10). Pillar 7 articles receive +0.5 bonus when compared against non-Pillar-7 articles in scheduling decisions.

### Month 7+ Deferred Articles — Reason Classification
The 37 articles deferred from Months 1-6 fall into four categories:
1. **Capacity — Pillar 1 tech depth** (lower conversion priority than Pillar 7 or Pillar 2): #1, #2, #6, #8, #9, #10, #11, #13, #26, #33, #38
2. **Capacity — Pillar 2/3 trust and architecture** (coverage sufficient from earlier articles): #15, #17, #18, #19, #27, #28, #34, #35, #63, #74, #76, #79
3. **Capacity — Enterprise/MENA channel-partner enablement** (1/month is sufficient): #54, #56, #57, #60, #62, #72
4. **Gated on production AI delivery case** (cannot publish responsibly without real metrics): #117 (social/entertainment AI), #118 (travel/hospitality AI)

The highest-scored deferred articles are #1 (9.2), #3 (8.5), #2 (8.8), #19 (8.5) — these should lead Month 7+.

### Decision-Stage Coverage
Decision-stage articles through Month 5: #5 (case study), #90, #91, #23, #22. That is 5 of 40 published articles (~13%). This is intentional — awareness content establishes the audience before decision-stage content converts them. The calendar includes a monthly review checklist to catch if decision-stage coverage falls below 8% of cumulative published count.
