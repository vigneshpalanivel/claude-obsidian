# AI Engineer's Reading List for 2026 (10 Books That Matter)

**Source**: [javarevisited.blogspot.com](https://javarevisited.blogspot.com/2026/04/the-ai-engineers-reading-list-for-2026.html) | javarevisited | April 2026

---

## Summary

Curated 10-book list spanning beginner to advanced AI/ML engineering. The most commonly cited books across 2026 learning-roadmap recommendations.

---

## The 10 Books by Level

### Beginner → Intermediate

**Prompt Engineering for LLMs** — John Berryman & Albert Ziegler. Few-shot prompting, CoT, prompt patterns. Entry point for AI product developers.

**Hands-On Large Language Models** — Jay Alammar & Maarten Grootendorst. Building and fine-tuning LLMs with Hugging Face + LangChain. Practical applications.

### Intermediate

**Prompt Engineering for Generative AI** — James Phoenix & Mike Taylor. Production-grade prompting across text, image, code.

### Intermediate → Advanced

**AI Engineering** — Chip Huyen. The recommended first book for 2026. AI engineering stack, turning ML models into products, pipelines, deployment, monitoring, scaling. Defines the discipline.

**Designing Machine Learning Systems** — Chip Huyen. ML system design, data drift, retraining, production reliability. Think like an ML product engineer.

**The LLM Engineering Handbook** — Paul Iusztin & Maxime Labonne. Prompt engineering, RAG pipelines, evaluation, fine-tuning, system architecture. Production LLM apps.

**Building LLMs for Production** — Louis-François Bouchard & Louie Peters. Fine-tuning, deploying, scaling, maintaining LLMs. Deployment challenges.

**Building Agentic AI Systems** — Anjanava Biswas & Wrick Talukdar. Autonomous agents with reasoning, planning, decision-making. Auto-GPT and LangGraph-style systems.

### Advanced

**Build a Large Language Model (from Scratch)** — Sebastian Raschka, PhD. Transformer-based LLMs in PyTorch. Tokenization, attention, training. Deep code-level understanding.

**The AI Engineering Bible** — Thomas R. Caldwell. Full AI lifecycle: architecture, infrastructure, deployment, monitoring. Enterprise implementation.

---

## Reading Sequence Recommendations

**Starting out (0-6 months)**
1. Chip Huyen - AI Engineering (read cover to cover)
2. Berryman & Ziegler - Prompt Engineering for LLMs

**Building production LLM apps (6-12 months)**
3. Iusztin & Labonne - LLM Engineering Handbook
4. Alammar & Grootendorst - Hands-On LLMs
5. Huyen - Designing ML Systems

**Advanced specialization (12+ months)**
6. Raschka - Build an LLM from Scratch (if going deep)
7. Bouchard & Peters - Building LLMs for Production
8. Biswas & Talukdar - Building Agentic AI Systems
