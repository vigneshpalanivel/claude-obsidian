# Cleary Gottlieb — 2026 Digital Assets Regulatory Update

## Source Summary

Cleary's annual digital-assets summary covering U.S. regulatory developments through 2025 and previewing 2026. Notes the structural shift in SEC posture and the legislative anchor (GENIUS Act) that gives institutional capital comfort to enter tokenized markets.

## What It Contributes

### SEC Posture Shift
Under Chairman Paul Atkins (post-January 2025 inauguration), the SEC explicitly stepped back from "regulation by enforcement" against crypto and centered on fraud + investor harm. This benefits Reg S issuers because:
- Lower risk of being second-guessed on token classification at the offering stage
- More room to use SAFTs and tokenized securities under existing safe harbors

### GENIUS Act
Established a federal framework for payment stablecoins. Not directly Reg S — but creates a regulated foundation that makes tokenized debt and tokenized asset offerings (often run as Reg D + Reg S) easier to underwrite.

### Standard Token Offering Structure (2025–2026)
- **Reg D 506(c)** for U.S. accredited investors (general solicitation allowed)
- **Reg S** for non-U.S. participants (offshore tranche, no U.S. directed selling efforts)
- SAFT or tokenized note as the instrument
- KYC/AML at the buyer-onboarding layer to enforce U.S. person screening

### What's Still Open in 2026
- Tokenized money market funds (BlackRock BUIDL precedent)
- DeFi disclosure regime (no concrete proposal yet)
- Cross-border token resale on offshore venues — Reg S transfer restrictions still apply
