## The 10 In-Demand Skills with Salary Ranges (USD, senior to principal)

1. **LLM Fine-Tuning** — $195K–$350K+
2. **Deep Learning Engineering** — $180K–$280K
3. **Natural Language Processing** — $160K–$250K
4. **MLOps & Model Deployment** — $175K–$270K
5. **Computer Vision** — $165K–$240K
6. **Prompt Engineering** — $150K–$335K+
7. **PyTorch/TensorFlow** — $160K–$250K
8. **Cloud AI Platforms** — $160K–$230K
9. **Data Engineering for AI** — $155K–$240K
10. **AI Ethics & Responsible AI** — $150K–$280K

## Key Market Claims

- Average AI engineer compensation reached **$206,000 in 2025**, a $50K year-over-year increase. (confidence: medium)
- **75%+ of AI job listings** explicitly seek domain experts with deep focused knowledge rather than generalists. (confidence: high)
- Domain experts earn **30–50% more** than generalists at equivalent experience levels. (confidence: medium)
- Roles listing at least two AI skills pay **43% more** than comparable non-AI roles. (confidence: medium)
- LLM fine-tuning is the highest-premium specialization in enterprise AI as companies move beyond generic ChatGPT integrations toward custom models on proprietary data. (confidence: high)

> [!gap] US-only salary data. India/SEA rates differ significantly.
