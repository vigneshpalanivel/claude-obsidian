# ICP — Trioangle Launch-Ready Marketplace Platforms

**Source**: `.raw/ICP - Product.md` | Trioangle internal | 2026 | Status in source: draft

---

## What This Document Is

Trioangle's Ideal Customer Profile for their **product line** — readymade marketplace clone scripts and super-app platforms. Distinct from their service ICP (`ICP - Service`), which covers custom development.

---

## Company Summary

[[Trioangle]] — operating since 2015, 800+ platforms delivered, 150+ countries. Sells launch-ready marketplace scripts across 8 verticals: Delivery & Logistics, Travel & Hospitality, Commerce & Marketplaces, On-Demand Home Services, Ride-Hailing & Transport, Social & Entertainment, Education, Multi-Service / Super App.

**Business model**: B2B sales to founders and business owners who buy scripts for commercial deployment. Buyers then operate B2C/B2B2C consumer-facing marketplaces.

| Tier | Price Range | Best For |

## Core ICP Profile

- **Size**: 1–5 employees (Solopreneur / Co-founders)
- **Revenue**: Pre-revenue or < $10k/mo
- **Stage**: Pre-launch / Bootstrapped / Early Traction
- **Geography priority**: North America, UK, EU, Middle East
- **Tech maturity**: Laggard to Mainstream — understands the business of tech, not the code
- **Budget**: $500–$25k depending on tier

## Key Strategic Connections to AI-Era Research

**Persona 6 (AI Builder Who Hit the Wall)** is a direct product of the [[Vibe Coding]] wave. Buyers who spent 4–8 weeks with Cursor/Lovable/Replit arrive at Trioangle after hitting App Store rejection, real-time dispatch failures at scale, and payment gateway complexity. This maps directly to findings in [[Research: Running a Software Dev Company Post-Vibe-Coding]] — code generation is only 25-35% of a live product.

**Persona 1 AI-era risk**: The Visionary Velocity Founder is the most vulnerable to displacement by improving AI deployment tools (Lovable, Bolt). Counter-strategy: compete on **certainty of outcome** (guaranteed live date, proven App Store approval, 800+ deployments) not speed-to-demo.

**Positioning thread**: All six personas are buying the same thing underneath the surface — **a live, working business** — not code. This mirrors the core [[Outcome-Based Pricing]] finding: buyers in the AI era pay for certainty of outcome, not execution hours.

## Disqualifying Signals (from source)

- Needs fully proprietary custom architecture → route to ICP - Service
- Super-app ambition with sub-$5k budget → re-qualify or redirect
- Dev agencies reselling without a specific live client project
- "Just exploring" without a concrete business idea
- Wants absolute cheapest option regardless of outcome risk → let them try freelancer first, re-engage as Persona 2
