# Cleary Gottlieb — Regulation S Selling and Transfer Restrictions

## Source Summary

White-shoe capital markets practitioner guide. Cleary is one of the law firms that built the modern Reg S / 144A combined offering structure; this guide is consistently cited by other firms.

## What It Contributes

### Category Framework
Cleary explains the SUSMI-based sorting clearly:
- **Category 1**: no SUSMI in the security class, or foreign government issuer, or overseas-directed offering. Lightest restrictions.
- **Category 2**: equity of Exchange Act reporting foreign issuers, debt of any reporting issuer, debt of non-reporting foreign issuers. 40-day distribution compliance period.
- **Category 3**: catch-all (most U.S. private equity offerings). Distribution compliance: 1 year for U.S. equity, 6 months for foreign equity, 40 days for debt.

### Directed Selling Efforts — Examples
Captures: U.S. publication ads referencing the offering, U.S. radio/TV, U.S. promotional seminars, soliciting persons known to be in the U.S.

Permitted: tombstone ads required by law (with non-registration disclaimer), offering documents delivered to Rule 144A QIBs, ordinary-course business activity not designed to condition the U.S. market.

### Combined 144A / Reg S
Practical guidance on running both safe harbors in one global offering — separate tranches, separate purchase agreements, transfer legends preserving QIB-only and Reg S-only restrictions.

## Why High Confidence

Cleary represents a large share of cross-border bond and equity issuers. Their interpretive positions reflect what major banks and issuers actually do.
