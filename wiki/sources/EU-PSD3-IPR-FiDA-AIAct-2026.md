# EU PSD3, IPR, FiDA, AI Act, eIDAS 2.0: 2025-2026 Research

## Source Summary

Research across Norton Rose Fulbright, EY, Partisia, Taylor Wessing, Trilateral Research, LegalNodes, and the European Commission covering four payment and digital identity regulations entering force in 2025-2026.

---

## PSD3 / PSR — Payment Services Directive 3

### Status
- **November 27, 2025**: Provisional political agreement reached between European Parliament and Council
- **Expected Q1-Q2 2026**: Enter into force after publication in Official Journal
- **21-month transition period** after entry into force for full compliance
- **Full applicability**: Late 2027 / early 2028

### What Changes from PSD2

| Area | PSD2 | PSD3 / PSR |
|------|------|-----------|
| Scope | Payments only | Adds instant payments, BNPL, crypto, digital identity |
| Fraud liability | Partial PSP liability | PSPs fully liable for fraud losses if prevention was inadequate |
| Open banking | Basic API access | Strengthened with FiDA expansion to all financial data |
| E-money institutions | Separate authorization | Integrated as sub-category of payment institutions |
| Cash access | Branch-dependent | Consumers can withdraw cash in shops without purchase |
| SCA | Strong Customer Authentication required | Enhanced with EUDI Wallet-based authentication |

### PSR (Payment Services Regulation)
PSR is a companion instrument to PSD3 that replaces most of PSD2's conduct and market rules with a directly applicable EU regulation (no national transposition required). This removes regulatory arbitrage between member states.

## Instant Payments Regulation (IPR)

### Adopted
March 2024 by European Parliament and Council.

### Compliance Deadlines (Euro-Area PSPs)

| Obligation | Deadline |
|-----------|----------|
| Receive instant euro payments | January 9, 2025 |
| Send instant euro payments | **October 9, 2025** |
| Verification of Payee (VoP) service live | **October 9, 2025** |

### Key Requirements

1. **Instant settlement**: All euro credit transfers must settle within 10 seconds, 24/7/365
2. **Parity pricing**: Instant payment charges cannot exceed standard transfer charges
3. **Verification of Payee (VoP)**: Free service matching payee name to IBAN before transfer authorization. Responses: match / close match / no match / other
4. **Daily sanctions screening**: Verify customers against restricted persons lists daily (not just at onboarding)

### Compliance Challenges for Fintechs
- Real-time processing infrastructure: systems designed for batch processing need redesign
- VoP geographic coverage: must match IBANs cross-border across all SEPA countries
- Fraud monitoring: instant finality means no reversal window; fraud detection must be pre-authorization

## FiDA — Financial Data Access Regulation

### What It Is
EU's "Open Finance" regulation. Extends PSD2's Open Banking (payments only) to all financial data: savings, investments, loans, pensions, insurance, crypto assets.

### Status (April 2026)
- June 2023: Proposal published
- April 2025: Trilogue negotiations began
- **Early 2026**: Trilogue negotiations halted
- Likely adoption: H1 2026 (if resumed) or later
- **Implementation target**: 2027 (30-32 months post-adoption)

### Key Requirements (Once Adopted)
- Financial data holders must provide real-time standardized API access
- Customer consent-based: data shared only with explicit, revocable consent
- Customers get dashboards to monitor and manage all data sharing
- Institutions must join **Financial Data Sharing Schemes (FDSS)** — industry standards bodies for APIs, liability rules, and pricing
- Covers: mortgages, loans, savings, investment accounts, insurance products, pension accounts, crypto assets

### Fintech Opportunity
FiDA creates a class of **Financial Information Service Providers (FISPs)** — licensed entities that aggregate financial data across institutions on behalf of customers. This is the "PSD2 for all finance" moment.

## EU AI Act — Fintech Obligations

### Risk Tiers Relevant to Fintech

| AI Use Case | Risk Classification | Deadline |
|------------|--------------------|---------| 
| Credit scoring / loan approval | **High-risk** (Annex III) | August 2, 2026 |
| Fraud detection systems | **High-risk** | August 2, 2026 |
| AML/CFT risk profiling | **High-risk** | August 2, 2026 |
| Insurance underwriting | **High-risk** | August 2, 2026 |
| Biometric identification | **High-risk** | August 2, 2026 |
| Prohibited social scoring | **Unacceptable risk** | February 2, 2025 (already banned) |

### High-Risk System Requirements (from August 2, 2026)
- Conformity assessments and CE marking
- Quality management system documentation
- Human oversight mechanisms
- EU AI database registration
- Ongoing monitoring and post-market surveillance
- Technical documentation for regulators

### Penalties
- Prohibited practices: **€35M or 7% of global annual turnover** (higher)
- High-risk violations: **€15M or 3% of global annual turnover** (higher)
- Providing incorrect information: **€7.5M or 1.5%**

## eIDAS 2.0 — EU Digital Identity Wallet

### What It Is
Regulation (EU) 2024/1183 mandating all EU member states to provide free EU Digital Identity (EUDI) Wallets to citizens by December 31, 2026. Wallets hold verified identity credentials, professional qualifications, travel documents, and bank account information.

### Fintech Compliance Requirements
- Regulated financial institutions (banks, payment institutions, e-money providers) must accept EUDI Wallets as a **Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)** method
- Must register as a **relying party** with national authority to accept wallet credentials
- KYC APIs and onboarding workflows must support ISO/IEC 18013-5, W3C Verifiable Credentials, and ARF specifications
- Impacts: KYC onboarding, digital contract signing, cross-border identity verification

### Timeline for Fintechs
- Q1 2025: Gap analysis vs. EUDI Wallet requirements
- Q3-Q4 2025: Pilot participation (optional)
- Q1-Q3 2026: Certification as relying party
- **Q4 2026**: Production launch aligned with mandatory wallet availability
