## Why This Source Matters (Beyond Restating the ICP)

The Service ICP doc is 862 lines of strategy: personas, pains, channels, objection handling, loss reasons, competitive positioning. Reps reading it know *who* and *why*, but not *what to do Tuesday morning*. The playbook fills that gap — and was deliberately written to **reference, not duplicate, the ICP** so the two never drift.

The non-obvious decision in this playbook: **a separate Service-ICP operating layer was needed because Trioangle's existing Sales Initiatives and Sales KPIs lists are Product-ICP-scoped** (clone-script motion, $999 entry price, volume-driven). Importing them into Service sales context produces wrong floors, wrong cycle-time expectations, and wrong KPI bands. User formally marked those legacy files Product-ICP-only on 2026-05-09 in the same session this playbook was created.

## Key Claims Extracted

1. **Two non-negotiable framing rules** inherited from ICP — repeated verbatim because reps break them most often:
   - **AI-first, dev-second** — default to AI Audit Sprint as entry product unless inbound is explicitly non-AI rebuild
   - **Defend trust with structural guarantees, not adjectives** — Day-1 GitHub access, IP transfer, 48-hour SLA with credit, pre-contract engineer intro, paid Sprint with escape hatch. Banned words: "transparent," "passionate," "high-quality," "world-class"

2. **7-stage pipeline with kill criteria.** Each stage has explicit *exit* AND *kill* criteria. Stage 5 (Proposal Submitted) is gated: **default rule is no proposal without a paid sprint**. Exceptions: P4 MENA (relationship gates trust, not sprints), P3 on a formal RFP track. A stage that doesn't move in 30 days is not a real opportunity — force movement or close-lost.

3. **Lead scoring rubric** — ≥40 points qualifies; <40 nurture or disqualify. Hard disqualifiers (–25 to –50): pre-revenue/pre-launch, solo non-technical founder with no business, equity-only/deferred-payment ask, "build Uber in 3 weeks for $8k" briefs. Existing-Operator Filter (+15) is a *hard* requirement — failing it disqualifies regardless of total score.

4. **Pricing floors (do not sell below):**
   - Single AI feature build: **$15,000**
   - Web/mobile rebuild for established operator: **$10,000**
   - AI multi-feature program: $40,000
   - Enterprise digital transformation: $50,000
   - MENA flagship: $30,000 (often stretches to $80k–$120k+)
   - AI Audit Sprint / Discovery Sprint: $1,500

5. **Discount approval matrix:** AE 0–5%, Sales Manager 5–10%, Founder/CRO 10–20%, Founder-only above 20%. "They asked," "competitor was cheaper," and "it'll close the deal" are explicitly *not* sufficient justifications.

6. **Output/deliverable-based pricing only.** No retainers, no hourly billing, no per-engineer subscriptions. Reps quoting any of those are out of bounds. This is a deliberate operating-model choice, not a salesperson preference.

7. **Always-in-scope structural guarantees** (not negotiable for discount): Day-1 GitHub access · IP transfer on final payment · senior code review on every PR · 48-hour comms SLA with 5% contract credit penalty · pre-contract team intro · sprint deliverable handoff · 30-day post-launch warranty (bug fixes only).

8. **Negotiation walk-away discipline.** Specific buyer asks paired with defensible counters and explicit walk-away signals. Walking is a tool, not a failure — disqualifying yourself from a wrong-fit deal is the highest-leverage move.

9. **Sales → Delivery handoff** at Stage 7 (Closed-Won). Mandatory 30-min handoff call with BA/PM. Sales remains as warm-handoff support for first 30 days. Long-term ownership: P5 = same person across AI projects, P3 = formal AM, P4 = AE owns and never transitions.

10. **Account expansion targets the ~30 active accounts, not the 800 historical.** "We worked with you 5 years ago" plays are forbidden — those are cold leads, not active accounts. Per saved memory and [[Trioangle Growth Plan]].

11. **Service-ICP-specific KPI ranges** (calibrated for $10k–$120k+ engagements, 45–180 day cycles, sprint-gated proposals) — explicitly *not* the same as Product-ICP volume metrics. Sample: average deal size $25k–$60k blended; sales cycle 45–90 days (P5), 60–120 (P1/P2), 90–180 (P3), 30–60 (P4).

12. **Closed-lost post-mortem mandatory for deals >$25k.** Within 14 days of close-lost, 15-min call with the buyer ("not asking you to reconsider — asking what we got wrong"). Updates the lead-scoring rubric if the loss reveals a missing signal.
