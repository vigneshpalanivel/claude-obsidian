# Scaler: What Is AI Engineering (2026)

**Source**: [scaler.com](https://www.scaler.com/topics/what-is-ai-engineering/) | Scaler Topics | 2026

---

## Summary

A foundational definition of AI engineering as a distinct discipline from ML engineering and data science. Useful as the anchor definition for the [[AI Engineering]] concept page.

---

## Key Claims

**Definition** — "AI engineering is the work of turning artificial intelligence models into usable systems." It involves integrating trained models into production applications that handle real users, large datasets, system failures, and performance constraints. (confidence: high)

**Core responsibilities** — Converting ML/LLM models into products, building APIs and deployment infra, monitoring + cost control, ensuring reliability at scale, integrating AI with broader applications. (confidence: high)

**Vs. ML Engineering** — ML engineers focus on training pipelines and classical model deployment. AI engineers have wider scope: generative AI, RAG architectures, full system integration, stronger product orientation. (confidence: high)

**Vs. Data Science** — Data scientists emphasize analysis and business insights. AI engineers build production systems, not reports or dashboards. (confidence: high)

**2026 skills stack**: Python + clean API design, ML fundamentals, deep learning (neural networks, transformers), generative AI (LLMs, prompt engineering, RAG, embeddings), deployment/MLOps (Docker, cloud, monitoring). (confidence: high)
