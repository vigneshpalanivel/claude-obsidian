# Cornell LII — 17 CFR § 230.902 (Reg S Definitions)

## Source Summary

The authoritative regulatory text of Rule 902 — the definitions section that drives all of Reg S. Whenever a Reg S deal touches a hard edge, this is what counsel reads. Cornell LII republishes the eCFR text under stable URLs.

## What It Contributes

### Rule 902(k) — U.S. Person
Eight enumerated categories:
1. Natural person resident in the U.S.
2. Partnership or corporation organized or incorporated under U.S. law
3. Estate of which any executor or administrator is a U.S. person
4. Trust of which any trustee is a U.S. person
5. Agency or branch of a foreign entity located in the U.S.
6. Non-discretionary fiduciary account (other than estate or trust) for the benefit of a U.S. person
7. Discretionary account held by a dealer or fiduciary organized under U.S. law
8. Foreign entity organized by a U.S. person principally to invest in securities not registered under the Act (with carve-out for accredited investor entities not formed only by U.S. natural persons)

**Carve-outs (Rule 902(k)(2))** — these are *not* U.S. persons:
- Discretionary accounts held by foreign professional fiduciaries for U.S. clients
- Estates with foreign professional fiduciaries (when the estate is governed by foreign law)
- Foreign branches of U.S. banks/insurance companies in genuine local business
- IGOs (UN, IMF, World Bank, etc.)
- Employee benefit plans established under foreign law

### Rule 902(h) — Offshore Transaction
Two-prong test:
- (1) Offer is not made to a person in the United States, AND
- (2) Either:
  - (i) Buyer is outside the U.S. (or seller reasonably believes so) at the time the buy order originates, OR
  - (ii) Trade is executed on/through a physical trading floor of a foreign exchange or designated offshore securities market, and no pre-arrangement with a U.S. buyer

### Rule 902(c) — Directed Selling Efforts
"Any activity undertaken for the purpose of, or that could reasonably be expected to have the effect of, conditioning the market in the United States for any of the securities being offered in reliance on this Regulation S."

Includes: ads in publications with general U.S. circulation that mention the Reg S offering. Excludes: tombstone ads required by law (with disclaimer), routine bond market commentary, customary corporate disclosure.

### Rule 902(j) — Substantial U.S. Market Interest (SUSMI)
Equity SUSMI test: in the prior fiscal year, U.S. exchanges/inter-dealer markets were the single largest market for the class, OR ≥ 20% of trading occurred in U.S. markets and < some threshold abroad.

Debt SUSMI test: ≥ 300 U.S. record holders + ≥ 20% held by U.S. persons + ≥ $1B principal amount.

### Rule 902(f) — Distribution Compliance Period
Successor terminology to "restricted period" — the post-closing window during which Reg S resales to U.S. persons are blocked unless under another exemption.
