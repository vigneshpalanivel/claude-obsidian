# Cognizant

**Type**: US-headquartered IT services company, India delivery model (considered part of Indian IT)  
**HQ**: Teaneck, New Jersey, USA  
**Revenue (FY25)**: ~$20B

## AI Strategy (2025–2026)

**Declared goal**: Be the essential integration and transformation partner for enterprise AI adoption — through domain expertise, system integration, and workforce readiness.

## Workforce-First Differentiator
Cognizant's most distinctive bet is on workforce transformation as a product:

- **Skillspring** — AI-native conversational learning platform; embeds learning in workflow, aligns skills/roles to AI-driven work changes
- **Synapse** — train-to-hire, reskilling 1M+ individuals by 2026; targets underserved communities
- **Fresher hiring plan**: 24,000–25,000 in 2026 (+20% vs 2025); selecting for learnability > experience
- Junior uplift via Anthropic, Gemini, OpenAI partnerships

## 2026 Enterprise AI Predictions (Leadership View)
Notable non-obvious calls:
- **Context engineering** is more important than prompt engineering — capturing undocumented human knowledge for agent deployment
- **Productivity paradox**: AI makes employees busier, not less so (yet)
- Traditional departmental hierarchies will dissolve; dynamic AI-assembled teams
- End-to-end autonomy: self-orchestrating agent ecosystems are the next leap

## Competitive Positioning
Unlike TCS/Infosys (platform-heavy), Cognizant positions on sector-specific, context-driven solutions and client integration expertise. Strong in healthcare AI, financial services AI.

## Hiring Signal
Planning 25K fresher hires in 2026 while most peers cut headcount — bet that human-in-loop AI delivery at scale still requires large trained workforces.
