# Accenture

**Type**: Global professional services and IT consulting company  
**HQ**: Dublin, Ireland  
**Revenue (FY25)**: ~$67B  
**AI staff**: 80,000+ data and AI professionals

## AI Strategy (2025–2026)

**Declared goal**: "Reinvention" — AI embedded across everything; no longer a standalone business unit.

## Financial Performance
- **Advanced AI revenues (Q1 FY26)**: $1.1B (+120% YoY)
- **Advanced AI bookings (Q1 FY26)**: $2.2B (+76% YoY)
- **GenAI + Agentic AI revenues (12mo to Aug 2025)**: $2.7B (tripled)
- **GenAI bookings (12mo)**: $5.9B (nearly doubled)
- **Addressable AI market**: $70B by 2029 (40%+ CAGR)

## Scale
- 80,000+ AI and data professionals (doubled in 2 years)
- 550,000+ employees trained in GenAI fundamentals ("reinventors")
- 1,300+ AI clients; 3,000+ reusable AI agents deployed

## Key Platforms
- **AI Refinery** — enterprise AI ecosystem connector
- **GenWizard** — IT delivery automation platform

## Major Acquisition
- **Faculty** (Jan 2026, ~$1B) — 400+ AI specialists; Faculty CEO Marc Warner becomes Accenture CTO

## Strategic Framework
CEO Julie Sweet stopped reporting AI as separate metric — AI is now in "nearly everything." Framing: "Cognitive Digital Brain" = business reinvention, not AI feature add.

## Partnerships
OpenAI, Anthropic, Snowflake, Palantir — model + platform partnerships for enterprise delivery.

## Competitive Position
Scale and consulting credibility that Indian IT majors lack. Competes at top of market: C-suite AI strategy, enterprise reinvention, managed AI at global scale. First-mover in AI bookings disclosure — other firms playing catch-up to this level of AI revenue specificity.
