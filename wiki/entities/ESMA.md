# ESMA: European Securities and Markets Authority

**ESMA** is the EU's independent financial markets regulator and supervisor, based in **Paris**. Established 2011 under Regulation (EU) 1095/2010. Role: supervisory convergence across national competent authorities (NCAs) — ESMA sets rules and guidelines; NCAs enforce them at country level.

---

## Key Functions

- **Technical standards**: Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS) and Implementing Technical Standards (ITS) under MiFID II, MiCA, EMIR, CSDR, and others
- **Supervisory convergence**: Peer reviews, Q&A publications, guidelines to harmonize NCA enforcement
- **Direct supervision**: Certain entities (credit rating agencies, trade repositories, data reporting services) report directly to ESMA
- **Consumer protection**: Powers to temporarily prohibit or restrict financial products that threaten investor protection
- **Market monitoring**: Surveillance of systemic risk, market integrity, short selling

## ESMA and the MiFID II / MiCA Boundary

ESMA's March 2025 guidelines are **binding on NCAs**: national regulators must apply the cascade classification test when determining whether a crypto-asset is a financial instrument (MiFID II) or a crypto-asset (MiCA). This resolves prior jurisdictional inconsistency between member states.

---

## NCA Enforcement Landscape (2024)

- France (**AMF**): highest aggregate fines (€29.4M in 2024)
- Germany (**BaFin**): highest single fine (€12.9M, Citigroup, Art 17 algorithmic trading)
- ESMA has flagged "discrepancies in national enforcement action" and is pushing for greater convergence
- Total EU MiFID II/MiFIR fines 2024: **€44.5M** across **189 fines / 294 actions**

---

## Practical Note

ESMA guidelines ≠ binding regulation on firms directly — ESMA binds NCAs, who then apply the guidelines in their national jurisdiction. But "comply or explain" creates de facto mandatory compliance in all but unusual cases.
