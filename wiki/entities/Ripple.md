# Ripple

Ripple is a fintech company building blockchain-based cross-border payment infrastructure. It operates the XRP Ledger (XRPL) and offers enterprise payment solutions to banks and financial institutions globally.

## Core Products

- **RippleNet**: Global payments network connecting banks and payment providers; replaces correspondent banking with blockchain settlement
- **On-Demand Liquidity (ODL)**: Uses XRP as a bridge currency for real-time cross-border payments without pre-funded nostro accounts
- **RLUSD**: Ripple's USD-backed stablecoin
- **XRP Ledger (XRPL)**: Public blockchain purpose-built for payments; 1,500 TPS, 3–5 second finality

## Recent Developments (2025–2026)

- **December 2025**: OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency) granted conditional approval for Ripple's national trust bank charter — one of five digital asset charters approved
- **Acquisition spree**: 7 startup acquisitions in 2 years, including:
  - **Hidden Road** (prime brokerage)
  - **GTreasury** (treasury management software)
  - **Rail** (stablecoin payment platform)
- Pursuing **full-stack financial infrastructure** strategy: custody + payments + treasury + stablecoin

## Strategic Significance

Ripple's bank charter approval signals a regulatory maturation moment — crypto-native companies can now access traditional banking privileges. The acquisition strategy positions Ripple as a one-stop-shop for institutional digital asset infrastructure, competing directly with traditional correspondent banking networks.

## Distinction from CBDCs

Ripple has been selected by multiple central banks to explore CBDC infrastructure, running CBDC pilots on a private version of XRPL. Unlike its commercial stablecoin products, these are wholesale CBDC experiments for central banks.
