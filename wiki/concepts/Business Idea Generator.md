# Business Idea Generator

A repeatable process for finding, validating, and filtering business ideas — from broad inspiration to a shortlist worth pursuing.

---

## Step 1 — Browse Curated Ideas

Start with idea directories to seed your thinking. These surface real opportunities that others are already validating.

> [!tip] Start Here
> **[IdeaBrowser](https://www.ideabrowser.com/)** — curated database of business ideas with market context, difficulty ratings, and revenue potential. Browse by category or filter by effort level and market size.

Other sources to layer in:
- [Trends.vc](https://trends.vc) — weekly emerging trend reports with business angle breakdowns
- [Starter Story](https://www.starterstory.com/) — real founder stories with revenue numbers and how they started
- [Exploding Topics](https://explodingtopics.com/) — fast-growing niches before they peak
- [Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/) — what people are building and what's getting traction

---

## Step 2 — Define Your Filter Criteria

Before evaluating ideas, set your personal constraints. An idea that fits someone else may not fit you.

**Your filter (fill in):**

| Criteria | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| Revenue model preference | (SaaS / agency / marketplace / product) |
| Time to first ₹ | (weeks / months / 1+ year) |
| Capital available | (bootstrap / ₹X seed budget) |
| Existing skills you'd leverage | (dev, sales, design, etc.) |
| Audience you can reach | (existing clients, LinkedIn, community) |
| Risk tolerance | (low / medium / high) |

> [!note] For Trioangle
> Filter for ideas that leverage existing dev capabilities, sell to Indian SMBs or global SaaS, and can reach MRR within 6 months.

---

## Step 3 — Generate Your Own Ideas

Layer your constraints on top of discovered problems. Use these prompts:

**Problem-first:**
- What do your existing clients complain about repeatedly?
- What do you manually do that could be automated?
- What does your industry pay consultants for that could be productized?

**Skill-first:**
- What can you build in a weekend that others would pay for?
- What have you built for clients that multiple people need?
- What tool do you use daily that has a worse competitor you could beat?

**Trend-first:**
- What new regulations create compliance needs?
- What new tech (AI, voice, mobile) is underserved in your market?
- What behavior shift happened post-COVID that's now permanent?

---

## Step 4 — Score Each Idea

Rate each idea 1–5 on each dimension. High total = worth validating.

| Idea | Market Size | Urgency | Ease of Entry | Your Advantage | Revenue Speed | **Total** |
|------|------------|---------|--------------|----------------|--------------|-----------|
| Idea A | | | | | | |
| Idea B | | | | | | |
| Idea C | | | | | | |

**Scoring guide:**
- **Market Size** — 5 = millions of buyers; 1 = very niche
- **Urgency** — 5 = people are actively searching for a solution right now; 1 = nice-to-have
- **Ease of Entry** — 5 = you can ship an MVP in days; 1 = requires years/capital
- **Your Advantage** — 5 = you have unique access, skills, or relationships; 1 = anyone can do this
- **Revenue Speed** — 5 = first paying customer within 30 days; 1 = 12+ months to revenue

---

## Step 5 — Validate Before Building

> [!warning] Don't skip this step
> Most ideas die in validation. Better to find out now than after 3 months of building.

**Validation checklist:**

- [ ] Can you find 5 people who have this problem right now?
- [ ] Have you asked them what they currently do about it?
- [ ] Did any of them offer to pay before you build?
- [ ] Is there a working competitor (proof of market)?
- [ ] Can you describe the customer in one sentence?

**Fastest validation method:** Write a one-page landing page describing the solution. Post it in relevant communities. Count signups or DMs within 72 hours.

---

## Step 6 — Choose One and Define the MVP

Pick the highest-scoring validated idea. Define the smallest version that generates revenue.

**MVP template:**

- **The problem:** [one sentence]
- **The customer:** [who exactly, not "everyone"]
- **The solution:** [what you're building]
- **Revenue model:** [how you charge]
- **MVP scope:** [what's in v1 — ruthlessly scoped]
- **Time to ship:** [realistic, not optimistic]
- **First customer target:** [specific person or channel]
