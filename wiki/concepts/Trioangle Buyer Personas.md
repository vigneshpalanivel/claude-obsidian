# Trioangle Buyer Personas

Six buyer archetypes for [[Trioangle]]'s product line (clone scripts and super-app platforms). Each archetype is defined by **who the buyer emotionally is**, not which product they intend to buy. The archetype determines the sales approach; the product line determines which demo to run.

---

## Quick Reference

| # | Archetype | Core Emotion | Speed | Budget | Key Proof |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Visionary Velocity Founder | First-mover urgency | Fast (<20 days) | $1k–$8k | Live demo, app store links |
| 2 | Burned Builder Comeback | Controlled determination | Slow (30–60 days) | $3k–$12k | Testimonials, refund policy, live chat |
| 3 | Diaspora Digital Entrepreneur | Community pride + opportunity | Moderate (20–40 days) | $5k–$15k | Diaspora case studies, multilingual demo |
| 4 | Small Business Digitalizer | Survival (not disruption) | Slow (30–60 days) | $1k–$6k | Admin panel walkthrough, ROI calculator |
| 5 | Super-App Ecosystem Builder | Regional dominance | Slow (30–60 days) | $8k–$25k | Full Gojek demo, multi-service proof |
| 6 | AI Builder Who Hit the Wall | Sunk-cost urgency | Fast (<14 days) | $1k–$8k | App store approvals, deployment SLA |

---

## Persona 1 — Visionary Velocity Founder

**Who**: Solo Founder / CEO, 25–35, pre-launch. Spots a local market gap and must move before a competitor does. Already knows what they want to build — they're choosing a vendor, not evaluating whether to build.

**Emotional driver**: Fear of losing first-mover advantage. Time is the primary constraint.

**Do say**: *"Launch your MVP in 2 weeks and save $50k"*
**Don't say**: *"We will architect a custom proprietary platform"*

**AI-era warning**: Most vulnerable persona to displacement by improving AI deployment tools (Lovable, Bolt). When AI deployment automation matures, the production gap narrows. Counter with **certainty of outcome** — guaranteed live date, 800+ proven deployments, App Store approval record. Speed to *live* beats speed to demo.

**Key objection**: *"Why not just use Lovable or Cursor?"*
> AI builds the demo in days. Getting it live on the App Store with real GPS dispatch, real payment flows, and real users is the hard part. Most Persona 6 buyers spent 4–8 weeks in Cursor before landing here.

---

## Persona 2 — Burned Builder Comeback Founder

**Who**: Founder / Business Owner, 30–45, has already failed once — vanishing freelancer, outdated script, or increasingly: weeks of vibe coding that produced a demo, never a live app. Lost $5k–$20k on the first attempt.

**Emotional driver**: Controlled determination. Will not be fooled again. Exhaustively researches every vendor before engaging.

**Do say**: *"Dedicated support team, free installation, 100% functional out of the box"*
**Don't say**: *"Just download and configure it yourself"*

**Proof required**: Video testimonials, transparent refund/support policy, ability to speak with a past buyer directly.

**Key objection**: *"I've been burned before. How is this different?"*
> Operating since 2015, 800+ live businesses across 150+ countries. Free installation, app store submission, 6-month support. Here are real apps in the stores — call those founders directly.

---

## Persona 3 — Diaspora Digital Entrepreneur

**Who**: Founder / Investor, 28–40, lives in US/UK/Canada/Gulf but sees a clear opportunity in their home country. Has disposable income from Western employment and deep local market knowledge.

**Emotional driver**: Community pride, financial independence back home, staying connected.

**Do say**: *"Fully managed remote setup — we handle servers, deployment, and app store submission"*
**Don't say**: *"You'll need a local server admin to manage the deployment"*

**Channel**: Diaspora WhatsApp groups and Facebook communities. Peer referral is the highest-trust signal for this persona.

---

## Persona 4 — Small Business Digitalizer

**Who**: Business Owner / Operations Manager, 35–55, runs a successful offline business (taxi fleet, cleaning company, food delivery by phone) losing share to app-based competitors. Already has customers, drivers, and providers.

**Emotional driver**: Digital survival — not disruption. Wants technology on top of what already works.

**Do say**: *"Add an app to your existing business — customers book through the app, you manage from a simple dashboard"*
**Don't say**: *"Disrupt the market with a cutting-edge on-demand platform"*

**Key objection**: *"My staff aren't tech-savvy."*
> Driver app: accept job, navigate, mark complete. Most drivers learn it in under 30 minutes. We've onboarded fleets of 200+ drivers for buyers who had the same concern.

---

## Persona 5 — Super-App Ecosystem Builder

**Who**: Founder / CEO, 28–45, has validated one vertical and wants to build the Gojek of their region. May be an existing operator or a well-funded founder racing against big players.

**Emotional driver**: Regional dominance before a funded competitor moves in.

**Do say**: *"One app, all your on-demand services — full control from a single admin dashboard. Launch in under 30 days."*
**Don't say**: *"Start with ride-hailing and add features later"*

**Sales note**: Qualify with the Gojek native scope check. If all required services are within native scope (taxi, delivery, grocery, car wash, laundry, beauty, handyman, DeliveryAll) → Gojek Super-App, no merge needed. Any service outside scope → Developer Merge required.

---

## Persona 6 — AI Builder Who Hit the Wall

**Who**: Technical Solopreneur / Developer-adjacent Founder, 22–38. Started with Cursor, Lovable, or Replit and built a working demo in days. Then hit the wall: App Store rejection, GPS tracking failure at scale, payment gateway complexity, broken dispatch logic. Has invested 4–8 weeks and has something that looks like a product but can't go live.

**Emotional driver**: Sunk-cost urgency. Already invested time and possibly money. The moment they accept their prototype won't make production, they move fast.

**Do say**: *"Production-ready and App Store approved from day one — real GPS tracking, real payment flows, real support when it goes live"*
**Don't say**: *"You can always customise the code yourself"*

**Key objection**: *"My prototype is 80% done. Why throw it away?"*
> Your prototype is 80% of a demo — not 80% of a live business. The remaining 20% is App Store compliance, production server architecture, real-time dispatch under load, payment gateway production setup. That 20% will take 3–6 more months with AI tools — and you'll maintain it alone with no SLA. Our platform has crossed that finish line 800+ times.

**Cross-link**: This archetype is the most direct product of the [[Vibe Coding]] wave. Directly validates findings in [[Research: Running a Software Dev Company Post-Vibe-Coding]] — code generation is 25-35% of a live product; the rest (infrastructure, compliance, App Store, production ops) is where the real work lives.

## Sales Routing Logic

```
Archetype identified?
  ↓
Persona 1 or 6 → Lead with live demo link + app store links; respond same day
Persona 2 → Lead with testimonial video + trust signals; never rush
Persona 3 → Lead with diaspora case study; connect to buyer from their home region
Persona 4 → Lead with admin panel walkthrough + ROI calculator
Persona 5 → Run full Gojek demo; qualify service scope first
```
