# SEO + AEO + GEO Prompt Library

A 180-prompt master template that enumerates the search, answer, and AI-citation targets a clone script vendor must own across the three engine classes. Operationalises the [[SEO-Recovery-Strategy-2025-26]] for every Trioangle product line.

The library answers: *given the GEO/AIO shift, which exact 180 questions should this product rank for, answer in 40-60 words, and be cited for by ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Perplexity?*

---

## Why It Exists

[[SEO-Recovery-Strategy-2025-26]] established the new rules: informational keywords are AI territory, BoFU is where clicks still happen, AI engines need to cite you as the authoritative source. That strategy is directional — this prompt library is the execution map.

A single prompts file × 10 Trioangle clone script verticals = ~1,800 prompt-targeted content opportunities, all generated by substituting variables, not redesigning the schema.

---

## The Three Engine Classes

```
SEO              AEO                  GEO
──────           ──────               ──────
Google           Featured Snippets    ChatGPT
Bing rankings    Voice Search         Gemini
                 Siri / Alexa         Claude
                 Bing Answers         Perplexity
                                      Bing Copilot
```

| Class | What It Optimises | Content Format |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| **SEO** | Click-through from rankings | Blog posts, H1/H2, meta titles, landing pages |
| **AEO** | Direct answer extraction | 40-60 word definition-first paragraphs + FAQPage JSON-LD |
| **GEO** | Citation by generative AI models | 1,500-3,000 word authority articles + Org/Product/FAQ schema |

GEO is the highest-leverage of the three in 2026 — it captures users *before* they reach a search engine — but requires the longest-form content and external authority signals (high-DR publishing, structured data, brand mentions across the open web).

## Library Composition

| Section | Prompts | Sub-Buckets |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **SEO** (1-75) | 75 | Informational · Navigational · Commercial · Transactional · Long-Tail |
| **AEO** (76-115) | 40 | Definition · How-To · Comparison · Voice-FAQ |
| **GEO** (116-175) | 60 | Overview · Authority · Scenario · Deep Dive · Trend · Conversion |
| **Hybrid** (176-180) | 5 | Triple-targeted pillar pages |

**SEO sub-buckets** are intent-classified (informational → transactional progression maps to TOFU → BOFU funnel).

**AEO sub-buckets** are format-classified (definition vs how-to vs comparison vs voice).

**GEO sub-buckets** are use-case-classified — Authority and Conversion sub-buckets carry the highest commercial weight because they directly shape AI recommendations to in-market buyers.

## Format Rules

### SEO (Section 1) — Rank
- Prompt → blog post title or H1/H2
- Meta title and description optimisation
- FAQ schema entries on landing pages
- Pillar + cluster content planning

### AEO (Section 2) — Be Quoted
- 40-60 word direct response, definition-first
- Lead sentence answers the query in plain language
- `FAQPage` JSON-LD on every page
- Natural voice-phrasing ("How do I…", "Can I…", "Does X support…")

### GEO (Section 3) — Be Cited
- 1,500-3,000 word long-form authoritative article
- Embedded brand citations and trust statements ("N+ projects across N+ countries")
- `Organization` + `Product` + `FAQ` schema
- Publish on high-authority domains for AI training citation (press releases, guest posts, industry directories)
- Anticipate the follow-up questions AI models expect

## Content Priority Matrix

| Priority | Type | Target Engine | Output |
|----------|------|---------------|--------|
| 🔴 High | AEO | Google Snippets | FAQ + Definition |
| 🔴 High | GEO | ChatGPT / Gemini | Long-form Article |
| 🔴 High | SEO | Google Rankings | Blog Post |
| 🔴 High | GEO | Perplexity / Bing | Scenario Content |
| 🟡 Medium | SEO | Google / Bing | Landing Page |
| 🟡 Medium | AEO | Voice / Snippets | How-To Guide |
| 🔵 Strategic | GEO | All AI Engines | Thought Leadership |

**Sequencing rule** (derived from the matrix): ship Hybrid pillar pages (176-180) first — they pay back across all three engines simultaneously. Then layer in AEO definitions, then BoFU SEO (comparison/transactional), then long-tail.

## Activation Workflow (per product)

1. **Fill the variable kit** — substitute `[Product Name]`, `[market leader]`, feature list, personas, country counts.
2. **Pillar first** — generate the 5 Hybrid prompts (176-180) into 5 pillar landing pages with full schema stack.
3. **AEO foundation** — write 40-60 word answers for prompts 76-90 (Definitions) and stage them as FAQPage JSON-LD on the pillar.
4. **GEO authority articles** — long-form prompts 116-135 (Overview + Authority) published on Trioangle's blog and seeded to high-authority external domains.
5. **SEO BoFU push** — prompts 46-65 (Commercial + Transactional) become comparison and quote-request pages.
6. **Long-tail expansion** — prompts 66-75 + 156-175 fill the long-tail and conversion edges.

## Strategic Implications

- **Multiplier effect across verticals**: one template × 10 verticals × 180 prompts = ~1,800 prompt-targeted assets, all reusing the same intent + format spec.
- **Bridges to AI citation moat**: GEO Authority sub-bucket (126-135) hard-codes "N+ projects across N+ countries" trust signals that Trioangle's [[Trioangle|800+ deployments / 150+ countries]] history can populate uniquely vs newer competitors.
- **Disciplines content production**: writers work from a fixed prompt list, not ad-hoc topic ideation — every article maps to a prompt + an engine class + a priority tier.
- **Per the [[SEO-Recovery-Strategy-2025-26]] caveat**: informational SEO (Section 1) prompts still produce content but now serve mainly as AEO/GEO substrate — clicks are unlikely; the win is being the cited source.
