# Trioangle Channel Partner Personas

Three intermediary archetypes for [[Trioangle]]'s channel partner program. These are **not** end-buyers of software; they are agencies and consulting firms who sub-contract development delivery to Trioangle. Each persona is defined by **what they are trying to expand** within their own business.

Channel partners are the only path to two [[Trioangle Service ICP]] segments (Digital Transformation Enterprise Buyer and MENA Relationship Buyer) that are not reachable via Trioangle's direct outbound.

---

## Quick Reference

| # | Persona | Core Motivation | Decision Speed | End Client Budget Unlocked |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Overflow Agency Lead | Say YES to every client project without hiring | Moderate (2-6 weeks once live opportunity exists) | $10k-$120k |
| 2 | Consulting Upseller | Capture build revenue after strategy engagement | Slow (4-8 weeks due diligence) | $30k-$120k |
| 3 | Vertical Specialist | Own full project lifecycle in their niche | Moderate (2-4 weeks with live client brief) | $30k-$120k |

---

## Persona 1 - The "Overflow Agency Lead"

**Who**: Founder / Managing Director, 32-50, heads a 10-50 person digital agency. Has grown through web design, branding, marketing. Clients now ask for apps. Has 1-2 in-house developers. Can't run 3 large development projects simultaneously.

**Core motivation**: Grow revenue without growing headcount. Protect client relationship and agency brand.

**Emotional posture**: Protective. Has been burned by generic offshore partners. Wants a delivery partner who is invisible to end clients but rock-solid in execution.

**Do say**: *"We are your invisible build team - you own the client, we deliver the code. Sub-contracting model with full NDA and non-circumvention."*
**Don't say**: *"We'll contact the client directly without your involvement."*

**Key proof needed**: NDA-compatible case studies showing domain depth, delivery SLA examples, testimonials from other agencies specifically on communication reliability and non-circumvention trust.

**JTBD**: *"When I win a project brief that requires mobile or web app development, I want a reliable silent build partner I can hand off to without risk, so I can grow revenue without growing my engineering headcount."*

**Key objection**: *"How do I know you won't go directly to my client and cut me out?"*
> We sign a strict NCNDA on every partner deal. Your client relationship is yours - we operate as a named or unnamed sub-contractor, never the prime vendor to your client.

**End clients served**: Primarily [[ICP-Product-Trioangle]] buyers (clone script range $500-$25k) and lower-tier [[Trioangle Service ICP]] Founder-CTO buyers ($10k-$40k custom builds).

---

## Persona 2 - The "Consulting Upseller"

**Who**: Principal Consultant / Partner, 38-55, at a boutique IT or strategy consulting firm (5-30 people). Builds transformation roadmaps that end with a "technology build" phase. Historically hands clients to a dev firm at this stage and loses the revenue.

**Core motivation**: Capture implementation revenue post-consulting. Transform the firm from "advisor" to "advisor + implementer" without building a dev team.

**Emotional posture**: Analytical, credibility-driven. Not an engineer, but evaluates technical quality from outcome/delivery perspective. Does formal due diligence - multiple calls, RFP process, reference checks.

**Do say**: *"We offer a formal sub-contracting partnership with SLAs, documentation standards, and white-label options - compatible with your consultancy's quality requirements."*
**Don't say**: *"Just send us the brief and we'll figure it out."*

**Key proof needed**: Formal partnership agreement template, delivery SLA document, sample project proposal in consultant-friendly format (executive summary, milestones, deliverables, risk matrix), domain case studies with business outcomes.

**JTBD**: *"When my consulting engagement reaches the technology implementation phase, I want a credible delivery partner I can bring in under my brand, so I can capture the build revenue and maintain the client relationship end-to-end."*

**Key objection**: *"How can I trust an outsourced team to meet the quality standards I've promised my client?"*
> We build to your documentation standard and give your client-facing PM full sprint visibility via shared dashboards. Load testing and QA reports included as deliverables - ready for your client presentation.

**End clients served**: Primarily mid-high [[Trioangle Service ICP]] personas - Scaling CTO ($30k-$80k), Digital Transformation Enterprise Buyer ($50k-$120k), MENA Relationship Buyer ($30k-$80k). This persona is the primary access path to the Enterprise Buyer segment.

> **Strategic note**: MENA-based consulting partners specifically unlock [[Trioangle Service ICP]] Persona 4 (MENA Relationship Buyer), who is not reachable through Trioangle's direct outbound channels.

## Persona 3 - The "Vertical Specialist"

**Who**: Founder / Head of Delivery, 30-48, at a boutique agency specializing in one industry (logistics, real estate, hospitality, on-demand, rentals). Has deep domain credibility but gets pigeonholed in strategy and basic web work. Clients want full platforms; the firm keeps losing implementation revenue to generalist dev shops.

**Core motivation**: Own end-to-end project delivery in their niche without hiring a specialist engineering team.

**Emotional posture**: Confident in domain knowledge, frustrated at capability ceiling. The sub-contractor's domain depth must equal or exceed their own - they cannot afford to educate a dev partner about their industry.

**Do say**: *"We've delivered platforms in your exact vertical - logistics dispatch logic, property marketplace flows, real estate multi-listing architecture - already solved, not learned on your client's budget."*
**Don't say**: *"We build for any industry."*

**Key proof needed**: Case studies with business outcomes specific to their industry vertical, named references in the same niche, architecture overview of how similar platforms were built, team bios showing vertical experience.

**JTBD**: *"When a client in my niche asks me to build a platform I don't deliver in-house, I want a specialist dev partner who already understands the domain logic, so I can own the full project lifecycle without losing credibility or margin."*

**Key objection**: *"How do I know your team understands our industry well enough to build for it?"*
> We've delivered 800+ projects across 150+ countries - logistics dispatch platforms, home services marketplaces, real estate portals, on-demand delivery apps - already live and running. We'll show you a live app in your exact vertical before the contract is signed.

**End clients served**: Primarily [[Trioangle Service ICP]] Scaling CTO ($30k-$80k) and Digital Transformation Enterprise Buyer ($50k-$120k) within the consulting firm's specific industry vertical.

## Partner Sales Motion

### Routing Logic

```
Partner type identified?
  ↓
P1 (Overflow Agency) → Lead with NCNDA template + live vertical demo; emphasize invisible sub-contractor model
P2 (Consulting Upseller) → Lead with formal partnership pack (SLA doc, proposal template, case studies with business outcomes); expect 4-8 week evaluation
P3 (Vertical Specialist) → Lead with vertical-specific live demo + named reference in their exact niche; domain credibility check is the gate
```

### Non-Circumvention is the Prerequisite

All three personas must clear the trust barrier before any client project is introduced. NCNDA must be signed on Day 1 - before any client details are shared. Without this, no partner engagement proceeds.

### Margin Model

Trioangle quotes a net development cost. The agency adds their project management, strategy, and agency margin on top. Most partners target 25-50% net margin on build cost.

### Discovery Sprint as Deal Accelerator

A paid Discovery Sprint ($1,500-$4,000) de-risks the agency's proposal to their end client. Deliverables: requirements spec, architecture blueprint, feature scope, fixed-price delivery estimate. All three personas can use this - particularly valuable for P2 (Consulting Upseller) whose clients expect formal documentation.

## Partner Onboarding Success Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| NCNDA + sub-contract signed | Day 1 | Removes primary trust barrier |
| First sprint kickoff | Day 3-7 | Speed signals reliability - agency judges responsiveness in Week 1 |
| First sprint review to agency PM | Week 2-3 | Agency decides here if they'd bring a second client |
| Agency invoices end-client for build phase | Month 1 | Validates the margin model worked |

**Second-engagement signal**: If a partner does not introduce a second client brief within 6 months of completing the first build, conduct a retention interview. Channel partner churn is as damaging as end-client churn.
