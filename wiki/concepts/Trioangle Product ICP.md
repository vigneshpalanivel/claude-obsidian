# Trioangle Product ICP

The ideal customer profile for [[Trioangle]]'s **product line** — readymade clone scripts and super-app platforms ($500–$25k). Covers the customer profile, qualifying criteria, product tier routing, pain points, and goals. The 6 buyer archetypes that map to this ICP are documented in [[Trioangle Buyer Personas]].

---

## Customer Profile

### Firmographics

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Size** | 1-5 employees (solopreneur / co-founders) |
| **Revenue** | Pre-revenue or <$10k/mo |
| **Stage** | Pre-launch / Bootstrapped / Early Traction |
| **Geography** | Priority: North America, UK, EU, Middle East. Secondary: SE Asia, Africa, Latin America, India Tier-2/3 cities |
| **Business model** | Trioangle sells B2B (to founders); buyer's platform operates B2C/B2B2C commission-based marketplace |

### Technographics

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Current solution** | Free tools + freelancers + manual ops; or an AI-generated prototype (Cursor/Lovable/Replit) that works in demo but cannot be deployed to production or App Stores |
| **Tech maturity** | Laggard to Mainstream — understands the business of tech, not the code |
| **Adoption readiness** | High, but needs white-glove onboarding (install, server setup, App Store submission) |

### Demographics

- **Age:** 25-45
- **Income:** Priority markets: $50k-$150k/yr; Secondary markets: $15k-$60k/yr local equivalent
- **Education:** University-educated (business/management); self-taught in digital marketing

### Psychographics

| Archetype identity | Core self-perception |
|---|---|
| Persona 1 | *"The Uber of my city"* |
| Persona 2 | *"The founder who finally gets it done"* |
| Persona 3 | *"Building something real back home"* |
| Persona 4 | *"A business owner who stays ahead"* |
| Persona 5 | *"The Gojek of my region"* |
| Persona 6 | *"I built the demo in a weekend — I just need it to actually work"* |

**Values:** Independence, speed, financial freedom, building something of their own
**Risk tolerance:** Moderate — willing to invest $1k-$25k but highly ROI-conscious

### Behavioral Traits

**Discovery channels:** Google Search, YouTube, Reddit, IndieHackers, WhatsApp/Telegram founder groups; increasingly AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) — asking "best Uber clone script company" and acting on the first credible result.

**Purchase behavior:** Research-heavy; 2-4 weeks (fast personas) to 30-60 days (Burned Builder, Super-App Builder). Converts fast once trust is established via live demo.

**Buying triggers:** Competitor launch, price gap discovery, live demo, AI tool recommending a marketplace app as passive income vehicle, failed AI-assisted development hitting the production wall (App Store rejection, real-time failure at scale, payment gateway complexity).

## Product Tier Routing Logic

```
Does buyer need 1 service only?
  → Single-Vertical Script ($500–$15k)

Do all required services fall within Gojek native scope?
  (taxi, delivery, grocery, car wash, laundry, beauty, handyman, DeliveryAll)
  → Gojek-like Super-App ($8k–$25k) — admin toggle, zero dev work

Any service outside Gojek native scope?
  (Airbnb/Rental, eCommerce, Classifieds, Social/Entertainment)
  → Developer Merge (script price + merge fee)

Fully proprietary / unique business logic?
  → Route to [[Trioangle Service ICP]] (custom dev, $10k+)
```

**Key qualifying question:** *"Which specific services do you need — and do all of them fall within taxi, delivery, handyman, car wash, laundry, beauty, or grocery?"*

## Qualifying Criteria

| Type | Criteria |
|---|---|
| ✅ Must-Have | Clear digital business model; defined market or geography; budget matched to tier |
| 👍 Nice-to-Have | Local supply secured (drivers, providers, listings); basic marketing plan |
| ❌ Disqualifying | Needs fully proprietary custom architecture → route to [[Trioangle Service ICP]]; super-app ambition with sub-$5k budget; dev agencies reselling at scale without a specific live client project; "just exploring" without a concrete business idea; wants source code only to resell; wants the absolute cheapest option regardless of outcome risk — let them try a freelancer and re-engage as Persona 2 |

## 5 Pain Points

| # | Pain Point | Severity | Stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| PP1 | Idea-to-Execution Technical Chasm | 9/10 | Pre-Purchase |
| PP2 | Trust Deficit from Past Failures | 8/10 | Pre-Purchase |
| PP3 | Post-Purchase Technical Helplessness | 7/10 | Post-Purchase |
| PP4 | Feature Customization Anxiety | 6/10 | Both |
| PP5 | Scaling Uncertainty | 5/10 | Post-Purchase |

**PP1 detail:** AI tools have become the first workaround — buyers now pass through a two-stage pattern: (1) attempt with Cursor/Lovable, (2) hit the production wall (App Store rejection, GPS failure at scale, payment gateway complexity), then arrive at Trioangle. This is the Persona 6 creation mechanism.

**PP2 detail:** The Burned Builder persona carries prior scars from vanishing freelancers, outdated scripts, or weeks of vibe coding that produced a demo but never a live app. Proof-first, never pitch-first.

## Goals

| Priority | Goal | Metric | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Launch to iOS/Android App Store | First paying transaction on live app | <30 days |
| Secondary | Validate business model | 20+ active providers; 50+ real orders | 30-60 days |
| Secondary | Prove unit economics | Commission revenue covering hosting + support | 60-90 days |
| Secondary | App growth | 500+ registered users; 4.0+ App Store rating | 90 days |
| Secondary | Seed funding (Persona 1) | Investor deck backed by live MVP traction | 60-90 days |

**Dream Outcome:** A self-sustaining commission-based marketplace with 1,000+ active users, reliable provider supply, and dominant brand recognition in their local market — without building or maintaining infrastructure themselves.

**JTBD:** *"When I validate a market gap in my city, I want to deploy a reliable, fully-branded marketplace instantly, so I can focus on acquiring providers and customers rather than debugging infrastructure."*

## Post-Purchase Arc

| Stage | Timeline | Buyer State | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deployment | Day 0-7 | Anxious — "will it go live?" | White-glove install, App Store submission, daily updates |
| First Transaction | Day 7-30 | Relieved but fragile | Celebrate first order; share provider acquisition checklist |
| Early Traction | Day 30-90 | Cautiously optimistic | Check-in; offer first customization (local payment gateway, language pack) |
| Growth | Day 90-180 | Confident — "what's next?" | Upsell: Developer Merge (second vertical), Super-App upgrade |
| Advocacy | Day 180+ | Proud founder | Testimonial, case study, referral program activation |

**Upsell triggers:** Second vertical (Developer Merge), Super-App upgrade, custom feature requests, annual support renewal, referral conversion.

> **AI era retention principle:** AI tools create dependency loops (buyer keeps prompting to fix issues). Trioangle must create a support relationship loop — buyers who know Trioangle will handle problems faster than any AI tool will stay, refer others, and expand.
