# Trioangle Product ICP

The ideal customer profile for [[Trioangle]]'s **product line** — readymade clone scripts and super-app platforms ($500–$25k). Covers the customer profile, qualifying criteria, product-tier routing, pain points, goals, and post-purchase arc. The 6 buyer archetypes that map to this ICP are documented in [[Trioangle Buyer Personas]].

---

## Track 1 — Cash Engine (Two-Track Context)

This ICP defines **Track 1** of the Two-Track strategy ([[Trioangle Growth Plan]]). Track 1 is the cash engine — clone scripts and super-apps sold to startup-shaped founders.

- The Track 1 buyer profile carries **~96% historical 5-year mortality** (structural to pre-revenue / 1–5-employee buyers — not service quality, not fixable via pricing).
- Mortality is why **Track 1 is not the company's recurring base**. AI add-on attach (per [[Trioangle 2.0 AI Pivot Plan]]) and sporadic post-launch customization are *upside on Track 1* — not the recurring engine.
- **Recurring revenue lives on Track 2** — AI services for Service ICP existing-operators (12+ months operational, revenue traction, established business). When a Track 1 buyer matures into that profile post-launch (most commonly Persona 4 or Persona 5), they graduate into a Track 2 candidate via AI Audit Sprint. See [[Trioangle Growth Plan]].

Every claim, persona, channel, and post-purchase mechanism in this document operates inside Track 1's mortality reality. Where this doc references compounding or retention, it means *capturing more value within a transactional cohort* — never converting Track 1 into a recurring book.

## "800+ Deployments" Calibration

*"800+ delivered since 2015 across 150+ countries"* is **cumulative**, not currently-live. It is the right framing for top-of-funnel credibility — exec summary, ads, AI-search FAQ pages — where buyers expect a multi-year track record stated cumulatively.

For buyers who do diligence — especially Persona 2 (Burned Builder) and Persona 3 (Diaspora Digital Entrepreneur) — back the cumulative number with a curated **by-region reference list** of currently-live deployments and named past founders willing to take peer calls. Persona 2 will DM past buyers on LinkedIn; Persona 3 will explicitly ask to speak with a past buyer from their home region.

**Operational requirement:** maintain a quarterly-refreshed reference list segmented by region (NA, UK, EU, MENA, SE Asia, Africa, LATAM, India) and vertical, with named founders opted in to peer reference calls. The structural reason only ~30 of 800+ historical buyers are still operating businesses (96% 5-year cohort mortality, driven by buyer profile not service quality) is documented in [[Trioangle Growth Plan]] — that document is the strategic source for this guard.

## Customer Profile

### Firmographics

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Size** | 1–5 employees (solopreneur / co-founders) |
| **Revenue** | Pre-revenue or <$10k/mo |
| **Stage** | Pre-launch / Bootstrapped / Early Traction |
| **Geography** | Priority: North America, UK, EU, Middle East. Secondary: SE Asia, Africa, Latin America, India Tier-2/3 |
| **Business model** | Trioangle sells B2B (to founders); buyer's platform operates B2C/B2B2C commission-based marketplace |

### Technographics

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Current solution** | Free tools + freelancers + manual ops; or an AI-generated prototype (Cursor/Lovable/Replit) that works in demo but cannot be deployed to production or App Stores |
| **Tech maturity** | Laggard to Mainstream — understands the business of tech, not the code |
| **Adoption readiness** | High, but needs white-glove onboarding (install, server setup, App Store submission) |

### Demographics

- **Age:** 25–45
- **Income:** Priority markets: $50k–$150k/yr; Secondary markets: $15k–$60k/yr local equivalent
- **Education:** University-educated (business/management); self-taught in digital marketing

### Psychographics

| Persona | Self-perception |
|---|---|
| 1 | *"The Uber of my city"* |
| 2 | *"The founder who finally gets it done"* |
| 3 | *"Building something real back home"* |
| 4 | *"A business owner who stays ahead"* |
| 5 | *"The Gojek of my region"* |
| 6 | *"I built the demo in a weekend — I just need it to actually work"* |

**Values:** Independence, speed, financial freedom, building something of their own
**Risk tolerance:** Moderate — willing to invest **$500–$25k** but highly ROI-conscious

### Behavioral Traits

**Discovery channels:** Google Search, YouTube, Reddit, IndieHackers, WhatsApp/Telegram founder groups; increasingly AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) — asking "best Uber clone script company" and acting on the first credible result.

**Purchase behavior:** Research-heavy; 2–4 weeks (fast personas) to 30–60 days (Burned Builder, Super-App Builder). Converts fast once trust is established via live demo.

**Buying triggers:**
- Competitor launches in the buyer's city or vertical
- Price-gap discovery (agency $50k+ quote vs. clone-script price)
- Receiving a live demo link from sales
- AI tool (ChatGPT, Perplexity) recommending a marketplace app as a passive-income vehicle
- Passive-income content framing commission-based apps as "set and forget"
- Tried AI-assisted development (Cursor, Lovable, Replit) and hit the production wall — App Store rejection, real-time failure at scale, payment-gateway complexity

## Product-Tier Routing Logic

```
1 service only?
  → Single-Vertical Script ($500–$15k)

All required services within Gojek native scope?
  (taxi, delivery, grocery, car wash, laundry, beauty, handyman, DeliveryAll)
  → Gojek-like Super-App ($8k–$25k) — admin toggle, zero dev work

Any service outside Gojek native scope?
  (Airbnb/Rental, eCommerce, Classifieds, Social/Entertainment)
  → Developer Merge (script price + merge fee)

Fully proprietary / unique business logic?
  → [[Trioangle Service ICP]] (custom dev, $10k+) — scope on inbound only;
    Service ICP outbound is not yet activated as of 2026-Q2
    (see [[Trioangle 2.0 AI Pivot Plan]])

Merge quote exceeds $30k?
  → Same — engagement is effectively custom development
```

**Key qualifying question:** *"Which specific services do you need — and do all of them fall within taxi, delivery, handyman, car wash, laundry, beauty, or grocery?"*

## Qualifying Criteria

| Type | Criteria |
|---|---|
| ✅ Must-Have | Clear digital business model; defined market or geography; budget matched to tier |
| 👍 Nice-to-Have | Local supply secured (drivers, providers, listings); basic marketing plan |
| ❌ Disqualifying | Fully proprietary architecture → [[Trioangle Service ICP]]; super-app ambition with sub-$5k budget; dev agencies reselling without a specific live client project; "just exploring" without a concrete business idea; wants source code only to resell; **wants the absolute cheapest option regardless of outcome risk** — let them try a freelancer/AI route and re-engage as Persona 2 |

### Cheapest-Option Disqualification — Enforcement

The "absolute cheapest" disqualifier is a strategic choice, not a guideline. It survives quota pressure only when operationalized:

| Lever | Implementation |
|---|---|
| **Sales authority** | AEs explicitly empowered to walk away from price-only leads without manager escalation; **no commission penalty** for disqualifying at first call |
| **Identification at first call** | Signal: discount asked before demo, or comparison only on price with no question about support/deployment/track record. Tag in CRM as `disqualified_price_only` with date and observed signal |
| **Re-engagement tracking** | When a tagged lead returns within 12 months as Persona 2, capture prior burn cause (freelancer / agency / AI tool / cheap script). This is the validation loop |
| **Policy review trigger** | Quarterly review against 12-month return rate. Below ~15% → assumption is wrong, relax the policy. Above ~30% → re-engagement pipeline is real, reinforce. 15–30% → hold, re-measure. Calibrate thresholds against the first four quarters of data |

Without these mechanisms the rule erodes within one quarter under quota pressure.

## 5 Pain Points

> [!NOTE] Severity scoring is directional
> Severity (1–10) and frequency labels are **directional weights**, not measured scores. Basis: aggregated qualitative signal from sales-team call notes, post-purchase support-ticket categories, and recurring inbound objection patterns since 2015. Use to prioritize messaging emphasis and proof-asset investment — not to claim quantitative research. Recalibrate annually against fresh sales-call review.

| # | Pain Point | Severity | Stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| PP1 | Idea-to-Execution Technical Chasm | 9/10 | Pre-Purchase |
| PP2 | Trust Deficit from Past Failures | 8/10 | Pre-Purchase |
| PP3 | Post-Purchase Technical Helplessness | 7/10 | Post-Purchase |
| PP4 | Feature Customization Anxiety | 6/10 | Both |
| PP5 | Scaling Uncertainty | 7/10 | **Both** (pre-purchase concern; post-launch impact) |

**PP1 detail:** AI tools have become the first workaround. Buyers now pass through a two-stage pattern: (1) attempt with Cursor/Lovable, (2) hit the production wall (App Store rejection, GPS failure at scale, payment-gateway complexity), then arrive at Trioangle. This is the Persona 6 creation mechanism.

**PP2 detail:** The Burned Builder carries prior scars from vanishing freelancers, outdated scripts, or weeks of vibe coding that produced a demo but never a live app. Proof-first, never pitch-first.

**PP5 detail (upgraded severity):** Scaling failure hits on the exact moments that drive growth (weekend peaks, promotional launches, viral posts) — first impression collapses, churned users don't return, App Store rating drops below the 4.0 threshold priority-market buyers filter on. Workaround is over-provisioning (cash burn) or praying — neither is a plan. Emotional impact is chronic founder-conviction erosion.

## Referral Program (Trioangle Founder Referral)

Named program — not vague word-of-mouth. Top-3 channel for Burned Builder, Diaspora, and Small Business archetypes.

| Attribute | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Eligibility** | Live deployment + 30 days of operational use |
| **Referrer reward** | 3 months free support extension **OR** 10% cash commission (referrer picks at activation) |
| **Referee reward** | 5% discount on selected script/merge, stackable with promotions up to a 15% cap |
| **Attribution window** | 90 days — covers slowest documented decision cycle (Burned Builder / Small Business, 30–60 days) with margin |
| **Tracking** | Dedicated dashboard in buyer's admin panel (live status, lifetime earnings, pending payouts) |
| **Activation** | Introduced in Early Traction (Day 30–90); re-activated in Advocacy (Day 180+) with case-study request |
| **Ineligible** | Self-referrals, employees, agencies reselling Trioangle scripts (those route through the channel partner program — see [[Trioangle Channel Partner Personas]]) |

## Secondary Markets — Distinct Playbook

| Attribute | Priority Markets | Secondary Markets |
|---|---|---|
| **Dominant channel** | Google Search BOFU, YouTube, LinkedIn | WhatsApp/Telegram, Facebook diaspora groups, local meetups, referral |
| **Paid vs. organic** | Paid-led, high CPC tolerance | Organic-led; paid burns budget |
| **Payment preference** | Card + wire | **Cash on delivery** for end-user flows; local gateways critical (Paystack, Flutterwave, Razorpay, PhonePe, MercadoPago, Xendit, GCash); split/milestone billing for the script purchase (Wise, local bank, USDT) |
| **Language** | English NA/UK/EU; Arabic for MENA | Bilingual + RTL; **local-language demo content** signals credibility before price |
| **Trust signals** | Clutch, G2, case studies, app store links | **Local founder testimonials in buyer's exact region**; WhatsApp over web form; price transparency; same-timezone response window |
| **Sales motion** | Form → demo → proposal | WhatsApp intro → demo → proposal (skip web form) |
| **Do NOT** | Over-index on WhatsApp (reads as unprofessional) | Push Clutch/G2 as primary proof (peer referral weighted 10× higher) |

**Strategic principle:** Secondary markets are where Trioangle's 150+ country deployment history is most defensible — no Western competitor has matching local payment-gateway production credentials or region-specific founder testimonials. Priority markets fund growth; secondary markets compound the moat.

## AI-Era Positioning Rule

> **Never position on "we build faster with AI." Position on certainty of outcome and risk reduction.**

Buyers in 2026 can vibe-code a prototype themselves. They are not buying speed — they are buying a **guaranteed live business**: App Store approval, production deployment, real-time dispatch under load, payment gateway in production, and a named team accountable when something breaks. Applies to all personas, all channels, and all content.

### Persona Re-Evaluation Triggers

Three personas are AI-era exposed and carry explicit re-evaluation triggers:

**Persona 1 (Visionary Velocity) — most AI-displacement-exposed.** Revisit TAM, pricing, and positioning when any fire:
1. Public release of one-click iOS submission by Lovable, Bolt, Cursor, or a mainstream peer with documented approval track record
2. Production-grade real-time multi-role dispatch (customer/provider/admin) shipped by a mainstream AI builder with documented SLA
3. Persona 6 inbound > Persona 1 inbound for two consecutive quarters
   - *Operational dependency:* requires inbound-by-persona tagging in CRM. If not currently tracked, stand up tagging before this trigger can be monitored.

**Persona 2 (Burned Builder) — composition shift trigger.** The archetype originally formed around freelancer/agency failures; an increasing share now carries an AI-tool burn instead. Revisit framing/objections/proof when:
- AI-tool failure displaces freelancer/agency failure as top reported burn cause among Persona 2 inbound for two consecutive quarters, OR
- The typical Persona 2 returns to market with a working AI-generated codebase rather than a vanished freelancer's incomplete code — at which point Persona 2 and Persona 6 effectively converge and may need to be merged or re-segmented around post-burn emotional state rather than burn cause
- *Operational dependency:* requires "prior burn cause" tag on Persona 2 inbound (freelancer / agency / AI tool / cheap script / multiple)

**Persona 6 (AI Builder Who Hit the Wall) — sunset trigger.** This persona exists *because* of the AI production gap; as the gap narrows, inbound declines and the persona will sunset. Plan the harvest while the gap is open. Retire-or-redefine when:
1. Symmetric inverse of Persona 1 trigger 1 — one-click iOS submission ships and works (kills Persona 6's reason to exist)
2. Symmetric inverse of Persona 1 trigger 2 — production-grade multi-role dispatch ships
3. Persona 6 inbound drops below 20% of total inbound for two consecutive quarters after sustained growth

## Positioning Statement

> *"For founders who need a live, revenue-generating marketplace business — not just code — Trioangle delivers launch-ready marketplace platforms: deployed, app-store-approved, and fully supported from day one, across a single on-demand vertical or a full multi-service super-app. **AI tools deliver a prototype; Trioangle delivers the live business** — App Store approved, in production, and backed by a named team when something breaks. A depth of deployment history across 150+ countries since 2015 that no AI tool, cheap script marketplace, or agency-tier custom build can match."*

**Phase note:** This is current-era Track 1 positioning. Per [[Trioangle 2.0 AI Pivot Plan]] Phase 2 (M7–10), the headline brand shifts to AI-services-led — *"Productized AI for marketplace, on-demand, and platform operators — built by the team that's shipped 800+ platforms in your vertical since 2015."* Track 1 keeps its own buyer-facing line; the company's primary brand surface (homepage, hero, ads to non-clone-script audiences) shifts to the Phase 2 framing. Until Phase 2 lands, the current statement is the canonical Track 1 frame.
