# Outcome-Based Pricing

A pricing model where clients pay for measurable results achieved rather than time spent, code shipped, or seats licensed. The dominant emerging model for software development services and AI-powered products in 2025-2026.

---

## Why Traditional Pricing Breaks in the AI Era

- **Hourly/time-based**: AI makes development 3-10x faster. Charging by the hour destroys margin when AI compresses delivery time.
- **Per-seat**: Irrelevant when one AI-augmented engineer replaces five traditional developers.
- **Flat-fee unlimited**: Economically inefficient when AI usage is variable and inference costs are real.

The fundamental issue: "no two commands create the same amount of work for an AI Agent." Traditional models were built for human labor, not agentic throughput.

---

## Three Dominant Models for Software Agencies (2026)

### 1. Outcome-Based
Charge for measurable results: features shipped, bugs resolved, conversion rates improved, support tickets closed.

- Example: Intercom's Fin AI charges $0.99 per resolved customer issue
- **Requires**: clear success metrics agreed upfront
- **Risk**: failed attempts go unmonetized

### 2. Action/Workflow-Based
Meter discrete work units. Credit abstractions work well across heterogeneous tasks.

- Example: Clay uses credits; N8N charges per workflow execution
- **Risk**: sticker shock when workloads spike without ceilings

### 3. Hybrid (Most Practical for Agencies)
Combine a predictable retainer with usage-based overages.

- Base fee: covers architecture, oversight, planning, project management
- Variable layer: AI compute costs, agent execution, additional deliverables
- **Advantage**: revenue stability for the agency, flexibility for client growth

---

## Choosing Your Model: Three Diagnostic Questions

1. **Value attribution**: Can your client link outputs to measurable business outcomes?
2. **Execution autonomy**: Does your team solve problems end-to-end without client intervention?
3. **Workload predictability**: How variable is your effort per engagement?

If (1) yes, (2) yes, (3) predictable → outcome-based is cleanest.
If workload is unpredictable → hybrid protects both sides.

---

## Positioning Value (Not Hours)

When AI compresses delivery, clients may expect lower prices. The correct frame: the value delivered is the same or higher; the time taken is irrelevant. Re-anchor on:
- Outcomes and impact, not labor
- Expertise and judgment, not execution
- Speed as a benefit delivered to client, not a discount trigger

30%+ of enterprise SaaS solutions incorporated outcome-based pricing by 2025.

---

> [!gap] Outcome-based pricing requires clear success metrics and strong client trust. Agencies without established client relationships may struggle to negotiate this model early. Hybrid is the safer entry point.
