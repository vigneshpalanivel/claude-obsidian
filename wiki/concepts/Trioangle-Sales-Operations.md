# Trioangle Sales Operations

CEO-level assessment of weak points, growth levers, active sales initiatives, and OKR KPIs. Source: internal planning documents.

---

## Weak Points

Six diagnosed problems at company level:

- **Sales team culture** — mindset and accountability gaps in the sales function
- **Product features not increasing** — product line is stagnant; no consistent feature roadmap
- **Not focused on growth rate** — team executing without a growth target orientation
- **Product quality issues** — quality problems affecting sales credibility and conversion
- **Profit target pressure** — being pushed to hit profit targets despite a tough financial situation
- **Leads growth issue** — insufficient volume or quality of leads entering the top of funnel

---

## Growth Levers

Operational and strategic levers being activated:

**Operational**
- Skill development across teams
- Minimum 10% growth target every quarter
- Marketing report tracking in Looker Studio
- Tracking department-wise growth rate
- PMS rating system
- Department growth targets for VPs and Managers

**Channels**
- Upwork bidding
- LinkedIn marketing and sales outreach
- Industry conferences attendance
- Growth marketing and growth hacking strategy

**Sales Strategy**
- Pitching industry-relevant products for upselling/cross-selling
- Industry-based team breakups
- Growth plan benchmarked against MNC case studies

**Content and Demand**
- Lead generation through content marketing (see [[Trioangle Content Strategy]])

## Ansoff Growth Framework

Trioangle's growth strategy maps to all four quadrants:

### Market Penetration (Existing Product × Existing Market)
Grow share in current markets with existing products:
- Increase clone-related keywords for SEO
- Increase referral leads
- Lead generation through content marketing for clone products

### Market Development (Existing Product × New Market)
Take existing products into new markets:
- Software category keywords
- Service industry keywords (Rental, Taxi, Delivery, Handyman, Entertainment)
- General service keywords (Web development, Mobile app development)
- Content marketing for industry verticals

### Product Development (New Product × Existing Market)
New products for existing customer base:
- Increasing product count (Instagram clone, WhatsApp clone, YouTube clone)

### Diversification (New Product × New Market)
Expanding into new markets with new offerings — not yet detailed.

## Sales Initiatives

Organized by function. These are active or planned process improvements.

### Demo and Product Quality

- Daily product demo checking across Web, Android, and iOS
- Demo device testing that mirrors client's actual device and OS version
- Product demo seed data checking (so demos don't show empty states)
- Daily product flow checking using a testing tool
- Weekly update from the Service team on product-related work
- Single Basecamp board for all product issues (easy sales visibility)
- Third-party API sheet for all products
- Full API documentation

### Sales Process and Pipeline

- New End-of-Day (EoD) reporting format for the sales team
- Team restructured into two stages: Leads-to-Opportunity and Opportunity-to-Sale
- Lead screening process defined and tracked in CRM
- Lead scoring system
- Auditing old opportunities in Salesforce
- Night-shift coverage plan

**New Opportunity Criteria** (what qualifies a lead as a real opportunity):
1. Needs and pain points confirmed
2. Buying intent demonstrated
3. Budget confirmed
4. Demo completed
5. Responsiveness (they reply)
6. Referral lead flag

### CRM and Daily Workflow

Sales team hourly daily task structure:
- CRM updates
- Pipeline review
- Sales training
- Product knowledge
- Follow-ups
- Check-in with existing clients
- Deployment status tracking
- Proposal follow-ups with BA
- Calendly review

Internal task assignment through the "Triage Sales" JIRA board, tracked in EoD reports.

### Client Materials and Proposals

- Personalized portfolio sharing per prospect
- Load testing report (Performance, Scalability)
- Security testing report
- Server architecture diagram
- ROI document
- Deliverables list with package options
- All sales materials tracked via DocSend (including workflow videos)
- Server cost and third-party running cost breakdowns shared with prospects
- XD designs for products without existing design files
- FAQ document
- USP list

### Pricing and Deal Size

- Default price entry point: $999 (for no-response-on-price situations)
- Makent floor: $999 with mobile app mandatory (to avoid very low-cost deals)
- Add-on pitching to increase average sale value
- Monthly target: at least one big sale around $5K
- "Company leads" approach: background checks on larger prospects with dedicated hiring support
- Clarity used to track client demo engagement

### Customer Success and Retention

- Customer case studies published in blogs
- Customer success stories on Trioangle main page
- Support SLAs shared during sales (builds trust)
- Check-ins with existing customers via JIRA checklist
- Customer onboarding process diagram
- Reviews collected from clients through the JIRA check-in checklist
- Relationship building: discuss their market, competitors, suggest features, care for their business problems
- New support policy
- New refund policy

### Communication and Calls

- Call summaries sent to client and logged in Basecamp or Salesforce
- Call count increasing (including demo calls)
- Support moved to WhatsApp to reduce support workload in sales
- Google Meet AI for language barrier handling

### Sales Enablement and Training

- Daily LMS training (PPT format)
- Competitor analysis: SWOT per competitor
- Competitor product demo analysis tracked in Excel
- Shared competitor list (used by both sales and marketing)
- Daily lead discussion with team
- Sales team performance review meetings
- 1:1 meetings between sales manager and individuals: discussion order is Results → Pipeline → Activity

### Compensation and Agreements

- Vismithan compensation plan (named individual)
- Sales team compensation plan (incentive structure)
- Pre-sale compensation for quotes and reviews
- Sales agreement for all customers: NDA + Agreement + Deliverables documented in email and invoice

### Marketing and Website

- Separate portfolio page on trioangle.com
- GoferJek landing page redesign (benchmark: V3cube)
- VPN users blocked on trioangle.com
- Portfolio review and updates quarterly

## Synthesis: What These Documents Reveal

The weak points and initiatives together show a company making a transition from reactive sales to a **structured, process-driven sales operation**:

- **The core problem** is a culture and process gap — sales is not operating with enough discipline around CRM hygiene, follow-up cadence, and demo quality.
- **The product problem** (quality, stagnant features) is upstream of sales but is being surfaced as a sales bottleneck.
- **The growth framework** (Ansoff) is sound: all four quadrants are addressed, with Market Penetration (SEO + referrals) and Market Development (industry keywords) as near-term levers.
- **The initiatives list** skews heavily toward process and tooling — which is correct when the primary weakness is execution discipline, not strategy.
- **The KPIs** focus on conversion ratios and engagement rates — the right metrics for a mid-funnel bottleneck problem.
