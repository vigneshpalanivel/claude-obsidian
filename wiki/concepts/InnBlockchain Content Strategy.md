# InnBlockchain Content Strategy

The umbrella content program for InnBlockchain — Trioangle's sister/group blockchain dev + smart contract security firm. Three parallel content strategies, each mapped to a distinct ICP. The Channel Partner ICP has no dedicated content program; partner content sits inside the other three.

## The Three Strategies

| Strategy | ICP | Audience | Primary Hook |
|----------|-----|----------|--------------|
| [[InnBlockchain Crypto Native Content Strategy]] | [[InnBlockchain Crypto Native ICP]] | Protocol founders, asset owners, Web3 CTOs | "We've seen this exploit. We understand MiCA." |
| [[InnBlockchain FinTech Content Strategy]] | [[InnBlockchain FinTech ICP]] | Regulated FinTech CTOs/CPOs (WealthTech primary Q2) | "Compliance-first blockchain for licensed businesses" |
| [[InnBlockchain Crypto Products Content Strategy]] | [[InnBlockchain Crypto Products ICP]] | Crypto-native founders buying clone scripts $5k-$25k | "Verifiable on-chain proof, not testimonials" |

## Cross-Cutting Rules

> [!key-insight] Company origin is the routing gate, not licensing
> A licensed crypto-native DEX stays in Crypto Native. A licensed incumbent adding crypto routes to FinTech. Every strategy embeds this routing clause in overlapping verticals (DEX, Wallets, Lending, RWA). First discovery question: *"Is your company a crypto-native startup, or an existing regulated financial business adding a crypto product?"*

- **EU-only until 5 projects close**. All three strategies share the same geography gate. MiCA is the dominant compliance framing across Crypto Native + FinTech.
- **RWA is the cross-ICP battleground**. Both Crypto Native (V1) and FinTech (Priority 1) target RWA. Disambiguation is explicit: Crypto Native = platform builders + smart contract architecture; FinTech = licensed institutions + MiFID II instrument classification.
- **Two motions never mix in one outreach**: Security Audit vs. Development/Engineering. Different buyers, cycles, ACVs.
- **AI-code audit overlay** activates immediately on any active segment deal — no gate, no segment dependency.

## Shared SEO Differentiation Protocol

Every topic that exists in multiple strategies carries an inline disambiguation note on both sides. Overlapping topics and their rules:

| Topic | Crypto Native angle | FinTech angle |
|-------|---------------------|----------------|
| RWA / Tokenization | Smart contract architecture, oracle design, ERC-3643, MiCA for platform builders | Liquidity, T+0 settlement, MiFID II, institutional investors |
| Upgrade patterns | Exploit vectors, storage collisions | Governance, auditability, regulatory liability |
| Audit checklists | Exploit-pattern testing | Compliance auditability, regulator sign-off |
| Travel rule | DeFi protocol smart contract implementation | Licensed CASP on-chain AML tooling |
| Account abstraction / passkeys / embedded wallets | Primitives for self-custody wallet startups | Friction reduction for licensed wallet operators |
| Hiring | Reentrancy auditing, ZK circuits, Solidity/Rust depth | Compliance-aware engineers, audit trails, KYC/AML integration |

Crypto Products strategy does not carry these disambiguation rules — it's a product-line content play targeted at crypto-native buyers only.

## Distribution Split

- **Crypto Native**: Crypto Twitter/X, ETHGlobal, Telegram dev groups, LinkedIn EU, Reddit r/defi/r/ethdev, Rekt News + DeFi Llama reactive (48h exploit trigger)
- **FinTech**: LinkedIn ABM (dominant), partner/referral, Money20/20 Europe + Sibos Europe (events), segment-specific follow-up assets by persona
- **Crypto Products**: AI Search (ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini) + Google SEO + Crypto Twitter + Telegram (Persona 1/2) + Facebook diaspora groups + WhatsApp communities (Persona 2 peer-driven) + Reddit (Persona 3/5 technical) + YouTube demos

## Open Questions (Cross-Strategy)

- No shared keyword/topic ledger between Crypto Native and FinTech. Disambiguation is prose per-article, not a central registry. First publishing conflict is the risk surface.
- Compliance Carol (FinTech CCO) is a veto-holder addressed via content but is not a buyer — attribution methodology for compliance content undefined.
- Crypto Native Phase 1 locks ~4-5 months of content on RWA before any DEX/DeFi/Wallet/NFT/Token Launch content ships. Opportunity cost vs. competitor velocity not explicitly reconciled.
- Channel Partner ICP has no dedicated content strategy — partner recruitment messaging lives inside the other three programs without explicit anchoring.
