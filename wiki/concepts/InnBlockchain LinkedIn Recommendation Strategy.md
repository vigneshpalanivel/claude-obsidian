# InnBlockchain LinkedIn Recommendation Strategy

LinkedIn recommendations are the social-proof layer that converts a profile view into a discovery call. For InnBlockchain's ICP, recommendations from named clients and peers are how Security Sasha and Compliance Carol validate Vignesh during diligence — without them, the profile reads as marketing copy regardless of how sharp the About is.

## Volume Targets

| Threshold | Count | When |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-Publish minimum | 2-3 | Before profile goes live ([[InnBlockchain Vignesh LinkedIn Profile]] Pre-Publish Hard Gate) |
| Outreach minimum | 5 | Before DM sequences begin ([[InnBlockchain LinkedIn Strategy]] published minimum) |
| Sasha-grade diligence pass | 7-8 | Recommended target before scaling outreach ([[LinkedIn-Outreach-Strategy-InnBlockchain]] Outreach Readiness Gate) |

## Five Recommender Categories Mapped to ICP Validation

| Category | Validates Which Persona | Realistic Target | Sasha-Grade Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prior FinTech client | Strategic Sam + Technical Tom + Compliance Carol | 1 | 2 |
| Prior Crypto-Native client | Founding Felix + Security Sasha | 1 | 2 |
| Security peer / auditor at recognised firm | Security Sasha specifically | 1 | 1 |
| Regulator-adjacent / Compliance lawyer | Compliance Carol specifically | 0-1 (often a website testimonial instead) | 1 |
| Engineering hire / Senior engineer | Technical Tom (confirms Vignesh is a real technical CEO) | 1 | 1-2 |

## Operating Principles

1. **Reciprocity drives 60-70% of exchanges.** Recommendations Vignesh writes are more valuable than recommendations he requests — lead with offering wherever the relationship justifies it.
2. **Send via LinkedIn DM, not email.** ~3x higher response rate — recommender can write the recommendation in the same window without context-switching.
3. **2-3 requests per week, spread over 4-6 weeks.** Staggered timestamps look earned; 8 recommendations landing in 48 hours reads as bought or organised.
4. **Talking-point bullets are critical.** Without them, recommendations come back generic ("great to work with"); with them, recommendations land specific and ICP-aligned.
5. **"No pressure either way" close materially increases response rate.** Gives the recommender a graceful "no" path, which paradoxically makes more people say yes.

## Hard Filters — Who NOT to Ask

- Family, friends, or anyone with the same surname (visible in 5 seconds to evaluators)
- Anyone whose own LinkedIn looks unprofessional (their credibility transfers to Vignesh)
- Anyone who hasn't done actual paid work with Vignesh
- People not spoken to in 12+ months (cold ask, <10% response rate, damages relationship)
- Anyone bound by NDA on the only relevant work (they decline or write something vague)

## Operational Schedule (6-week sequence)

| Week | Action |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Send 2 Prior FinTech client + 1 Engineering hire requests |
| Week 2 | Send 2 Prior Crypto-native client requests |
| Week 3 | Send 1 Security peer + 1 Regulator-adjacent requests |
| Week 4 | Follow up on non-responses (one polite nudge max); write 2-3 reciprocity recommendations for those who said yes |
| Week 5-6 | Receive and review recommendations; request edits if any are off-brand or generic; backfill any category at <target |

## Cross-Channel Dependency

Do not begin LinkedIn outbound sequences until at least 5 recommendations published — see Outreach Readiness Gate in [[LinkedIn-Outreach-Strategy-InnBlockchain]] for the full pre-outreach checklist.

## Language / Translation

EU clients may write recommendations in their native language (German, French, Spanish, Portuguese). LinkedIn shows recommendations untranslated.

- **For the EU-Continental ICP** (current geography until 5-project gate): native-language recommendations are a positive signal — proximity, real EU work
- **Post-5-project-gate** (broader audience): ask key recommenders to write in English for evaluator readability

## Handling Bad Recommendations

Recommendations are visible to Vignesh before publishing — LinkedIn lets you decline. The polite move when a recommendation comes back generic or off-brand:

1. Thank them
2. Request specific edits ("would you be willing to add the part about the audit timeline?")
3. Publish revised version only

Most recommenders appreciate the prompt; the 10-20% who don't were never going to give a Sasha-grade endorsement anyway.

## Realistic Caveats

- **Security peer category** — named-firm employees (Trail of Bits, OZ, Zellic, Halborn alumni) carry the most weight but are the hardest to get. Most firms have policies against endorsing competitors. Realistic sources: independent auditors, ex-employees now consulting, researchers who've published with or alongside Vignesh.
- **Regulator-adjacent category** — hardest to land. Compliance professionals have institutional and personal-liability reasons to avoid public endorsements. Realistic fallback: written reference letter or testimonial quote for the InnBlockchain website (doesn't carry LinkedIn association but still gives a name and firm to cite in pitches).
- **4-6 week timeline is the floor, not the median.** If existing relationships are thin or not warm, expect 8-10 weeks. Build the recommendation pipeline in parallel with the Featured PDFs and InnBlockchain CEO Experience entry — don't sequence them.
