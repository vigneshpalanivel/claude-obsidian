# Trioangle CITE Audit & Improvement Plan

A 2026-06-26 domain-authority audit of **trioangle.com** using the **CITE framework**, plus a companion improvement playbook to reach an Excellent score. Baseline: **62/100 (Medium)**. Target: **90+ (Excellent)**.

> [!note] The playbook is now a consolidated single source of truth
> As of 2026-07-10, [[CITE-Score-Improvement-Playbook-Trioangle|CITE Playbook]] (`.raw/.../SEO/CITE/CITE Playbook.md`) absorbs the earlier execution playbook, the referral/opportunity landscape, the projected-outcomes report, and the domain/page implementation guide. The separate "Master Playbook" and "Implementation" docs were folded in and deleted. This concept page summarises it; the raw file is canonical.

> [!key-insight] The score is a byproduct, not the goal
> The playbook's operating principle: **earn the score, don't game it.** 90+ is reached by attaining the real outcomes underneath — AI citations, press, authoritative backlinks, organic visibility — so the number and the business impact rise together. Slow is acceptable; hollow is not. This is why the plan is sequenced by **real-world impact**, not by cheapest-points-first.

## The CITE Framework

CITE scores 40 items (10 per dimension) as **Pass (10) / Partial (5) / Fail (0)**, weighted into a 0–100 score. Weights are tuned for a **Product & Service** domain:

| Dimension | Meaning | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **C — Citation** | Strength, quality, diversity of the external link profile + AI citations | 25% |
| **I — Identity** | How clearly/verifiably the entity is established across the web | 30% |
| **T — Trust** | Credibility, security, editorial-integrity signals | 25% |
| **E — Eminence** | Organic visibility, share of voice, niche authority | 20% |

> [!contradiction] Two different "CITE" definitions in the wiki
> [[SEO-Recovery-Strategy-2025-26]] defines "CITE Score" as a **4-component off-page SEO framework** (Editorial Links, Anchor-Text Mix, Knowledge Graph, Review Platforms) with **target 80**. This audit uses **CITE = Citation / Identity / Trust / Eminence**, a 40-item weighted domain-authority audit with **target 90+**. Same name, different model and target. Needs reconciliation — decide which "CITE" is canonical for Trioangle before reporting progress against either.

## Audit Result — 62/100 (Medium)

**Verdict: CAUTIOUS.** No hard veto (HTTPS enforced, no penalties, no review manipulation). Score capped by trust/citation gaps, not by red flags.

| Dimension | Raw | Pass items | Weighted |
|---|---|---|---|
| C — Citation | 50/100 | 1/10 | 12.5 |
| I — Identity | 70/100 | 4/10 | 21.0 |
| T — Trust | 75/100 | 5/10 | 18.75 |
| E — Eminence | 50/100 | 2/10 | 10.0 |
| **CITE** | | | **62/100** |

**Biggest drags (all Fail):** no media/press coverage (E04), no original research/data (E05), AI citation breadth near zero (C08). **Trust polish gaps:** outdated footer copyright ("2022" in 2026, T08), no cookie/GDPR banner (T04), no editorial policy (T05).

**Caveat baked into the audit:** several Citation items (C01/C04/C09/C10) and all AI-citation items (C05–C08) were scored "Partial pending tool data" — the audit had **no Ahrefs/Semrush or AI-citation-monitoring access**. Confirmed Pass on those requires tooling.

## What 90+ Requires (the honest math)

90+ needs **~9 of 10 items Pass in *every* dimension** → converting **~24 items** from Partial/Fail (12 of 40 Pass today: C 1/10, I 4/10, T 5/10, E 2/10). Closing the 37.75 weighted-point gap.

- **Controllable in weeks (on-site/declarative):** copyright, schema, social links, cookie banner, editorial policy, Crunchbase/Wikidata, bylines. ~8–12 points → **~70–74 (approaching Good)**.
- **Earned over 12–18 months (off-site authority):** referring-domain volume/quality, press, AI citation breadth, original-research reputation, rankings, share of voice. The remaining ~15 points. **No checkbox ships these.**

**90+ is a 12–18 month authority program, not a sprint** — and the 90+ milestone is the **18-month** end of the window, because **Citation (1→9 Pass) is the binding constraint** (C01/C04/C09 are slow link-profile signals that can't be forced). ~75 (Good) is a one-quarter target.

## AI Citations — the result that actually matters

Improving the CITE *number* and improving **AI citations** (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini) are *not* the same target. ~10 cheap on-site points (copyright, cookie banner, NAP) move AI citations by roughly **zero**. The levers that actually drive AI citations:

1. **Original research / proprietary data** (E05, C06) — highest leverage; the one quotable asset.
2. **Answer-engine content formatting** (C08, E06) — direct-answer-first + tables + FAQ schema = extractable.
3. **Entity / knowledge graph** (I01, I10) — Wikidata/Panel so engines know and disambiguate the entity.
4. **Organic retrievability** (E01) — can't be cited for a query you don't rank for.
5. **Third-party corroboration** (C02, C10, I06) — press + diverse authoritative links.

> [!note] Two clocks
> **Retrieval-time** citation (Perplexity, AI Overviews, ChatGPT search) ≈ SEO + extractability — movable in **weeks–months**. **Parametric** citation (the model "knows" you from training) requires broad web presence — **years**, a byproduct of authority, not directly targetable.

## Improvement Plan — sequenced AI-citations-first

Phases ordered by impact, not score efficiency. Expect the score to **lag early** — that's correct, not a problem.

| Phase | Window | Focus | Target score |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — AI-Citeable Content & Extractability** | 0–8 wks | Tooling first; flagship "On-Demand App Market Report" (E05); answer-engine restructuring; FAQ/Person schema | ~66–68 |
| **2 — Entity & Knowledge Graph** | 2–8 wks | Wikidata/Wikipedia; Organization schema + sameAs; Crunchbase/G2/LinkedIn; editorial policy + bylines | ~72–74 |
| **3 — Earned Off-Site Authority** | 1–6 mo | PR outreach; whitepapers; head-term rankings; verified-review drive (Clutch/G2 badges) | ~80 |
| **4 — Score Polish + Compounding** | parallel + 6–18 mo | Citation-neutral quick wins (copyright, cookie banner, social links, NAP); RD growth; confirmed AI-citation breadth; share of voice | **90+** |

The cheap, citation-neutral score points (copyright fix, cookie banner, social footer links, US NAP) are deliberately deferred to Phase 4 — they raise the number but buy zero citations. Phase 3 also folds in **referral-platform activation** (the opportunity landscape below).

## What the Consolidated Playbook Adds

Beyond the audit + phase plan, the SSOT carries four operational layers:

- **Opportunity Landscape** — named platforms with DA scores mapped to the specific CITE gap each closes: product/business listings (G2 92, Capterra 93, Crunchbase 91, Trustpilot 93 → C02/C10/I06/E03/E07), editorial/press (freeCodeCamp, Entrepreneur India, Hacker Noon, YourStory, Inc42 → C10/E04), the entity-corroboration hierarchy (**Wikidata → Wikipedia → Crunchbase → G2/Clutch**), research-amplification channels, and a vertical-by-product press map.
- **Projected Outcomes** — CITE progression, scenario planning (A +8.25→70 / B +11→81 / C +12.5→93), SEO/traffic, AI/GEO, conversion-rate levers, content-asset ROI. Figures are directional pending tool verification.
- **Product Page CITE Template** — a reusable per-product checklist (asset audit + 7 page-level fixes with `[product]`/`[primary keyword]` placeholders) so any product team applies CITE at the page level.
- **Benchmark examples** — HubSpot (research→citations), Appinventiv (800+ Clutch reviews→badge), Ahrefs (Wikidata/Wikipedia→first AI-cited source).

> [!contradiction] Entity facts disagree across source docs — resolve before I-dimension work
> Founding year (**2015** per audit vs 2016), HQ city (**Madurai** vs Chennai), portfolio size (**200+ clients / 100+ countries** vs 750+ projects). These feed Wikidata / NAP / `Organization` schema directly — publishing the wrong value sabotages the entity-consistency the Identity dimension is trying to build. The playbook carries the audit's figures as canonical with a "resolve first" flag.
