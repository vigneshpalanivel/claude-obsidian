# Trioangle Growth Plan: Recurring Revenue Architecture

## The Core Problem: Services Trap

Project revenue resets every month. You close a ₹5L project, deliver it, and then revenue drops back to zero. Growth feels impossible because there's no base — every month starts from scratch.

The fix is MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue). Once you have ₹5L MRR, you start every month already at ₹5L and layer new projects on top. That's what growth feels like.

**Revenue = Projects × Ticket** → resets monthly, no compounding  
**Revenue = MRR Base + New Projects** → compounds; growth becomes visible

---

## Why the AMC Pitch at $500 Fails

The typical "Annual Maintenance Contract" pitch fails because:

- **Fear-based framing** — "pay in case something breaks" — clients don't feel buying
- **No visible monthly deliverables** — what do they actually get each month?
- **Wrong positioning** — feels like insurance, not investment
- **Price anchoring problem** — $500/month sounds arbitrary without a deliverables list

The fix: reframe as value-based retainer tiers with specific monthly outputs. Clients buy deliverables, not availability.

## 4-Tier Retainer System

### Tier 1 — Care Plan ₹8,000–15,000/month
**Target:** Small clients, finished projects, low-touch  
**Deliverables:**
- Bug fixes and minor updates (up to 4 hours/month)
- Monthly uptime and performance report
- Hosting and SSL management
- Quarterly dependency/security audit

**Positioning:** "Your app keeps working. We handle everything behind the scenes."

---

### Tier 2 — Growth Plan ₹25,000–45,000/month
**Target:** Active businesses wanting to improve, not just maintain  
**Deliverables:**
- Everything in Care Plan
- 1 feature addition or UI improvement per month
- Monthly analytics review with 3 recommendations
- WhatsApp/email support response within 4 hours (business hours)
- Quarterly strategy call (1 hour)

**Positioning:** "We're your product team on retainer. Every month your app gets better."

---

### Tier 3 — AI Operations Plan ₹40,000–75,000/month
**Target:** Clients ready to use AI for actual business operations  
**Deliverables:**
- Everything in Growth Plan
- 1 AI workflow deployed per month (chatbot, automation, report generation, lead scoring)
- Prompt refinement and model updates
- AI usage report with ROI estimate (time/cost saved)
- Priority Slack/WhatsApp channel

**Positioning:** "We deploy AI into your business operations every month. You pay less than one employee, get AI-powered throughput."

---

### Tier 4 — Feature Sprint Subscription ₹60,000–1,00,000/month
**Target:** Product companies, SaaS, high-growth startups  
**Deliverables:**
- Dedicated sprint every month (1 full sprint cycle)
- Sprint planning + backlog grooming call
- 2 features or 1 major module per month
- Code review + deployment
- AI-integrated by default

**Positioning:** "Outsourced CTO + dev team. One monthly invoice. Predictable delivery."

## Hosting Margin Play

This is the fastest path to passive MRR and the easiest sell.

**How it works:**
1. Identify clients where you built and deployed their app
2. Offer to take over hosting management — handle servers, SSL, backups, monitoring
3. Bill client ₹8,000–12,000/month for "Managed Hosting + Operations"
4. Actual cost on AWS/GCP/Hetzner: ₹2,000–4,000/month
5. Net margin: ₹5,000–8,000 per client, zero ongoing work

**Why clients say yes:**
- They don't want to manage servers
- They're already paying someone or doing it badly
- "Handled by Trioangle" is a clean, trusted answer

**Revenue math at scale:**
| Clients | Margin/Client | Monthly MRR |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| 10 | ₹6,000 | ₹60,000 |
| 20 | ₹6,000 | ₹1,20,000 |
| 40 | ₹7,000 | ₹2,80,000 |

40 hosting clients = ₹2.8L/month with near-zero ongoing effort.

## Project-to-Retainer Conversion

The highest-leverage moment to sell a retainer is **the day the project goes live** — satisfaction is at its peak, the client trusts you, they're scared about what happens next.

**Go-live conversation script:**

> "The project is live — congratulations. Most clients now worry about: what if something breaks, who handles updates, how do we grow this. We have a retainer that covers all of that. It's ₹[X]/month and you can cancel anytime. Want me to set it up?"

Key principles:
- Offer it as the obvious next step, not a separate sales pitch
- Name a specific tier based on what you know about their needs
- "Cancel anytime" removes the fear
- Don't over-explain — short and confident closes better

## Re-Pitch for AMC Rejectors

For clients who already said no to your ₹500 AMC offer — reframe with deliverables:

**WhatsApp template:**

> "Hi [Name], quick update — we've restructured our support plans with specific monthly deliverables instead of the old AMC format.
>
> Our Care Plan at ₹10,000/month includes: bug fixes, monthly health report, hosting management, and security audits. Basically we handle everything so you never have to worry about the app.
>
> Want to try it for 3 months? If you don't find it valuable, cancel — no questions."

What changed vs. before:
- Specific deliverables (not vague "support")
- Lower anchoring (₹10K feels different from $500/month framing)
- Risk reversal ("cancel anytime")
- Not fear-based — value-based

## 30-Day Sprint to ₹5L MRR

### Week 1 — Hosting takeovers
- List all clients where you manage their deployment
- Send hosting transfer proposal to top 15
- Target: 10 hosting clients signed = ₹60,000–80,000 MRR

### Week 2 — Care Plan conversions
- Identify 10 clients with completed projects, low engagement
- Send personalized WhatsApp message (not mass blast)
- Target: 5 Care Plans at ₹10,000 = ₹50,000 MRR

### Week 3 — Growth Plan pitch to active clients
- Identify 5 clients with growing businesses who ask for changes regularly
- Call them — don't message; this tier needs a conversation
- Pitch Growth Plan: "instead of quoting each change, let's put you on a monthly plan"
- Target: 3 Growth Plans at ₹35,000 = ₹1,05,000 MRR

### Week 4 — AI Operations upsell
- From existing active clients, identify 2–3 who have expressed interest in AI or automation
- Demo one specific AI workflow that fits their business
- Target: 2 AI Operations Plans at ₹55,000 = ₹1,10,000 MRR

**End of Month 1 estimate:** ₹3,05,000–4,00,000 MRR from existing client base alone.

## Non-AI Business Foundations

These are the structural changes that make revenue predictable regardless of AI pivot progress:

### Billing Discipline
- Switch to milestone billing for all projects (30% upfront, 40% mid, 30% on delivery)
- Stop billing on delivery alone — you lose negotiating leverage at the end
- Add a late payment clause to all contracts (1.5% per month after 15 days)

### Contracts
- Use a signed agreement for every engagement — even small ones
- Include: scope boundaries, change request process, IP ownership, retainer terms
- Prevents scope creep and late payment disputes

### CRM / Pipeline Visibility
- Track every client and lead in one place (even a spreadsheet works)
- Know: last contact date, retainer status, next renewal date, upsell opportunity
- Without visibility you can't act on timing

### Equity / Profit Share Option
- For early-stage startup clients who can't afford rates: offer reduced fee + 1–2% equity
- Even 2–3 equity positions maturing in 3–5 years changes your personal outcome significantly
- Only do this for startups with real traction or funding

### Reputation Infrastructure
- Get 5 Clutch reviews from best clients — this is the #1 B2B credibility signal internationally
- One video testimonial > ten text reviews
- LinkedIn case study posts (problem → solution → result) build inbound pipeline over 6 months

### Referral Program
- Give existing clients 10% of first invoice for any referred client who signs
- Simple, transparent, no expiry
- Most referrals come from clients who trust you but never thought to mention you — the program gives them a reason to act
