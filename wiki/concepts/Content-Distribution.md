# Content Distribution

Content distribution is the process of publishing and amplifying content across channels to reach the intended audience. It is a distinct phase from content creation and requires its own planning.

## Channel Types

**Owned** — website/blog, email newsletter, social profiles, podcast feed. Full control, no cost-per-reach.

**Earned** — guest posts, PR coverage, organic shares, backlinks. Zero cost but unpredictable volume.

**Paid** — boosted posts, content ads, sponsored placements. Predictable reach, scalable, costs money.

## Distribution Priority by Channel (2025 data)

1. Website/blog — 90% of organizations use as primary channel
2. Email newsletter — 69%
3. Organic social — most popular promotion channel overall
4. Paid social — 51%
5. Organic search — 33%

(Source: [[Reliablesoft-Content-Marketing-Checklist]])

## Key Distribution Tasks

- **SEO publish checklist** — URL slug, meta title, meta description, canonical tag, internal links
- **Cross-posting** — adapt format and length for each secondary channel
- **Email send** — segment list if content is audience-specific
- **Social schedule** — platform-specific format (thread vs. carousel vs. clip)
- **Community sharing** — relevant Slack groups, Reddit threads, LinkedIn groups, niche forums
- **Paid amplification** — boost top performers; set geographic and demographic targeting
- **Redistribution cadence** — republish / reshare at 14 days, 30, 90, 120 days post-publish

## Distribution ≠ Repurposing

Distribution sends the *same* asset to more places. Repurposing *transforms* the asset into a new format. Both are required; they serve different goals.
