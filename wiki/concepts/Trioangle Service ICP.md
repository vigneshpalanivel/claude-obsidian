# Trioangle Service ICP

Five buyer personas for [[Trioangle]]'s custom development and AI engineering service line. **This is Track 2** in the two-track Growth Plan model — the recurring-revenue growth engine. [[Trioangle Buyer Personas]] is Track 1 (cash engine, Product ICP).

> [!IMPORTANT] Status — Track 2 Documented, Outbound Not Yet Activated
> Service ICP is documented but **not currently generating direct outbound revenue** as of 2026-Q2. Track 2 outbound activates once Phase 0/1 of [[Trioangle 2.0 AI Pivot Plan]] is stable. Until then: inbound conversion only, AI add-on flow runs through the Product ICP handover process. See [[Trioangle Growth Plan]] for activation sequencing.

> [!NOTE] Operating Layer
> This page is the **strategy layer** — who/why/what-they-buy. The **operating layer** (what reps do day-to-day: pipeline stages, scripts, scoring, pricing floors, discount matrix, KPI ranges, handoff checklist) lives in [[Trioangle Service Sales Playbook]]. The two are deliberately separated so they don't drift into duplicate sources of truth.

> [!IMPORTANT] Two Non-Negotiable Positioning Rules
> **AI-Era Positioning Rule** — Never position on "we build faster with AI." Position on domain expertise, certainty of delivery, and risk reduction. Service buyers in 2026 have already seen too many AI demos that delivered nothing. They are buying a named team with 800+ domain-specific engagements who already know their vertical's edge cases — and can ship without a learning curve.
>
> **Trust & IP Positioning Rule** — Never defend trust with promises ("we're transparent," "we communicate well," "we're senior"). Defend it with verifiable structural guarantees: Day-1 GitHub access, IP transfer clause, 48-hour silence → 5% contract credit, pre-contract engineer introduction, Discovery Sprint escape hatch. Every Decision-stage CTA references at least one structural mechanism, never an adjective.

## Active Marketing Focus — Narrower than Historical Delivery

Trioangle has delivered across **9 verticals historically** (the credibility moat backing the 800+ stat). **Active SEO and outbound marketing focus narrows to 8 verticals** for sharper conversion and defensible AI positioning.

**Active marketing verticals (8):**
Ride-hailing · On-demand (umbrella; broad on-demand search funnels into ride-hailing / delivery / home-services) · Delivery · Marketplace · Home-services · **Rentals** (new standalone — vacation + car + boat + equipment + gear + fashion + furniture P2P) · Super-app · Ecommerce.

**Dropped from active marketing** (historical delivery preserved as credibility): Travel/hotel/OTA-only · Social-media · Video-streaming/OTT · EdTech · Dating · Short-video · B2B Fleet Management · Real-estate/proptech.

**Vertical AI footprint:** AI engineering has shipped production cases in 5 verticals (ride-hailing, logistics & fleet, marketplace/commerce, home services, delivery). When a buyer asks about an out-of-marketing vertical, the credibility claim stands (we've shipped it historically) but lead with vertical-delivery framing, not AI claims — those don't have production cases.

## Existing-Operator Filter (Track 2 Qualifier)

The filter that makes Track 2's recurring-revenue thesis work — without it, Service ICP recreates the Product ICP mortality risk:

✅ Required:
- Platform operational for **12+ months** OR established offline business with revenue traction
- Revenue traction (not pre-revenue) — **funding alone does not qualify**
- $1M+ ARR target; established operating business with proven revenue
- For Persona 5 specifically: existing platform with active users — no greenfield/pre-launch

❌ Disqualified:
- Pre-launch / pre-revenue operators → route to [[Trioangle Buyer Personas]] (Product ICP) or decline
- Strong in-house engineering teams handling changes internally (no recurring demand)
- "Just exploring AI" without a specific business problem (P5)
- Equity-only or deferred-payment requests
- Solo non-technical founders with <$10k

## Discovery Sprint vs AI Audit Sprint — Two Pre-Sales Products

Both are paid 2-week engagements, credited toward the full engagement. Pricing tiers live in [[Trioangle Service Sales Playbook]] §8. The difference is scope.

| Product | For | Deliverable Set |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Audit Sprint** 🥇 | **P5** (default entry) · also offerable to **P4** (relationship-demonstration) and **P3 in Phase 2** (after platform exists, ~6–12 months post-launch) | Data audit · AI feature shortlist (ranked) · reference architecture (service isolation, fallback, rollout) · build-vs-buy-vs-SaaS recommendation per feature · fixed-price quote for top feature · AI engineer team intro |
| **Discovery Sprint** 🥈 | **P1** and **P2** arriving with a rebuild brief · **P3 in Phase 1** (net-new digital platform build) | ERD + data model · system architecture + tech stack · wireframes (P2) · critical path map · fixed-price delivery estimate · team intro |

**Default rule:** Lead with AI Audit Sprint unless prospect arrives with a clear non-AI rebuild brief. When Discovery Sprint is signed (P1/P2 rebuild path), **always name AI Audit Sprint as the planned follow-on** so the deal trajectory is rebuild → AI features, not rebuild → done.

### AI Funnel Disambiguation

Two AI-related entry offerings sit at different funnel stages:

| Product | Stage | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AI Readiness Call** | Top of funnel · qualification | 1-hour live call (free) | Identify 2–3 highest-ROI AI use cases; qualify; surface whether a paid AI Audit Sprint is the next step |
| **AI Audit Sprint** | Pre-engagement · formal entry | 2-week paid engagement | Full data audit, feature shortlist, reference architecture, build-vs-buy recommendations, fixed-price quote |

**Standard funnel:** Free AI Readiness Call → (if qualified) AI Audit Sprint → (if proceed) signed AI feature build with sprint fee credited.

## Persona 1 — The "Scaling CTO" (*Tara*) 🎯 *Direct secondary*

**Who**: Chief Technology Officer, 32–48. Company is an **established platform business with active users** — Series B+ scale or profitable mid-market operator (12+ months operational, real revenue traction). Often ex-FAANG or serial entrepreneur. Evaluates vendors like senior engineering hires: technical interviews, code reviews, architecture whiteboards. Deeply skeptical of sales pitches.

**AI angle (primary pitch)**: AI roadmap pressure from board and competitors, but they frame the immediate problem as architecture/scaling. Entry conversation starts with platform pain; AI surfaces in the second meeting. Sign AI Audit Sprint if AI scope engages; Discovery Sprint with explicit AI Audit follow-on otherwise.

**Buying trigger**: Platform outage at high traffic, failed acquisition tech DD, new country expansion, compliance audit failure, key engineering lead departure, vertical competitor ships AI feature.

**Do say**: *"Dedicated senior engineers, CI/CD pipelines, automated test coverage, full GitHub access from Day 1."*
**Don't say**: *"We have a pre-made template that almost does this."*

**JTBD**: *"I'm not buying a development engagement. I'm buying the institutional knowledge of 800+ platforms delivered across my domain over the last decade — and the engineering team that can ship AI features into the architecture they already understand."*

## Persona 2 — The "Non-Technical Founder-Operator" (*Felix*) 🎯 *Direct secondary*

**Who**: CEO / Founder / Managing Director, 32–48. Company is an **established operating business** (12+ months revenue traction, profitable or near-profitable) where the original technical co-founder has left or never existed — typically a founder-led marketplace, services business, or vertical platform that grew on operational excellence rather than technical sophistication.

**AI angle (primary pitch)**: Sees competitors shipping AI and feels behind, but lacks technical confidence to evaluate AI vendors. Wants AI explained in business outcomes ("would lift conversion X%; reduce support load Y%") with honest build-vs-hype calls.

**Do say**: *"We act as your technical partner — translating business goals into engineering execution and giving you board-ready progress updates without disrupting your running operation."*
**Don't say**: *"We need detailed technical requirements before we can quote."*

**JTBD**: *"I run a real business with real customers. I need someone to rebuild what we've already proven works — and explain every decision to me in plain English."*

## Persona 3 — The "Digital Transformation Enterprise Buyer" (*Diana*) 🤝 *Channel only — Dev-first*

**Who**: Head of Digital / VP of Product / CIO, 38–55, at an established offline business (50–500 employees) in an industry that overlaps Trioangle's 9 verticals — actively marketed (on-demand home services, commerce/marketplace) plus channel-inbound from historical verticals (hospitality, logistics & fleet). Disrupted by digital natives. Internal IT but no product development capability. Burned by multiple failed "digital transformation" initiatives from Big-4 consultancies.

**Service-line angle — DEV-FIRST, AI in Phase 2**: P3 has no digital product yet — the brief is *build the platform*, not *embed AI into existing*. Phase 1 = digital transformation (platform build with vertical-specific architecture); Phase 2 = AI features layered on once the platform has been operational long enough (typically 6–12 months) to generate the data AI needs. Entry product: Discovery Sprint. AI Audit Sprint is Phase 2 entry, not the first.

**Access route**: NOT reachable via Trioangle's direct outbound. Requires a management consulting firm to introduce as the delivery partner. See [[Trioangle Channel Partner Personas]] Persona 2 (Consulting Upseller) and Persona 3 (Vertical Specialist).

**Repeat-Buy Cadence**: 1–2 large projects per year typical. **Typical 3-phase sequence: Phase 1 platform build (Year 1) → Phase 2 AI feature initiative once platform is live and data has accumulated (Year 2) → Phase 3 compliance/scaling/new geography (Year 2–3).**

**Do say**: *"We combine the speed of a startup engineering team with the enterprise-grade delivery standards your compliance team requires: SLAs, documentation, security certifications."*
**Don't say**: *"We're a scrappy startup team that moves fast."*

**JTBD**: *"I've sat through the Accenture pitch decks. I need someone who will actually build the thing."*

## Persona 4 — The "MENA Relationship Buyer" (*Rashid*) 🤝 *Channel only*

**Who**: CEO / Managing Director / Head of Innovation, 35–55, at a UAE/Saudi family-group conglomerate, government-linked enterprise, or Gulf-region established operating business with revenue traction (12+ months operational). Holds final decision authority personally. Relationship-first culture — business is built on trust and reputation networks, not RFPs.

**AI angle (primary pitch)**: Vision 2030 calls out AI as a national priority — P4 sees AI as both strategic mandate and prestige signal. AI Audit Sprint surfaces in relationship-building (Month 1–3), often *before* formal scope is defined. Channel-partner introduction required.

**Access route**: NOT reachable via cold outbound. Warm introduction from a MENA-based consulting firm or agency partner outweighs all portfolio evidence. UAE/KSA-based [[Trioangle Channel Partner Personas]] are the primary access channel.

**Primary comms channel**: WhatsApp (not email, not forms).

**Repeat-Buy Cadence**: 2–3 projects per year typical, often through the same family-group across multiple sister businesses. The relationship is the unit of cadence — once trust is built, the next project is assumed unless something breaks it.

**Do say**: *"We have delivered platforms for clients in [UAE/KSA/Qatar] and can share references in your network. We assign a dedicated point-of-contact reachable on WhatsApp."*
**Don't say**: *"Fill out our discovery form and we'll get back to you within 2 business days."*

**JTBD**: *"I don't buy software. I enter partnerships. Show me you understand my business and my market — then we can talk numbers."*

## Persona 5 — The "AI-Forward Platform Operator" (*Aisha*) 🎯 *Direct primary*

**Who**: CTO / CPO / Head of Product, 30–46. **Company**: Established profitable mid-size platform business or Series B+ scale operator in one of Trioangle's 8 active marketing verticals — ride-hailing, on-demand, delivery, marketplace, home-services, rentals (vacation/car/equipment P2P), super-app, ecommerce (working platform with active users).

**Context**: Runs an already-functioning platform — not a broken MVP, not a greenfield build. The platform works. But they're watching competitors use dynamic pricing, AI-assisted matching, demand forecasting, or conversational booking, and they know they're falling behind. Internal engineers are heads-down maintaining the existing product and have no ML/AI experience.

**This is the recurring-revenue base for Track 2.** Per [[Trioangle Growth Plan]], P5 generates the most reliable repeat-project cadence because the AI roadmap delivered in the AI Audit Sprint *is* the project queue.

**Repeat-Buy Cadence**: 2–4 *total* projects/year per active client. Mix is roughly 1–3 AI feature builds + 1–2 non-AI projects (platform extension, geo/vertical expansion, performance, compliance).

**Account Cadence**: Quarterly AI roadmap review — *"what's working, what's next, what's blocking"* — no pitch deck. Single account contact across AI and non-AI work.

**Decision speed**: Fast — 2–4 weeks for self-approved AI feature builds under $25k; 4–6 weeks for larger scope requiring CEO/CFO visibility. Engineering lead is the technical gate, not procurement.

**Do say**: *"We've already built AI-powered dynamic pricing / intelligent dispatch / demand forecasting into platforms in your exact vertical. We plug into your existing codebase — we're not rebuilding anything, we're adding the intelligence layer on top."*
**Don't say**: *"We can train a custom LLM for your use case."* (Overengineered and irrelevant for most platform AI needs.)

**JTBD**: *"I don't need someone to explain what AI is. I need someone who already knows what AI features actually move the needle in a marketplace business — and can ship them without breaking my existing platform."*

> [!IMPORTANT] Commercial Model — Direct AI Buyer for the AI Pivot Plan
> Persona 5 is the **direct enterprise AI buyer** for [[Trioangle 2.0 AI Pivot Plan]]. AI deals here are sold as **one-time, outcome-based engagements** ($15k–$120k+) — not retainers, not metric-based recurring, not subscriptions. Repeat business is tracked separately in [[Trioangle Growth Plan]].

## Sales Motion by Persona

### Channel 1 — LinkedIn ABM (P1 + P2 direct secondary)

**Trigger**: Established platform operators showing scaling signals (job postings, expansion, outages, key-engineer departures). Targeted outbound + inbound conversion. **Not** cold outbound at scale — qualified by trigger.

3-step soft sequence: connection (no pitch) → value-first message (architecture insight) → soft ask (20-min peer call).

### Channel 2 — Clutch.co / G2 (All direct personas)

Inbound channel. 50+ verified reviews tagged across all 9 verticals filters in zero-trust-building inbound leads.

### Channel 3 — Established Operator Networks (P4 + P5 + channel-routed P3)

Industry associations, vertical operator forums, MENA accelerators (Hub71/DIFC/Flat6Labs/STV), late-stage VC growth-fund portfolio referrals — **not** Seed/Series A.

### Channel 4 — AI Engineering Outbound (P5)

**Trigger**: Competitor announces AI feature · ML/AI Engineer job posting · investor deck mentions AI roadmap · public underperformance signal (dispatch latency, fraud false positives).

3-step engineering-language sequence:
1. **Day 0** — Connection referencing competitor's AI move; share Trioangle's analogous vertical case
2. **Day 4** — Vertical-specific case proof with named metric
3. **Day 10** — Soft ask: free 1-hour AI Readiness Call

**Banned language**: "AI transformation," "strategy session," "we can train a custom LLM for you," "AI roadmap discussion."
