# AI Services Business Model

## The Old Model (Breaking Down)

**Time-and-materials (T&M) / headcount billing:**
- Revenue = headcount × billable rate × hours
- Scale by adding people
- Junior developers generate most revenue volume
- Client pays for inputs (hours worked), not outputs (results delivered)
- AI automates the junior work → revenue collapses without model change

---

## The New Model (AI Era)

### 1. Outcome-Based / Fixed-Fee
- Client pays for a defined outcome (migration done, system live, cost reduced by X%)
- Provider absorbs risk of delivery method
- AI automation increases margin for the provider if delivery uses fewer human hours
- "Price your expertise, not your time"

### 2. Platform + Services (Hybrid)
- Sell access to a proprietary AI platform (recurring SaaS-like revenue)
- Layer delivery/implementation services on top
- Example: Infosys Topaz Fabric subscription + Topaz-powered consulting
- Example: HCLTech AI Force platform + managed AI services

### 3. Retainer / Managed AI Services
- Client pays monthly retainer for ongoing AI operations
- Provider manages AI stack: monitoring, retraining, agent updates, evals
- Recurring revenue, sticky because switching costs are high
- Most scalable model for smaller firms without enterprise sales force

### 4. AI Transformation Consulting
- Time-limited, premium-priced advisory engagements
- Map AI opportunities, design architecture, build roadmap
- Often leads to platform or retainer relationship
- Accenture-style: "reinvention" consulting → AI implementation → managed services

---

## Revenue Model Comparison

| Model | Pricing | Margin Profile | Scalability | Risk |
|-------|---------|---------------|-------------|------|
| T&M headcount | Hours × rate | Low-medium | Linear (hire to grow) | Client pulls back hours |
| Fixed-fee project | Defined scope | High if AI-assisted | Good | Scope creep, rework |
| Platform subscription | Monthly/annual | High (SaaS-like) | Excellent | Build cost, churn |
| Managed AI retainer | Monthly retainer | Medium-high | Good | Churn, delivery quality |
| Consulting | Day rate | Very high | Poor (expertise bottleneck) | Market cycle |

---

## The Indian IT Giants' Shift

All major Indian IT firms are explicitly moving:
- **Away from**: T&M, headcount expansion, junior developer volume
- **Toward**: outcome-based, platform-led, managed AI services

Evidence:
- HCLTech, TCS, Infosys all guided lower T&M mix in 2025–2026 quarterly calls
- Headcount shrinking while revenue grows = revenue per employee rising
- Platform investments (Topaz, AI Force, WisdomNext) are bets on subscription-like platform revenue

---

## For Smaller AI Companies (SMBs Like Trioangle)

Practical mix recommendation for a boutique AI firm targeting SMBs:

1. **Discovery / Scoping (fixed-fee, small)**: ₹5–15K scoping call → paid phase 0 defines project
2. **Project delivery (fixed-fee or milestone)**: ₹1–5L per project; AI-assisted delivery improves margins
3. **Monthly retainer (managed AI)**: ₹8–50K/month depending on complexity; target 60%+ of revenue
4. **Reseller channel**: Agency partners white-label the service → removes direct sales cost

Avoid pure T&M for AI work — clients won't understand why AI work should be billed by the hour when AI itself is cheap.

## Key Metrics for an AI Services Business
- **Revenue per delivery engineer** — rising means AI is being leveraged
- **Retainer MRR** — health of the recurring revenue base
- **Project → retainer conversion rate** — how many one-time clients become monthly clients
- **Gross margin per project type** — which service lines are most AI-leveraged
- **Churn rate** — especially important for retainer clients
