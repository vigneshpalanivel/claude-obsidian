# EU Listing Act — Horizontal Package to Re-Attract Public Capital

The EU Listing Act is a 2024 **horizontal reform package** of three existing financial-services regulations and two directives, designed to reduce listing costs, narrow ongoing-disclosure burden, and bring SMEs back to EU public markets after a decade of net-negative IPO flow. Centre piece is **Regulation (EU) 2024/2809 of 23 October 2024**, which amends the Prospectus Regulation (2017/1129), the Market Abuse Regulation (596/2014), and MiFIR (600/2014). Companion directives (EU) **2024/2810** (amending MiFID II + repealing the 2001 Listing Directive) and **2024/2811** (multi-vote share structures on SME growth markets) travel in the same package. In force **4 December 2024**; phased application **4 Dec 2024 / 5 Mar 2026 / 5 Jun 2026**; companion-directive transposition deadlines **5 Jun 2026** (2810) and **5 Dec 2026** (2811).

## What the Listing Act IS — and What It Is NOT

**Three things the Listing Act does at Regulation level (Reg 2024/2809):**

1. **Streamlines prospectus content, formats, and exemptions** *(Article 1, amending Reg 2017/1129)* — shorter standard prospectus with a 300-page cap; two **new prospectus formats** (EU Follow-on for already-listed issuers ≥18 months; EU Growth issuance for SMEs/SME-growth-market companies); fungibility exemption **20 % → 30 %**; **single €12M offer threshold** (or MS-elected **€5M**) replacing the old €1M / €1M-to-€8M dual structure; longer URD validity (frequent-issuer status after **1** consecutive year, down from 2); language regime narrowed to summary-only translation.
2. **Reduces ongoing MAR burden for listed issuers** *(Article 2, amending Reg 596/2014)* — **Article 17 protracted-process disclosure narrowed to the final event only** (the single biggest ongoing-compliance change); buy-back reporting routed to **one NCA (most relevant market)** instead of every venue's NCA; managers'-transaction threshold raised **€5k → €20k** (MS opt-up to €50k); SME-proportionate administrative-sanction floors; new cross-border order-data and collaboration mechanisms (new Articles 25a, 25b).
3. **Narrows MiFIR Article 25** *(Article 3, amending Reg 600/2014)* — surgical adjustment of order/transaction record-keeping scope for investment firms; do not confuse with the broader **MiFID/MiFIR Review (Reg 2024/791)** which is a separate parallel package.

**Two things the package does at Directive level (companions to 2024/2809):**

4. **Directive (EU) 2024/2810** — amends MiFID II to relax investment-research bundling for issuers with market cap < €1bn, refines SMEGM liquidity-contract regime, and **repeals the 2001 Listing Directive (2001/34/EC)**. Transposition by **5 Jun 2026**.
5. **Directive (EU) 2024/2811** — Member States must permit **multi-vote share structures at first admission to an SME growth market** subject to MS-defined safeguards (sunset clauses, escalating-vote ratios, related-party protections). Transposition by **5 Dec 2026**.

**What the Listing Act is NOT:**

- Not a new substantive regulation — every Listing Act obligation lives in an *amended* host regulation (Prospectus / MAR / MiFIR / MiFID II). The Listing Act itself is an amender, like [[IPR-Regulation]].
- Not a venue typology change — RM / SMEGM / MTF / DLT MTF distinctions remain as before.
- Not retro-applicable for multi-vote shares — only first-admission IPOs benefit from the 2024/2811 carve-out.
- Not a replacement for the MiFID/MiFIR Review (Reg 2024/791 + Dir 2024/790) — the two packages are independent and run on different calendars; tracking only one will produce gaps.

## The Problem the Listing Act Tries To Solve

| Symptom | Cause cited in recitals | Listing Act remedy |
|---|---|---|
| Net-negative EU IPO flow 2015–2023; SMEs delisting to US or staying private | Prospectus drafting cost; €1M/€8M MS-dispersed threshold creating regulatory shopping; 20% fungibility cap forcing full prospectuses for small follow-ons; EU-Growth prospectus still too close to standard prospectus | New 300-page standard prospectus; EU Growth issuance prospectus with materially lower disclosure; fungibility → 30%; single €12M threshold; URD frequent-issuer status after 1 year |
| Listed issuers cite Article 17 MAR as the most onerous ongoing-disclosure rule; "intermediate step" disclosure forces premature announcements | MAR Article 17(1) interpreted as requiring disclosure of intermediate steps in protracted processes | Disclosure trigger narrowed to **final event**; intermediate steps must be kept confidential but not disclosed |
| PDMR €5k threshold caught immaterial small transactions; high notification noise | Threshold unchanged since MAR 2014 | Raised to €20k (default) / €50k (MS opt-up) |
| Sell-side research coverage of SMEs collapsed under MiFID II unbundling | Unbundling rule prohibited bundling research with execution payment | Companion Dir 2024/2810: bundling permitted for issuers with market cap < €1bn |
| One-share-one-vote default deterred founder-led SMEs from EU IPO vs US dual-class listings | EU listed-companies law required equal voting | Companion Dir 2024/2811: multi-vote shares permitted at SMEGM IPO |

## Reform-Limb Map — Who Cares About What

| Reader | Limb that bites | What to actually do |
|---|---|---|
| Founder / CFO planning EU IPO in 2026+ | **Prospectus** + **MAR** + (if SMEGM) **multi-vote shares** | Plan filing date around 5 Mar 2026 (new formats) and 5 Jun 2026 (new threshold); decide RM vs SMEGM with updated cost model; assess multi-vote share appetite |
| Already-listed issuer (RM or SMEGM) | **MAR Art 17 protracted-process** (highest impact); MAR Art 19 PDMR; MAR Art 5 buy-back routing | Rewrite disclosure-committee playbook for "final event only"; update PDMR notification template to €20k; re-route buy-back reporting to single NCA |
| SME considering secondary follow-on | Prospectus fungibility 30 % + EU Follow-on prospectus | Use 30 % fungibility exemption (in force since 4 Dec 2024) before deciding whether to file follow-on; if filing, wait for 5 Mar 2026 EU Follow-on format if timing permits |
| Investment firm — execution / research | **MiFIR Art 25** narrowing; Dir 2024/2810 research bundling | Update record-keeping policy; rework inducement / research-payment policy ahead of 5 Jun 2026 |
| Trading venue / SMEGM operator | Dir 2024/2810 (liquidity contracts + SMEGM regime) + new MAR Arts 25a, 25b (collaboration platforms, cross-border order data) | Adjust market-rules; participate in ESMA collaboration platforms; prepare for MS designation under Art 25a if EU equity turnover ≥ €100bn and cross-border ratio ≥ 50 % |
| Third-country issuer | Reformed Art 29 PR (equivalence) + Art 30 PR (cooperation) | File third-country prospectus with home-MS NCA (no re-approval); re-validate equivalence framework — exclusions for non-cooperative tax / high-AML-risk jurisdictions |

> [!warning] Do not confuse the Listing Act with the MiFID/MiFIR Review
> Both run in parallel; both touch MiFIR; both have application dates in 2026. They are different reform packages with different objectives. The Listing Act narrows MiFIR Art 25 only and amends Prospectus + MAR; the MiFID/MiFIR Review (Reg 2024/791 + Dir 2024/790) substantively rewrites MiFIR transparency and reporting and amends MiFID II. See [[MiFID-III]] / [[MiFIR-Operational-Mechanics]] for the Review.

## Tokenised Securities Under the Listing Act

Tokenised transferable securities (security tokens, tokenised bonds, tokenised equity) are financial instruments under MiFID II Art 4(1)(15). They sit **inside** the Prospectus / MAR / MiFIR perimeter — and therefore inside the Listing Act perimeter — even when issued and traded on DLT infrastructure under the [[DLT-Pilot-Regime]]. The Listing Act does **not** change the classification taxonomy. Practical consequences:

- **Prospectus reforms apply identically** to tokenised issuances. The new EU Follow-on / EU Growth issuance formats are available where eligibility criteria are met.
- **MAR Article 17 final-event rule** applies identically. On-chain visibility of internal process steps (e.g. multisig confirmations, off-chain governance votes) does **not** create a disclosure trigger; leaks must be handled under MAR Art 17(7).
- **PDMR transactions on-chain** are aggregated to the €20k / €50k threshold in fiat-equivalent value. Wallet-monitoring tooling is required.
- **MiCA crypto-assets** sit **outside** the Listing Act. Asset-referenced tokens, e-money tokens, and pure utility tokens follow the MiCA regime — see [[MiCA-Regulation]].
