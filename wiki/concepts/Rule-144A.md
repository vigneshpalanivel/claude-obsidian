# Rule 144A — QIB Resales of Restricted Securities

Rule 144A is a non-exclusive safe harbor under the U.S. Securities Act allowing **resales of restricted securities to Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIBs)** without registration. Adopted in 1990 (same package as Reg S), it created the U.S. institutional private market — the equivalent of a Eurobond market for big pools of regulated capital inside the U.S.

It is a **resale** safe harbor, not an issuance one. Issuers do not sell directly under 144A; they sell to an initial purchaser (typically an investment bank), which immediately resells to QIBs in reliance on 144A.

## Who Is a QIB

Per Rule 144A(a)(1), a QIB is, broadly:
- An institution owning and investing on a discretionary basis ≥ **$100M** in securities of unaffiliated issuers (e.g., insurance companies, investment companies, employee benefit plans, banks, registered investment advisers' managed accounts)
- A registered broker-dealer owning and investing ≥ $10M
- Banks satisfying both the $100M test and a net worth ≥ $25M

Family offices, accredited individuals, and small institutions are **not** QIBs.

## Why It Matters Alongside Reg S

The two safe harbors are routinely combined into a single global offering:
- **U.S. tranche** sold under Rule 144A to QIBs
- **Offshore tranche** sold under Reg S to non-U.S. persons

Each tranche relies on its own safe harbor. The deal documents (purchase agreement, offering memorandum, transfer legends) must cleanly preserve the boundary so that one tranche's marketing does not become "directed selling efforts" against the other.

Recent examples cited in market commentary include MicroStrategy's $3B convertible notes and AECOM's $1B bond issue — both 144A/Reg S structures.

## Information Requirement

If the issuer is not an Exchange Act reporting company, 144A requires that QIB buyers have access (on request) to a basic information package: company description, products/services, recent financial statements (audited where available). Most issuers satisfy this with a private placement OM.

## Resale Restrictions

Securities sold under 144A are **restricted securities** — buyers must hold them or resell only:
- To another QIB (chained 144A resales), or
- Offshore under Reg S, or
- After Rule 144 holding period (6 mo / 1 yr depending on issuer reporting status), or
- Pursuant to another exemption / registration

Form 144A securities trade on PORTAL and similar QIB-only systems; pricing screens are gated.

## When To Use 144A vs 144A + Reg S vs Reg S Only

| Goal | Structure |
|------|-----------|
| Raise from U.S. institutions only | 144A only |
| Raise from U.S. institutions + offshore investors | 144A + Reg S (global offering) |
| Raise from offshore investors only, U.S. is passive at most | Reg S only |
| Raise from U.S. retail accredited investors | Reg D 506(b) or 506(c) — not 144A |

A 144A/Reg S combined offering is "more intensive, expensive and time-consuming than a Reg S only offering" — typically only worth it above ~$100M deal size.
