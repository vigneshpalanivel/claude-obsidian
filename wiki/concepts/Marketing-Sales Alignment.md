# Marketing-Sales Alignment

The structural and operational integration of marketing and sales functions around shared revenue goals. The default state in most B2B companies is misalignment — 82% of C-suite executives believe they're aligned while 65% of practitioners report they're not.

The financial cost of misalignment: $1 trillion burned annually across B2B organizations. Aligned companies grow 19-20% faster and close 38% more deals.

---

## Why It Matters More in the AI Era

When AI compresses delivery time, **the sales and marketing story becomes the primary differentiator**. The product (code, features, software) is no longer the moat — clarity, trust, and certainty of outcomes are. Alignment ensures a consistent message from first marketing touch through contract close.

---

## The Five Structural Practices

### 1. Shared Definitions (MQL/SQL)
Create joint criteria for what qualifies as a Marketing Qualified Lead and a Sales Qualified Lead. Without this, 79% of MQLs do not convert because sales didn't agree they were ready.

- Write a Service Level Agreement (SLA) specifying: how many leads marketing delivers per month, what quality looks like, how fast sales follows up
- Agreed lead stages: Subscriber → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Customer

### 2. Shared KPIs (Not Departmental Vanity Metrics)

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| MQL-to-SQL conversion rate | Is marketing sending quality leads? |
| Sales cycle length | Are prospects arriving educated? |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (blended) | Are we efficient together? |
| Marketing-sourced revenue % | How much pipeline did marketing create? |
| Win rate | Is messaging matching buyer expectations? |

### 3. Shared Tools / Single Source of Truth
One CRM (HubSpot or Salesforce) integrated with marketing automation. Both teams see the same lead data, engagement history, and pipeline in real time. 80% of marketing content goes unused because sales can't find it — a shared content library inside the CRM solves this.

### 4. Collaborative Content Creation
Sales knows the objections. Marketing knows how to package stories. The loop:
- Sales provides a monthly "objection and question" list
- Marketing builds content (case studies, one-pagers, email sequences) that address those specific objections
- Sales reports back on what content actually moved deals

### 5. Regular Cadence
- **Weekly**: 15-minute cross-team stand-up — pipeline, conversion, blockers
- **Bi-weekly**: Strategy sync — what's working in messaging, what's falling flat
- **Monthly**: Deep review — channel performance, content usage, win/loss analysis
- **Quarterly**: ICP review — who are we winning with, who are we losing to

## RevOps as the Operating Model

Revenue Operations (RevOps) is the function that enforces consistency across marketing, sales, and customer success. 57% of B2B companies have a dedicated RevOps function or are building one. For small software companies, this can be one person — or a shared responsibility between a marketing lead and sales lead with clear ownership.
