# Blog Article Templates — Trioangle Custom Development & AI Engineering

> Reusable writer briefs for all 7 pillars of the Service content strategy. Fill in the `[VARIABLES]` below for any pillar/vertical/persona combination to produce a complete article brief.
>
> Reference: [[ICP - Service]] · [[Content Strategy - Service]]

> [!IMPORTANT] Two Non-Negotiable Positioning Rules
> Every template must comply with both rules before submission.
>
> **1. AI-Era Positioning Rule** — Never position on "we build faster with AI." Position on domain expertise, certainty of delivery, and risk reduction. Service buyers in 2026 have seen too many AI demos that delivered nothing. They are buying a named team with 800+ domain-specific engagements, not speed. "Faster with AI" invites "then why are you expensive?" and loses.
>
> **2. Trust & IP Positioning Rule** — Never defend trust with promises ("we're transparent," "we communicate well," "we're senior"). Defend it with verifiable structural guarantees: Day-1 GitHub access, contractual IP transfer, 48-hour silence → 5% contract credit, pre-contract engineer introduction, Discovery Sprint escape hatch. Every Decision-stage CTA block must reference at least one structural guarantee — never an adjective.

> [!NOTE] Persona Abbreviations
> - **P1** — Scaling CTO (funded Series A/B, ex-FAANG, deeply technical)
> - **P2** — Founder-CTO (non-technical founder, post-Seed, investor pressure)
> - **P3** — Enterprise Digital Buyer (Head of Digital, legacy modernization, RFP-driven)
> - **P4** — MENA Relationship Buyer (UAE/KSA, relationship-first, Vision 2030)
> - **P5** — AI-Forward Platform Operator (live platform, skeptical of AI vendors, domain-specific AI needs)
>

## Global Writer Briefs — Apply to All Templates

### Proof & Credibility (every Decision / Consideration article)

- Every Decision and Consideration-stage article must include at least one `[PROOF_METRIC]` from the Part 10 Proof Signal Mapping table in the conclusion paragraph, immediately before the CTA.
- The "800+ platforms across 150+ countries since 2015" stat is self-reported pending internal audit ([[ICP - Service]] validation warning). Do not cite it in high-stakes RFP responses or enterprise proposals without verifying the underlying figure. For regular blog copy, use it with confidence but never as the *only* proof point — always pair with a specific named outcome.
- Proof metrics must be specific: "$X saved," "Y% uptime," "N-week delivery," not "faster," "cheaper," "reliable."

### Structural Trust (every Pillar 2 + every Decision-stage article)

- Before the CTA, name at least one structural guarantee from the `[STRUCTURAL_GUARANTEE]` list. This is non-negotiable in Pillar 2 and Decision-stage content.
- Never use the words "transparent," "communicative," or "senior team" without immediately pairing them with a verifiable mechanism (GitHub access, contract clause, escrow).

### Objection Pre-Emption

- Identify the 1–2 most likely objections for the article's persona from the Part 10 Objection Handler Mapping table. Embed the response language naturally in the body — never as a standalone FAQ or Q&A block.
- For articles targeting post-failed-agency prospects (#79, vendor-fatigue context), lead with empathy, acknowledge the pain, and do **not** offer a Discovery Sprint CTA — only a no-pitch architecture review.

### CTA Compliance Check

Before submitting any draft, verify:
1. The CTA matches the article's persona per Part 3 CTA Strategy (wrong CTA on right article = wasted traffic).
2. Seed-stage Founder-CTO articles (#45, #46, #47, #77) use the **free 20-min fit call** CTA, not Discovery Sprint — qualification comes first.
3. Enterprise articles (Pillar 5) reference both the 80% cost anchor and 16-week delivery anchor before the CTA.
4. MENA articles (Pillar 6) use the relationship-first CTA — no forms, no response-SLA language, no pricing.
5. AI-Forward articles (Pillar 7) use engineering-language CTAs — never "AI transformation," "strategy engagement," or "train a custom LLM."
6. `†`-tagged articles are scheduled for *publishing* normally but **withheld from outbound ABM** until the matching `[TRIGGER_EVENT]` is confirmed for the specific prospect.

### AI-Era Compliance Check

- No headline or CTA positions Trioangle on "faster with AI" or "AI-powered development speed" — violates the ICP positioning rule.
- If the article mentions AI coding tools (Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code), acknowledge what they do well before documenting the domain-expertise gap. Dismissive framing loses P5 immediately.
- AI feature claims must cite A/B test framing: "[metric] improved by X% vs rule-based baseline, measured over N weeks in production." No vibe claims.

## Adding a New Vertical (Future Expansion)

When Trioangle adds a new platform vertical (e.g., insurance, healthcare marketplace, B2B procurement), the minimum content expansion is:

1. **Add a row** to the Vertical Variable Table above with platform type, 2–3 domain edge cases, and reference clients.
2. **Produce T1** (Cluster 3.1 vertical platform architecture article) as the foundation.
3. **Produce T14** for the 1–2 most obvious AI features in the new vertical (Cluster 7.2).
4. **Optional T11** if the vertical has enterprise-buyer relevance (Cluster 5.2).
5. **Cross-link** the new T1 article to all relevant Cluster 3.2 core component articles per Part 6 Rule 6.
6. **Update** [[Content Strategy - Service]] Part 4 vertical keyword table with the new keyword row.
7. **Update** [[ICP - Service]] Trioangle Product Verticals reference if this is a new formal vertical, not a sub-segment of an existing one.

Minimum of 2 articles per new vertical (T1 + one T14). Scale up from there based on keyword volume and pipeline signal.
