# UCITS Business Readiness Checklist
## The Legal Advisor's First-Meeting Guide for UCITS Fund Managers and Management Companies

**Prepared by InnBlockchain** | Smart Contract Engineering & Security Audit for Regulated FinTechs
**Regulation:** UCITS Directive — Directive 2009/65/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council (recast); as amended by Directive 2014/91/EU (UCITS V on depositaries) and Directive (EU) 2024/927 (AIFMD II, which also amends UCITS)
**Status:** In force. Core UCITS V depositary provisions applicable since 18 March 2016. AIFMD II UCITS amendments transposition deadline 16 April 2026.
**Document type:** Pre-authorization readiness assessment — to be completed before establishing a UCITS management company or launching a UCITS fund

---

> **Who this document is for:** Fund promoters, portfolio managers, legal counsel, compliance officers, and fund administrators structuring EU retail investment funds, including tokenized UCITS. UCITS is the dominant retail fund framework in the EU and globally: over €12 trillion in net assets across approximately 50,000 funds. Any pooled fund distributed to retail investors across the EU should be assessed against UCITS first. This checklist mirrors the intake assessment a specialist EU fund-regulatory lawyer would walk you through in a first advisory session.

---

> **How to use this checklist:** Work through each section in order. Every checkbox represents a concrete requirement. UCITS is a product passport as well as a manager-authorization regime — both the management company (or self-managed investment company) and the fund itself must satisfy regulatory requirements. Items you cannot tick represent gaps that must be closed before authorization is applied for or units are marketed.

## SECTION 1 — WHAT IS A UCITS AND DOES IT APPLY?

*UCITS is a specific, defined fund type. Not every retail fund qualifies or needs to qualify as UCITS. If your fund structure meets the UCITS definition, it must be authorized as UCITS or cannot be marketed as UCITS. You cannot use the UCITS passport without authorization.*

---

### 1.1 — Does Your Fund Qualify as a UCITS?

A UCITS is a collective investment undertaking that:

- [ ] **Sole purpose** is the collective investment of capital raised from the public *(Article 1(2)(a) — the AIF carve-out above is the reverse)*
- [ ] **Operates on the principle of risk-spreading** — diversified portfolio
- [ ] **Units are, at the request of holders, repurchased or redeemed** directly or indirectly out of the undertaking's assets — UCITS is inherently open-ended with redemption rights

If your fund proposes to be open-ended, raise from the public (including retail investors), and invest in a diversified, regulated portfolio, the UCITS framework is likely applicable.

---

### 1.2 — Carve-Outs: What Is NOT a UCITS?

- [ ] **Closed-ended funds** — UCITS must be open-ended with redemption rights; closed-ended structures are AIFs *(see AIFMD checklist)*
- [ ] **Funds that do not operate on risk-spreading** — concentration strategies do not qualify
- [ ] **Funds not marketed to the public** — private funds with restricted investor bases may be structured as AIFs without the UCITS product constraints
- [ ] **Funds investing primarily in real assets** (real estate, commodities, infrastructure) — these investment strategies are generally not eligible for UCITS (see Section 6 on eligible assets)
- [ ] **Loan originating funds** — UCITS cannot originate loans as a primary strategy
- [ ] **Hedge funds with unrestricted leverage** — UCITS leverage is strictly limited; a leveraged absolute-return strategy may exceed UCITS limits

> **Legal advisor note:** A fund structured as UCITS must accept the full product constraints — eligible assets, diversification limits, leverage limits, borrowing limits, and continuous redemption obligations. If the investment strategy requires any flexibility outside these constraints, UCITS is not the right vehicle. Use AIFMD for the fund and consider whether the target investor market justifies the UCITS constraints. Many fund promoters opt for Luxembourg SICAV-RAIF (AIFMD) or Irish QIAIFs for strategies that need UCITS economics but AIFMD flexibility.

**Reference:** UCITS Article 1 (scope and definitions)

---

### 1.3 — UCITS Structures (Article 1(3))

UCITS may be structured as:

- [ ] **Open-ended investment company (SICAV/OEIC)** — a corporate structure; the investment company manages itself or appoints a ManCo
- [ ] **Common fund (FCP/CCF)** — a contractual structure; requires a management company to manage it *(no legal personality)*
- [ ] **Unit trust** — contractual / trust structure; requires a management company

The structure choice determines whether you need an external ManCo or whether the fund can be self-managed as an internally managed investment company.

---

## SECTION 2 — MANAGEMENT COMPANY AUTHORIZATION

*Every UCITS that is a common fund or unit trust requires an authorized management company (ManCo). An investment company may either appoint an external ManCo or manage itself internally.*

---

### 2.1 — Do You Need a Management Company?

- [ ] **Common fund (FCP/CCF)** → **external ManCo required** — the fund has no legal personality and cannot act without a ManCo
- [ ] **Unit trust** → **external ManCo required** — the trustee is not the ManCo; a ManCo must be separately appointed
- [ ] **Investment company (SICAV/OEIC)** → choice:
  - Appoint an **external ManCo** (most common for institutional fund promoters)
  - Manage **internally** (investment company is its own ManCo) — the investment company itself must meet ManCo authorization requirements *(Article 29)*

---

### 2.2 — Home Member State for ManCo Authorization (Article 7)

- [ ] **Home MS identified** — the home MS for a ManCo is the MS where its registered office is located *(Article 2(1)(e))*
- [ ] **Application filed with home MS NCA** — UCITS ManCo authorization is granted by the home MS NCA (Luxembourg CSSF, Ireland CBI, Germany BaFin, France AMF, etc.)
- [ ] **ManCo passporting** — a ManCo authorized in one MS can manage UCITS established in other MSs and can market UCITS in all MSs under the European management company passport *(Article 17)*

---

### 2.3 — Application Content (Article 7)

The ManCo authorization application must include:

- [ ] **Programme of operations** — types of UCITS to be managed, organizational structure, human resources, outsourcing arrangements
- [ ] **Identity of shareholders or members with qualifying holdings** — ≥10%; evidence of suitability
- [ ] **Identity of directors and senior management** — minimum two; fit-and-proper documentation (CVs, criminal-record certificates, conflict declarations)
- [ ] **Initial capital evidence** — proof of paid-up capital *(see Section 3)*
- [ ] **Close links assessment** — description of any close links between the ManCo and other persons that might impair effective supervision
- [ ] **Depositary information** — if applicable at authorization stage

> **Legal advisor note:** Authorization decisions under UCITS are to be made within 6 months of a complete application. In practice, NCAs in Luxembourg and Ireland run a pre-application process — early dialogue with the NCA is standard and typically accelerates the formal review period. Budget 9–15 months from start of preparation to authorization for a new ManCo.

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 5–10 (authorization); Article 7 (conditions and procedure)

---

## SECTION 3 — CAPITAL REQUIREMENTS

*UCITS ManCos must maintain minimum capital continuously. The requirements mirror those applicable to AIFMs.*

---

### 3.1 — Initial Capital (Article 7(1)(a))

| ManCo / Company type | Minimum initial capital |
|---|---|
| **External ManCo** (managing UCITS for others) | **€125,000** |
| **Investment company managing itself internally** | **€300,000** |

- [ ] Capital demonstrated at authorization
- [ ] Capital maintained continuously; below-capital events require immediate NCA notification

---

### 3.2 — Additional Own Funds for AUM Above Threshold (Article 7(1)(a)(ii))

Where total assets under management exceed **€250,000,000**:

**Additional own funds = 0.02% × (total AUM − €250,000,000)**

- [ ] AUM calculated correctly — net asset value of all UCITS managed *(Article 7(1)(a)(ii) specifies: for portfolios of UCITS managed by the ManCo)*
- [ ] **Cap applied:** Total required own funds (initial + additional) need not exceed **€10,000,000** *(Article 7(1)(a)(ii) second subparagraph)*
- [ ] **25% of prior-year fixed overheads** checked — capital must always be at least 25% of prior-year fixed overheads *(Article 7(1)(a)(iii))*
- [ ] **Alternative — professional indemnity insurance (PII):** half of the additional own-funds requirement may be met by PII where the NCA accepts this *(Article 7(1)(a)(iv))*

> **Practical note:** The UCITS ManCo capital formula is numerically identical to the AIFM formula (€125k / €300k base, 0.02% above €250M, €10M cap). A super ManCo authorized under both UCITS and AIFMD manages combined AUM across both portfolios for the capital calculation.

**Reference:** UCITS Article 7(1)(a) (own funds requirements)

## SECTION 4 — OPERATING CONDITIONS FOR THE MANAGEMENT COMPANY

*UCITS ManCos must operate to high standards. The requirements are substantially similar to AIFMD Article 12–18 standards.*

---

### 4.1 — General Principles (Article 14)

- [ ] **Act in the best interests of the UCITS and the unitholders** — fundamental duty
- [ ] **Act honestly, fairly, and professionally** in conducting activities
- [ ] **Avoid conflicts of interest** or, where unavoidable, manage and disclose them
- [ ] **Treat unitholders fairly** — no group receives preferential treatment at the expense of others

**Reference:** UCITS Article 14; Commission Directive 2010/43/EU (implementing measures for organisational requirements, conflicts of interest, conduct of business)

---

### 4.2 — Organisational Requirements

- [ ] **Sound administrative and accounting procedures** *(Article 12(1)(b))*
- [ ] **Electronic data processing facilities** adequate for the management and administration of UCITS
- [ ] **Internal control mechanisms** — management body oversight; risk management; compliance; internal audit
- [ ] **Valuation procedures** — policies for accurate and independent NAV calculation
- [ ] **Compliance function** — adequate, independent, with documented annual compliance report to the management body
- [ ] **Risk management function** — independent of portfolio management; monitors and manages investment risk, liquidity risk, counterparty risk, and operational risk for each UCITS
- [ ] **Remuneration policy** — consistent with Annex II of UCITS V; must not encourage excessive risk-taking; disclosure in annual report

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 12–13 (general obligations of ManCo); Commission Directive 2010/43/EU

---

### 4.3 — Permitted Activities (Article 6(2)–(3))

A UCITS ManCo authorization covers:

- [ ] **Collective portfolio management** (mandatory core activity):
  - [ ] Portfolio management
  - [ ] Risk management
  - [ ] Administration (legal, accounting, NAV calculation, regulatory compliance, unitholder register)
  - [ ] Marketing
  - [ ] Activities related to the assets of the UCITS

- [ ] **Additional non-core activities** (permitted but separate authorization not required if included in UCITS ManCo authorization):
  - [ ] Management of AIFs (AIFMD authorization required separately — dual authorization = "super ManCo")
  - [ ] Discretionary portfolio management for individual investors *(MiFID II permissions required)*
  - [ ] Investment advice *(MiFID II permissions required)*
  - [ ] Safe-keeping and administration of units *(custodian/depositary authorization required)*

> **Legal advisor note:** A UCITS ManCo that also manages AIFs (super ManCo) must hold dual authorization under both UCITS and AIFMD. The two authorizations are granted by the home NCA but are legally separate — there is no single "super ManCo" license. NCAs in Luxembourg and Ireland routinely grant combined authorizations in a single process.

**Reference:** UCITS Article 6 (scope of authorization)

## SECTION 5 — DEPOSITARY REQUIREMENTS

*Every UCITS must have a single depositary. This is a structural requirement with no exceptions.*

---

### 5.1 — Depositary Eligibility (Article 23)

For each UCITS:

- [ ] **Depositary established in the home Member State** of the UCITS *(standard rule; limited cross-border flexibility introduced by AIFMD II — see Section 15)*
- [ ] **Eligible entities:**
  - [ ] **Credit institution** with registered office in the EU, authorized under the Capital Requirements Directive *(Article 23(2)(a))*
  - [ ] **MiFID II investment firm** authorized to provide the ancillary service of safekeeping and administration of financial instruments *(Article 23(2)(b))* — only for UCITS established as investment companies
  - [ ] **Other institution subject to prudential regulation and ongoing supervision** with minimum own funds of €730,000 — only available to investment companies that have designated a ManCo *(Article 23(2)(c))*
- [ ] **Written depositary agreement** — single written contract between the UCITS/ManCo and the depositary; defines all obligations

---

### 5.2 — Depositary Functions (Article 22)

Three distinct duties:

#### Safe-keeping (Article 22(5)):
- [ ] **Financial instruments** — held in custody in a segregated account; depositary liable for loss *(see liability below)*
- [ ] **Other assets** — ownership verified; title and ownership register maintained

#### Cash monitoring (Article 22(4)):
- [ ] **All cash flows monitored** — all subscriptions into a cash account in the UCITS' name, or the ManCo's name on behalf of the UCITS, or the depositary's name on behalf of the UCITS
- [ ] **Subscription monitoring** — ensure subscription proceeds are received and correctly attributed to unitholders

#### Oversight (Article 22(3)):
- [ ] **Subscription/redemption procedures verified** — consistent with applicable law and fund rules
- [ ] **NAV calculation oversight** — depositary verifies calculation process
- [ ] **Instructions from ManCo** — executed consistently with fund rules and applicable law
- [ ] **Distribution monitoring** — income and capital distributions verified

---

### 5.3 — Depositary Liability (Article 24)

- [ ] **Strict liability for loss of financial instruments held in custody** — UCITS V (Directive 2014/91/EU) aligned UCITS depositary liability with AIFMD Art 21(12) standards; depositary is liable for loss of financial instruments even if loss occurred through a sub-custodian; discharged only by proving external event beyond reasonable control with unavoidable consequences *(Article 24(1))*
- [ ] **Negligence standard for other losses** — oversight function failures; negligence-based liability
- [ ] **Unitholder direct claims** — unitholders may bring claims against the depositary through the UCITS or its ManCo *(Article 24(3))*
- [ ] **Sub-custodian arrangements** — depositary may delegate safekeeping to eligible sub-custodians; liability regime follows AIFMD Art 21(11)–(13) model

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 22–26 (depositary); Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2016/438 (depositary implementing rules)

---

## SECTION 6 — INVESTMENT POLICIES AND ELIGIBLE ASSETS

*UCITS product constraints are strict. The eligible assets and diversification rules are the single most important product differentiator between UCITS and AIF structures.*

---

### 6.1 — Eligible Asset Categories (Article 50)

A UCITS may ONLY invest in the following asset categories:

- [ ] **Transferable securities** — admitted to or dealt on a regulated market in an EU/EEA Member State, or equivalent third-country market; or newly issued transferable securities where admission is expected within one year *(Article 50(1)(a)–(c))*
- [ ] **Money market instruments** — instruments normally dealt on money markets, liquid, value determinable at any time; either admitted to regulated market or issued/guaranteed by central bank, government, local authority, or EU institution *(Article 50(1)(h))*
- [ ] **Units / shares of other UCITS and UCIs** — eligible CIUs meeting specific requirements *(Article 50(1)(e), subject to Article 55 limits)*
- [ ] **Deposits** with credit institutions — repayable on demand or within 12 months; credit institution in EU/EEA or OECD member country *(Article 50(1)(f))*
- [ ] **Financial derivative instruments (FDIs)** — admitted to regulated market; or OTC derivatives meeting specific conditions (underlyings must be eligible assets, indices, financial rates, exchange rates, or currencies; regularly and reliably valued; closed at any time by AIFM at fair value; adequate risk management) *(Article 50(1)(g))*
- [ ] **Money market instruments not on regulated market** — issued by specified eligible bodies (sovereign, central bank, company securities, EU institutions) *(Article 50(1)(d))*
- [ ] **Ancillary liquid assets** — up to 20% of NAV; cash pending investment

**Prohibited assets (non-exhaustive):**
- [ ] **Real estate** — direct real estate ownership not eligible (indirect exposure via listed REITs that are transferable securities may qualify)
- [ ] **Physical commodities** — not eligible (commodity derivatives may be eligible within FDI limits)
- [ ] **Private equity / unlisted companies** — not eligible unless newly issued and expecting admission within 12 months
- [ ] **Loan origination** — UCITS cannot lend money to borrowers as an investment strategy
- [ ] **Short selling of transferable securities** — direct short selling not permitted (short exposure via FDIs subject to leverage limits)

---

### 6.2 — Transferable Securities — Special Cases

- [ ] **Closed-ended funds** — units in closed-ended funds listed on regulated markets qualify as transferable securities (not subject to UCITS CIU limits in Article 55, subject to concentration limits in Article 52)
- [ ] **Embedded derivatives** — transferable securities or money market instruments with embedded derivatives must be identified; the embedded derivative must be included in risk management exposure calculations

**Reference:** UCITS Article 50 (eligible assets); Commission Directive 2007/16/EC (eligible assets implementing measures — still applicable)

---

## SECTION 7 — DIVERSIFICATION AND INVESTMENT LIMITS

*The 5/10/40 rule is the most important numerical limit in UCITS. Breaches trigger a mandatory remediation obligation.*

---

### 7.1 — The 5/10/40 Rule (Articles 52–53)

**Core rule:**

- [ ] **5% limit per issuer** — UCITS may invest no more than **5% of its NAV** in transferable securities or money market instruments issued by any single issuer *(Article 52(1))*
- [ ] **10% exception per issuer** — MS may raise the per-issuer limit to **10%** of NAV where total investments in issuers each exceeding 5% do not collectively exceed **40% of NAV** — the "40% aggregate cap" *(Article 52(2))*
- [ ] **10% + 40% rule in practice:** a UCITS may hold up to 10% in any single issuer, but the sum of all positions > 5% must not exceed 40% of NAV *(the "40% basket")*

**Additional per-issuer limits:**

- [ ] **20% limit for deposits** — maximum 20% of NAV in deposits with any single credit institution *(Article 52(1) second subparagraph)*
- [ ] **5% counterparty limit for OTC FDIs** — OTC derivative exposure to any single counterparty may not exceed 5% of NAV; raised to **10%** for credit institutions *(Article 52(1) third subparagraph)*
- [ ] **Combined aggregate limit** — total exposure from transferable securities + money market instruments + deposits + OTC FDI exposure to a single entity must not exceed **20%** of NAV *(Article 52(4))*

---

### 7.2 — Government and Supranational Bonds (Article 54)

For EU Member State or government bonds, a UCITS may hold up to **35% of NAV** in bonds of a single state or public issuer, provided this is permitted by the fund rules and the issuer is listed in the prospectus, and this position is caused by exceptional market circumstances:

- [ ] **35% state-bond exception assessed** — may be used only where "exceptional market circumstances" justify it; must be disclosed in the prospectus

**Sovereign bond concentration exception (Article 54(1)):**
- [ ] **UCITS investing primarily in sovereign bonds** may invest up to **100%** in bonds of any of six or more sovereign issuers if: each issue represents no more than **30%** of NAV; this is permitted by fund rules; disclosed in the prospectus; investors informed that this strategy entails concentrated sovereign risk
  - Examples: EU, ECSC, EIB, EIF, EIF bonds; securities issued by EU Member States

---

### 7.3 — Limits on Other CIUs (Article 55)

- [ ] **Maximum 10% of NAV** in any single UCITS or other eligible CIU *(Article 55(1))*
- [ ] **Maximum 20% of NAV** in any single eligible non-UCITS CIU *(Article 55(1) — interpretation; confirm with counsel)*
- [ ] **Maximum 30% of NAV in aggregate** in eligible non-UCITS CIUs *(Article 55(2) — subject to national flexibility)*
- [ ] **UCITS investing mainly in other CIUs** — total CIU exposure limited such that combined indirect exposure to eligible assets through CIUs does not create concentration beyond single-entity limits
- [ ] **Feeder UCITS** — feeder investing ≥85% of assets in master UCITS qualifies for the master-feeder regime *(Articles 58–67)*; separate Section 11

---

### 7.4 — Index-Replicating UCITS (Article 53)

For UCITS designed to replicate an index authorized by the home MS NCA:

- [ ] **Index replication UCITS** — may hold up to **20%** of NAV in securities of a single issuer (instead of 5/10%) *(Article 53(1))*
- [ ] **Concentrated index exception** — where a single constituent represents an unduly large share, **35% limit** may be applied for one issuer if necessary to reflect the underlying index *(Article 53(1) second subparagraph)*
- [ ] **Index criteria met** — index must be sufficiently diversified, representative benchmark, published in an appropriate manner *(Article 53(1) — criteria in detail in Commission Directive 2007/16/EC)*

---

### 7.5 — Borrowing Limits (Article 83)

- [ ] **Maximum 10% of NAV** for temporary borrowing — UCITS may borrow up to 10% of net assets for liquidity needs, but not for investment purposes *(Article 83(2)(a))*
- [ ] **Maximum 10% of NAV** for acquisition of immovable property essential for direct pursuit of business (investment company structure only) *(Article 83(2)(b))*
- [ ] **Combined borrowing cap** — total borrowing must not exceed **15% of net assets** (10% + 10% provisions cannot be combined beyond 15%) *(Article 83(3))*
- [ ] **Back-to-back transactions** — certain currency transactions do not constitute borrowing for this purpose *(Article 83(4))*

---

### 7.6 — Leverage and Short Selling via FDIs (Article 51)

UCITS may use FDIs to obtain investment exposure (including short exposure) subject to:

- [ ] **Global exposure** — UCITS must ensure global exposure relating to FDIs does not exceed total NAV *(Article 51(3) — effectively a 2:1 leverage cap on total portfolio exposure: 100% NAV physical assets + up to 100% NAV additional FDI exposure = 200% total exposure)*
- [ ] **Risk management procedure** — UCITS must employ a risk management process enabling monitoring and measurement of the risk of FDI positions and their contribution to overall portfolio risk *(Article 51(1))*
- [ ] **Global exposure calculation method** — either Commitment Approach (standard for vanilla FDI strategies) or Value-at-Risk (VaR) approach (for sophisticated strategies):
  - [ ] **Commitment approach** — FDI positions converted to equivalent delta positions in underlying assets; netting/hedging allowed
  - [ ] **VaR approach** — relative VaR (no more than twice the VaR of a comparable benchmark portfolio) or absolute VaR (no more than 20% of NAV at 99% confidence over 20 days) *(CESR/10-788 — Absolute VaR / Relative VaR methodology)*
- [ ] **Cover rule** — UCITS must hold sufficient liquid assets to meet its potential obligations arising from FDIs *(cannot create uncovered short exposure)*

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 50–57 (investment rules); Commission Directive 2007/16/EC; CESR/ESMA guidelines on risk measurement

## SECTION 8 — VALUATION (NAV CALCULATION)

*Accurate, independent NAV calculation is a core UCITS operational requirement — it directly determines the price at which units are issued and redeemed.*

---

### 8.1 — NAV Calculation Requirements (Article 85)

- [ ] **NAV per unit / share calculated at least once per fortnight** (practically: most UCITS calculate daily or at each subscription/redemption)
- [ ] **Valuation methodology documented** for each eligible asset class — market price where available; fair value methodology where not actively traded
- [ ] **Independent NAV verification** — NAV calculation by the ManCo or external administrator, with verification by the depositary
- [ ] **Errors and corrections procedure** — documented policy for identifying and correcting NAV calculation errors; materiality thresholds; investor compensation for material errors

**Reference:** UCITS Article 85 (NAV calculation and publication); Commission Directive 2010/43/EU, Articles 16–21

---

## SECTION 9 — INVESTOR DISCLOSURE

*UCITS has a comprehensive investor disclosure regime. The Key Information Document (KID/PRIIPs) has replaced the KIID for retail investors.*

---

### 9.1 — Key Information Document (KID/PRIIPs) (Article 78)

**For retail investors:** Since 1 January 2023, the UCITS KIID has been replaced by the PRIIPs KID for retail investors under Regulation (EU) 1286/2014 (PRIIPs Regulation, as amended).

- [ ] **PRIIPs KID prepared** for each share class offered to retail investors — maximum 3 pages, prescribed format and content *(PRIIPs Annex I format)*
- [ ] **KID content compliant** with PRIIPs RTS (Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2017/653 as amended):
  - Product name, ISIN, and type
  - What is this product? (objective, investment policy, target investor)
  - What are the risks and what could I get in return? (SRI classification 1–7, performance scenarios)
  - What happens if [manufacturer] is unable to pay out? (protection mechanisms)
  - What are the costs? (cost table — entry/exit/ongoing/performance fees)
  - How long should I hold it and can I take money out early? (recommended holding period, liquidity)
  - How can I complain?
- [ ] **KID updated** — annually as a minimum; immediately when any significant change affects the information
- [ ] **KID provided to investors** before any subscription — providing KID post-subscription is a breach

> **Legal advisor note on UCITS KIID vs. PRIIPs KID:** The PRIIPs KID has been mandatory for retail UCITS investors since 1 January 2023 (following the extension originally set for 1 January 2022). The UCITS KIID remains required for institutional UCITS investors who do not fall within PRIIPs scope. Confirm the investor classification for each distribution channel before deciding which document to produce.

---

### 9.2 — Prospectus (Articles 68–69)

- [ ] **Full prospectus prepared** for each UCITS — mandatory; must be made available free of charge to investors before any subscription
- [ ] **Simplified prospectus** — replaced by KID/PRIIPs; full prospectus now the primary retail disclosure document alongside KID
- [ ] **Prospectus content (Article 69 + Annex I/Schedule A–C):**
  - [ ] Legal and administrative information (name, address, NCAs, depositary, auditor)
  - [ ] Investment objectives and policies *(including specific reference to benchmark, where relevant)*
  - [ ] Risk factors — comprehensive; asset-specific and market-specific
  - [ ] Eligible assets eligible for the UCITS
  - [ ] Subscription and redemption procedures — including cut-off times, settlement, fees, minimum investment
  - [ ] Fee and cost structure — management fees, performance fees, entry/exit charges
  - [ ] Dividend/distribution policy
  - [ ] Conflicts of interest
  - [ ] Depositary information
  - [ ] Applicable law and jurisdiction
- [ ] **Prospectus updated** — annually when accounts are published; immediately on material changes; NCA notified of material changes before publication

---

### 9.3 — Annual and Semi-Annual Reports (Articles 68–70)

- [ ] **Annual report** published within **4 months of financial year-end** *(Article 68(1))*
- [ ] **Half-year report** published within **2 months of end of first 6 months of financial year** *(Article 68(1))*
- [ ] **Annual report content (Article 68(3) + Annex I Schedule B):**
  - Statement of net assets and comparative statement
  - Portfolio of investments
  - Number of units in issue at year-end
  - NAV per unit
  - Income and expenditure account
  - Comparative performance data
  - Remuneration disclosures *(UCITS V amendment)*
- [ ] **Reports available to unitholders** free of charge; filed with home NCA and ESMA UCITS register

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 68–82 (obligations to investors)

---

## SECTION 10 — CROSS-BORDER MARKETING — THE UCITS PASSPORT

*The UCITS passport is the most powerful marketing tool in European retail fund distribution. A single authorization allows marketing in all 27 EU Member States plus EEA.*

---

### 10.1 — Notification Procedure (Article 91)

For each EU Member State in which the UCITS intends to market its units:

- [ ] **Notification to home NCA** — UCITS submits notification file per Article 93:
  - [ ] Letter of intent to market in the host MS
  - [ ] Fund rules / instruments of incorporation
  - [ ] Prospectus *(and any recent amendments)*
  - [ ] KID *(PRIIPs format for retail distribution)*
  - [ ] Most recent annual report and half-year report (if more recent)
  - [ ] Details of any sub-UCITS (master-feeder)
- [ ] **Home NCA transmits to host NCA** within **10 working days** *(Article 93(4))* — far faster than the old UCITS IV procedure (2 months)
- [ ] **Marketing may begin on transmission date** — no host NCA approval required; host NCA cannot block marketing of an authorized UCITS *(Article 93(7))*
- [ ] **Local facilities arrangements** — host MS may require a physical local agent or online facility for investors to: subscribe/redeem units, obtain documents, receive complaint responses, receive price information *(Article 92)*

---

### 10.2 — Changes to Cross-Border Marketing (Article 93a)

- [ ] **Material changes to notification file** — at least one month's written notice to home NCA and host NCA before implementing the change *(Article 93a(1))*
- [ ] **Denotification / withdrawal from host MS** — structured process; investors in the host MS must be informed and given at least 30 working days to redeem before denotification takes effect *(Article 93a(2)–(5))*

---

### 10.3 — Local Facilities (Article 92)

For retail distribution in each host MS, the UCITS must maintain or appoint:

- [ ] **Local paying agent** or equivalent — to process subscriptions, redemptions, and distributions in the local currency
- [ ] **Document availability** — prospectus, KID, annual and semi-annual reports available free of charge in the official language of the host MS
- [ ] **Investor complaints processing** — in the host MS
- [ ] **Price information** — current NAV per unit published in the host MS through an appropriate channel

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 88–98 (cross-border marketing procedures)

---

## SECTION 11 — MASTER-FEEDER STRUCTURES (Articles 58–67)

*UCITS V introduced a harmonized master-feeder framework allowing one UCITS to invest substantially all its assets in another UCITS.*

---

### 11.1 — Feeder UCITS Conditions (Article 58)

A UCITS is a **feeder UCITS** if it invests at least **85%** of its assets in units of another UCITS (the "master UCITS"):

- [ ] **85% minimum investment in master** — remaining up to 15% may be held in deposits, FDIs (hedging only), ancillary liquid assets
- [ ] **Master UCITS must be authorized in an EU Member State** and not itself be a feeder
- [ ] **Agreement between master and feeder** — written master-feeder agreement including exchange of information obligations, supervisory cooperation arrangements *(Article 60)*
- [ ] **Depositary cooperation agreement** between master depositary and feeder depositary *(Article 60(2))*
- [ ] **ManCo cooperation agreement** if managed by different ManCos *(Article 60(1))*
- [ ] **Investor disclosure** — feeder prospectus and KID must disclose the master fund, investment policy, fee structure of both, and consolidated costs

---

### 11.2 — Master UCITS Obligations

- [ ] **Master UCITS may not itself be a feeder** — master must invest directly in assets (not in another UCITS)
- [ ] **Master must provide all documents and information** to feeder ManCo required for the feeder's regulatory compliance
- [ ] **Conversion of existing UCITS to feeder** — requires NCA approval and prior investor notification

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 58–67 (master-feeder structures)

---

## SECTION 12 — MERGERS (Articles 37–57)

*UCITS V established a harmonized regime for cross-border UCITS mergers, enabling fund consolidation across the EU without requiring investor approval in all cases.*

---

### 12.1 — Types of UCITS Mergers

- [ ] **Absorption merger** — assets and liabilities of merging UCITS transferred to receiving UCITS; merging UCITS ceases to exist *(Article 2(1)(p)(i))*
- [ ] **New UCITS merger** — assets and liabilities of merging UCITS transferred to newly constituted receiving UCITS *(Article 2(1)(p)(ii))*
- [ ] **Cross-border merger** — merging and receiving UCITS in different EU Member States; supervised by respective home NCAs; ESMA peer-review process

---

### 12.2 — Merger Requirements

- [ ] **NCA approval** of the proposed merger plan *(Article 39)*
- [ ] **Investor notification** — unitholders of both merging and receiving UCITS notified at least 30 days before effective date; notified of right to request redemption free of charge *(Article 43)*
- [ ] **Independent auditor or depositary certification** of valuation methodology for asset transfer *(Article 41)*
- [ ] **Unitholders' right to free redemption** in the 30-day period following notification *(Article 43(4))*

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 37–57 (mergers)

---

## SECTION 13 — DELEGATION BY MANAGEMENT COMPANY (Article 13)

*ManCos may delegate specific functions but must remain responsible and cannot delegate to the extent of becoming a letter-box.*

---

### 13.1 — Conditions for Delegation

- [ ] **Mandate to delegate documented** and in fund rules where relevant
- [ ] **Delegatee authorized** for investment management or otherwise competent
- [ ] **Letterbox prohibition** — ManCo must retain capacity to supervise delegated functions and take management decisions
- [ ] **Home NCA notified** before delegation takes effect
- [ ] **Sub-delegation** — requires prior written consent of ManCo; delegatee cannot sub-delegate portfolio management without ManCo approval
- [ ] **Third-country delegation** — cooperation agreement between home MS NCA and delegatee's country supervisor required

> **AIFMD II note:** AIFMD II amends both AIFMD and UCITS delegation rules. For super ManCos and UCITS-only ManCos, the enhanced delegation notification requirements of AIFMD II apply to the UCITS delegation arrangements from the home MS national transposition date. Confirm the exact national implementation with your home NCA.

**Reference:** UCITS Article 13 (delegation); Commission Directive 2010/43/EU, Articles 75–82

---

## SECTION 14 — SUPERVISION, PENALTIES, AND REMEDIATION

*UCITS supervision is conducted by the home MS NCA. Penalties are set by national law, not UCITS Level 1.*

---

### 14.1 — NCA Powers (Articles 98–102)

Home MS NCA has powers to:

- [ ] **Require information** from UCITS, ManCo, depositary, and connected parties
- [ ] **Conduct on-site inspections**
- [ ] **Suspend subscriptions or redemptions** where investor protection requires *(Article 98(3)(b))*
- [ ] **Withdraw UCITS authorization** where conditions no longer met *(Article 20)*
- [ ] **Withdraw ManCo authorization** *(Article 11)*
- [ ] **Publish penalty decisions** — publication of administrative sanctions in certain circumstances *(Article 99(2a) as introduced by UCITS V)*

---

### 14.2 — Breach Remediation

- [ ] **Investment limit breach** — if any investment limit is breached due to market movements or exercise of subscription rights, the ManCo must make it a priority to remedy the situation within a reasonable period, taking account of the best interests of unitholders *(Article 49(2))*
- [ ] **Temporary breaches** — limits in Articles 52–54 may be exceeded for up to 6 months from authorization of a newly constituted UCITS *(Article 56)*
- [ ] **Concentration limit monitoring** — automated monitoring system capable of generating alerts at 90% of any limit threshold is standard market practice

**Reference:** UCITS Articles 98–108 (supervision and penalties)

---

## SECTION 15 — AIFMD II UCITS AMENDMENTS (Transposition Deadline 16 April 2026)

*AIFMD II (Directive (EU) 2024/927) also amends UCITS. These changes require ManCo documentation and process updates.*

---

### 15.1 — LMT Framework for UCITS (Mirroring AIFMD II Annex V Changes)

AIFMD II introduces a mandatory LMT framework for UCITS parallel to the AIFMD II amendments for open-ended AIFs:

- [ ] **All UCITS are inherently open-ended** — mandatory LMT activation applies to all UCITS
- [ ] **At least two LMTs from the Annex V list activated** and always available:
  - Redemption gates
  - Notice periods for redemptions
  - Redemption fees
  - Anti-dilution levies (ADL)
  - Swing pricing
  - Redemption in kind
  - Side pockets (for illiquid assets — limited use in UCITS given eligible-asset constraints)
  - Suspension of subscriptions and redemptions
- [ ] **Suspension always available as backstop**
- [ ] **NCA notified of LMT selection for each UCITS**
- [ ] **Fund rules updated** to include LMT provisions
- [ ] **KID and prospectus updated** to describe LMTs available and conditions for activation

---

### 15.2 — Cross-Border Depositary Flexibility

- [ ] **National law check** — AIFMD II permits Member States to allow UCITS to appoint a depositary established in another MS where no appropriate domestic depositary is available; confirm whether your home MS has enacted this flexibility in national transposition

---

### 15.3 — Delegation Changes (UCITS Parallel to AIFMD II Amendments)

- [ ] **UCITS ManCo delegation notifications updated** with enhanced content if home MS national transposition requires

**Reference:** AIFMD II Articles 2–3 (amending UCITS Directive); national transposition measures
