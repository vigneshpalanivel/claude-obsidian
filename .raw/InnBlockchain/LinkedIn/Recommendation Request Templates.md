# LinkedIn Recommendation Request Templates — Vignesh / InnBlockchain

Operational outreach copy for requesting LinkedIn recommendations that support InnBlockchain's ICP-aligned profile positioning. Sister document to `LinkedIn Profile Vignesh Content.md`.

## Strategy Before Copy

1. **Recommendations you write are more valuable than recommendations you request.** Reciprocity drives 60-70% of exchanges. Lead with offering wherever the relationship justifies it.
2. **Send via LinkedIn DM, not email.** ~3x higher response rate — the recommender can write the recommendation in the same window without context-switching.
3. **2-3 requests per week, spread over 4-6 weeks.** Staggered timestamps look earned; eight recommendations landing in 48 hours reads as bought or organised.
4. **Pre-publish minimum: 6-8 recommendations across the five categories below.** 5 is the [[InnBlockchain LinkedIn Strategy]] published minimum; 8 passes Sasha-grade diligence.

## Master Template (Customise per Recommender)

```
Hi [Name],

[1-2 sentence reference to a recent shared interaction, project,
or piece of work.]

Would you be open to writing a short LinkedIn recommendation about
working with me? 3-4 sentences is plenty — most recommendations
are shorter than people think.

If it helps make it easier, here are a few things you might touch
on (only if true and natural for you):
• [Talking point 1 — specific outcome]
• [Talking point 2 — quality/methodology signal]
• [Talking point 3 — character/professional signal]

No pressure either way, and happy to write one back if it would
be useful for you.

Thanks,
Vignesh
```

**Why the structure works:**
- "No pressure either way" close gives the recommender a graceful "no" path — paradoxically increases yes-rate
- Reciprocity offer converts ~40% of declines into "yes, let's swap"
- Talking-point bullets prevent generic recommendations. Without them, you get "great to work with"; with them, you get ICP-aligned specifics

## Category 3 — Security Peer / Auditor at Recognised Firm

**Validates:** Security Sasha specifically

```
Hi [Name],

[Reference to shared context — e.g., conference, mutual project,
disclosed finding, recent post of theirs you engaged with.]

I'm building up the credibility surface on my LinkedIn for
InnBlockchain's audit practice and wanted to ask if you'd be
willing to write a short peer-recommendation. 3-4 sentences is
plenty.

A few angles you might touch on (only if you genuinely think so):
• Technical depth or methodology you've observed in my work
• Specific vulnerability classes or protocol types you've seen
  me handle well
• Industry behavior — responsible disclosure, methodology
  transparency, peer collaboration

Happy to write one back — let me know what you'd want me to
focus on. No pressure either way.

Thanks,
Vignesh
```

**What good looks like:**

> I've reviewed Vignesh's published methodology and crossed paths with him on a [protocol type] audit. Strong on access-control and integer-arithmetic edge cases — the kinds of findings that don't show up in surface-pass reviews. Methodology is transparent and his disclosure behavior is what you want from a peer.

**Caveat:** Peer recommendations from named-firm employees (Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin, Zellic, Halborn alumni) carry the most weight but are the hardest to get — most firms have policies against endorsing competitors. Realistic sources: **independent auditors, ex-employees now consulting, and researchers who've published with or alongside you.**

```
  work (vs. compliance-as-legal-review-at-the-end)
• Quality of the regulatory documentation we produce for
  [MiCA / MiFID II / DORA / specific framework] engagements
• Working with our team alongside [a regulated client's]
  compliance function

If not, completely understood — happy to keep the working
relationship as-is.

Thanks,
Vignesh
```

**What good looks like:**

> I've worked alongside InnBlockchain on two MiCA-regulated client engagements. They treat compliance as an engineering deliverable, not a post-build checkbox — which is rare. The regulatory documentation they produce (vendor risk pack, sample audit reports, jurisdiction briefs) holds up to formal compliance review without rework.

**Caveat:** This is the hardest category to land. Compliance professionals have institutional and personal-liability reasons to avoid public endorsements. Realistic fallback: **a written reference letter or testimonial quote for your website** — doesn't carry their LinkedIn association but still gives you a name and firm to cite in pitches.

## Recommender Selection Filter — Do NOT Ask

- Family, friends, or anyone with the same surname (visible in 5 seconds)
- Anyone whose own LinkedIn looks unprofessional (their credibility transfers to yours)
- Anyone who hasn't done actual paid work with you
- People you haven't spoken to in 12+ months (cold ask, <10% response rate, damages relationship)
- Anyone bound by NDA on the only relevant work (they decline or write something vague)
- Recommenders who would all post within a 48-hour window (stagger)
