## 🧾 Copy decisions (rationale — for future edits)

- **Track-record claim** — "Seven years / 15 protocols / zero critical vulnerabilities on mainnet" is concrete and checkable. A CCO running the vendor-risk pack will probe it. Keep only while provable; update the numbers as the portfolio grows.
- **"Zero critical vulnerabilities on the deployments we've taken to mainnet"** — deliberately bounded. Avoids the ambiguous "zero critical findings," which can read as "we don't find criticals" (the opposite of what an auditor should advertise).
- **RWA ≠ MiCA** — "the regime that actually governs ownership tokens, not MiCA" encodes the Art. 2(4) misframe from both ICPs: RWA ownership tokens are MiFID II financial instruments, excluded from MiCA. This is a credibility signal to Compliance Carol, who catches the MiCA-default on call one.
- **FinTech regimes mapped per-segment, "not defaulted to MiCA"** — mirrors the [[ICP - FinTech]] discipline that the dominant regime varies by segment (WealthTech = MiFID II/Prospectus/DLT Pilot · ExchangeTech = MiCA CASP · LendTech = CCD2/EU AI Act · WalletTech = PSD3/eIDAS 2.0).
- **Audit-queue duration = "2–3 month"** — matches the ICP's own figures (6–12 weeks / ~3 months at top firms). Do NOT inflate to "3–6 months" — Founding Felix knows top-firm queue times and will spot the overstatement.
- **"no mandatory retainer"** (not "you never need us again") — keeps the no-lock-in value prop without contradicting "continuous review" or signalling that a security partner walks away post-launch.
- **`EU AI Act`** retained as a named regulation in the FinTech compliance list (LendTech/RegTech credit-scoring + fraud AI is high-risk under it) — it is regulatory, not AI product-positioning. All AI *marketing* angles (the "we audit what the AI wrote" line, the AI-POV X hook) were removed per 2026-06-22 direction.
- **RWA buyer types collapsed into one company-page bullet** — the ICPs gate crypto-native-RWA (Crypto Native Seg 1) vs. regulated-institution-RWA (FinTech Seg 1) for routing. A company page casts a net rather than routes a lead, so both buyer types should see themselves here. Do not split unless the page's purpose changes.
