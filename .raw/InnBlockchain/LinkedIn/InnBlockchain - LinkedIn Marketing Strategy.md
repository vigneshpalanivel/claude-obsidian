## ⚙️ 3. LinkedIn Algorithm — How to Work With It

Understanding the algorithm determines when and how to post.

### 3.1 Ranking Signals (in order of weight)

1. **Early engagement velocity** — likes, comments, and shares in the first 60–90 minutes after posting are the strongest signal. Post when your ICP is online (see Section 3.2).
2. **Comments over reactions** — a comment carries 3–5× more weight than a like. Write posts that invite a short, easy response.
3. **Dwell time** — how long viewers spend reading. Long-form carousels and multi-paragraph text posts outperform short posts.
4. **Connection proximity** — 1st-degree connections see posts first, then 2nd-degree. Building a 1st-degree network of target ICPs directly amplifies organic reach.
5. **Relevance signals** — LinkedIn matches post content to viewer interests based on their activity and job function. Use ICP-specific keywords (MiCA, RWA, Solidity, smart contract audit) in every post — they act as targeting.

### 3.1.1 What the Algorithm Penalises

- External links in the body of the post (moves viewers off LinkedIn — drop to comments or first reply instead)
- Tagging people who do not engage (dilutes reach)
- Low early engagement — if a post gets no engagement in the first hour, it's suppressed. Delete and repost at a better time rather than leaving it to decay.
- Excessive hashtags (3–5 is optimal; 10+ signals spam)

### 3.2 Optimal Posting Times (EU audience)

| Day | Best Window | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Tuesday | 08:00–09:30 CET | High mid-morning engagement, post-Monday momentum |
| Wednesday | 09:00–10:30 CET | Peak mid-week engagement |
| Thursday | 08:00–10:00 CET | Decision-maker activity spike |
| Monday | 07:30–09:00 CET | Monday morning feed check |
| Friday | Avoid | Low B2B engagement after 12:00 CET |

Post personal (Vignesh) and company page on alternating days — not the same day — to avoid competing for the same audience's attention and to maintain a steady daily presence in the feed.

## 📝 4. Organic Content — Platform Execution

> Content topics and ICP mapping are defined in [[Content Strategy - FinTech]] and [[Content Strategy - Crypto Native]]. This section covers how to execute on the LinkedIn platform.

### Post Format Guide

| Format | Algorithm Strength | Best Use | Length |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Text post** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Thought leadership, takes, contrarian views | 150–300 words; 3–5 short paragraphs |
| **Carousel (PDF)** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Step-by-step guides, checklists, explainers | 7–12 slides; slide 1 = hook, last slide = CTA |
| **Short video (native)** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Founder on camera; trending topic quick takes | 30–90 seconds; captions mandatory (85% watch muted) |
| **LinkedIn Article** | ⭐⭐⭐ (SEO value) | Long-form SEO authority pieces | 1,200–2,500 words; cross-link to InnBlockchain.com |
| **Poll** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Engagement driver; audience research | 2–4 options; follow up with a post using the results |
| **Document post** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Same as carousel but single-file | Use for reports, guides, checklists |
| **Image post** | ⭐⭐ | Announcements, milestones | Only when image adds information — not decoration |

### Content Cadence

| Account | Frequency | Mix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vignesh (personal)** | 4–5x / week | 3× text posts · 1× carousel · 1× video or poll |
| **InnBlockchain (company page)** | 3x / week | 2× repurposed Vignesh posts + 1× company-specific (case study, milestone, service post) |

### Hook Formula (First 2 Lines)

LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters — the first 2 lines decide whether anyone clicks "see more." Use one of these patterns:

- **Counterintuitive claim:** *"Most crypto exchanges get hacked not because of blockchain — it's poor API key management."*
- **Specific number:** *"3 smart contract patterns EU WealthTech platforms get wrong before a MiCA audit."*
- **Fear consequence:** *"One oracle exploit on an RWA platform doesn't drain a pool — it transfers someone's property."*
- **Challenge a common belief:** *"Passing a Slither audit doesn't mean your contracts are safe. Here's what it misses."*

### Post Structure (Text Posts)

```
[Hook — 1–2 lines, stops the scroll]

[Context — 2–3 lines, frames the problem]

[Body — 3–5 bullet points or short paragraphs with the insight]

[Close — 1 line takeaway or call to action]

[Hashtags — 3–5 max, placed at the bottom]
```

### Hashtag Strategy

Use 3–5 hashtags per post. Rotate between:
- **Broad reach:** `#blockchain` `#Web3` `#DeFi` `#FinTech`
- **ICP-specific:** `#MiCA` `#RWATokenization` `#SmartContractSecurity` `#WealthTech`
- **Platform discovery:** `#LinkedInFinTech` `#BlockchainSecurity`

Do not use hashtags in the body of the post — group them at the bottom, after the CTA.

### Engagement Tactics

1. **Comment on ICP posts before publishing your own** — engage on 3–5 posts from target ICPs (Strategic Sam, Technical Tom, Founding Felix) each morning. This warms the algorithm to your profile and increases the chance your post surfaces in their feed.
2. **Drop external links in the first comment**, not the post body — write the post without a link, publish, then immediately add a comment with the article or resource URL. Tags the link as "extra" not "core" to the algorithm.
3. **Reply to every comment within the first hour** — each reply re-triggers the algorithm and keeps the post in active circulation.
4. **Tag sparingly, deliberately** — only tag someone if they are genuinely referenced and likely to engage. One high-value tag that generates a comment > five tags that get ignored.
5. **Repost with commentary** — repost ICP-relevant news (MiCA enforcement update, notable DeFi exploit) with 2–3 lines of your own analysis. Positions you as the filter for your audience.

## 🏆 5. Authority Building

Authority on LinkedIn is not about follower count — it is about being the name that comes to mind when a FinTech CTO or DeFi founder thinks *"I need a blockchain engineering partner."* That association is built through consistent ownership of specific topics, visible expertise, and social proof signals.

---

### 5.1 Own a Topic Cluster

LinkedIn's algorithm categorises creators by topic. Posting consistently on the same subject cluster causes LinkedIn to surface your content to people who engage with that topic — even outside your existing network.

**InnBlockchain's authority topic clusters:**

| Topic Cluster | Primary ICP | Target Association |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| MiCA compliance engineering | FinTech (Sam, Tom, Carol) | *"Vignesh is the person to talk to about MiCA + blockchain"* |
| RWA tokenization security | FinTech WealthTech + CN RWA Felix | *"InnBlockchain builds RWA platforms that survive regulatory review"* |
| Smart contract audit readiness | Crypto Native (Felix, Sasha) | *"They know how to get a protocol audit-ready fast"* |
| AI-generated smart contract risk | Both ICPs | *"They flagged this problem before anyone else did"* |
| Web3 talent gap solutions | Both ICPs (Sam, Paulo) | *"They solve the engineering capacity problem without the hiring risk"* |

**Rule:** At least 3 out of every 5 posts must fall within these clusters. Avoid posting outside them — every off-topic post dilutes the algorithm's categorisation of your profile.

---

### 5.2 LinkedIn Newsletter

A LinkedIn Newsletter sends a direct notification to every subscriber when a new issue is published — it bypasses the feed algorithm entirely.

- **Launch one newsletter** under Vignesh's Creator Mode profile
- **Suggested name:** *"Secure by Design"* — blockchain engineering and Web3 security for FinTech and protocol teams
- **Cadence:** Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks) — consistency matters more than frequency
- **Format:** 400–700 words. One topic per issue. End with a question or a CTA to reply.
- **Topic rotation:** Alternate between FinTech angle (MiCA, RWA, compliance) and Crypto Native angle (audit readiness, protocol security, AI-generated code risk)
- **Growth tactic:** Promote each new issue as a LinkedIn post on publish day — post engagement feeds newsletter discovery

> [!tip] Why Newsletter Over Articles
> LinkedIn Articles have weak distribution — they rely on search and shares. A newsletter subscriber list is a direct audience you own on the platform. Aim for 500 subscribers in the first 90 days.

---

### 5.3 Strategic Comment Strategy

Leaving substantive comments on high-reach posts exposes Vignesh's profile to the commenter's entire audience — often thousands of ICP-relevant followers.

**Who to comment on:**

| Account Type | Examples | Why |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| FinTech thought leaders | Finextra editors, Money20/20 speakers, MiCA regulatory commentators | Their audience is Strategic Sam and Technical Tom |
| Web3 / DeFi ecosystem voices | Protocol founders, DeFi researchers, Web3 VCs | Their audience is Founding Felix and Security Sasha |
| EU blockchain regulatory voices | MiCA implementation commentators, ESMA/EBA discussions | High relevance to both ICPs |

**Comment quality rules:**
- Add a specific point, counterargument, or data point — not "great post" or agreement-only responses
- Keep it to 3–5 sentences — long enough to show expertise, short enough to read
- End with a question when possible — it invites a reply and extends the thread, which the algorithm rewards
- Comment within the first 30 minutes of a post going live — early comments get the most visibility

**Target:** 5–10 high-quality comments per day, before publishing your own post.

---

### 5.4 Social Proof Signals

Authority is validated by what others say about you — not what you say about yourself.

**Recommendations:**
- Target: 5 minimum on Vignesh's profile — at least 2 from FinTech clients, 2 from Crypto Native clients
- Ask immediately after a successful project delivery — not cold, not months later
- Give a specific prompt: *"Could you mention the MiCA compliance side / the audit outcome / the on-time delivery?"*
- Full guidance: [[LinkedIn Profile Audit Vignesh]]

**Being cited and reshared:**
- When a post gets reshared by someone with a large following, engage their repost with a reply — it keeps the thread alive and signals credibility to their audience
- Publish content that is worth bookmarking (checklists, frameworks, post-mortems) — saves and shares carry more algorithm weight than likes

**Speaker and event mentions:**
- After any conference talk, webinar, or panel — post about it on LinkedIn with a key takeaway, not just a photo
- Tag the event organiser (they will often reshare it to their page)
- Link any recording or slide deck in the first comment

---

### 5.5 Consistency & Long-Game Signals

LinkedIn authority compounds slowly, then suddenly. The signals that build long-term authority:

| Signal | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Posting consistency** | Never go more than 3 days without a post — gaps reset algorithm momentum |
| **Topic ownership** | Return to the same topic clusters repeatedly — the algorithm needs 30+ posts on a topic before categorising you as an authority in it |
| **Profile completeness** | A complete, ICP-aligned profile converts authority into inbound — see [[LinkedIn Profile Audit Vignesh]] for the 30-day rebuild checklist |
| **Engagement reciprocity** | Like and comment on posts from ICP followers — they are more likely to engage back on your posts, feeding your reach |
| **Creator Mode on** | Required for newsletter, analytics, and "Follow" CTA — must be enabled |

> [!note] Authority Timeline
> Expect 60–90 days of consistent posting before measurable authority signals appear (search appearances rising, inbound DMs from ICP, content being reshared by others). The first 30 days are invisible — post anyway.
