# Rule 144 — Resale Safe Harbor for Restricted and Affiliate-Held Securities Compliance Checklist
## The Legal Advisor's First-Meeting Guide for Resales of Restricted Securities and Affiliate-Account Sales

**Regulation:** U.S. Rule 144 — 17 CFR § 230.144 (Securities Act of 1933)
**Issuing Authority:** U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
**Status:** In force (as of eCFR update 6 June 2026)
**Document type:** Pre-sale readiness assessment for any resale of restricted securities or any sale for the account of an affiliate of the issuer

---

> **Who this document is for:** Holders of restricted securities (including securities acquired in Reg D private placements, Reg S Category 3 domestic-issuer equity offerings, Rule 144A transactions, and other non-public offerings), affiliates of the issuer (officers, directors, sponsors, controlling shareholders), brokers handling such sales, and counsel structuring resale paths. This checklist mirrors the intake assessment a U.S. securities lawyer would walk you through before any restricted-securities resale or affiliate-account sale.

---

> **How to use this checklist:** Work through each section in order. Every item marked with a checkbox (☐) represents a concrete requirement or determination you must resolve. Items you cannot check off mean the Rule 144 safe harbor is not available for the contemplated transaction — meaning the seller may be a "statutory underwriter" under Section 2(a)(11), the sale may require registration, or another exemption must be relied on. Note that Rule 144 is a **non-exclusive safe harbor** (Preliminary Notes): failure to satisfy Rule 144 does not automatically mean the sale is unlawful — other Section 4(a)(1) analyses may still be available, but at significantly higher risk.

---

## SECTION 1 — SCOPE & STATUTORY FRAMEWORK

*Understand WHY Rule 144 exists before applying it.*

---

### 1.1 — The Statutory Problem Rule 144 Solves

- [ ] **Section 5 of the Securities Act** requires registration for any sale of securities, unless an exemption applies
- [ ] **Section 4(a)(1)** (formerly Section 4(1)) provides an exemption for *"transactions by any person other than an issuer, underwriter, or dealer"*
- [ ] **Section 2(a)(11)** defines "underwriter" broadly to include any person who has *"purchased from an issuer with a view to ... the distribution of any security"* — meaning a person who buys with intent to resell may be a statutory underwriter
- [ ] **Rule 144 is a safe harbor from the Section 2(a)(11) underwriter definition.** A seller who satisfies all applicable Rule 144 conditions is deemed NOT to be engaged in a distribution and therefore NOT an underwriter, making Section 4(a)(1) available

> **Legal advisor note:** Rule 144 does not exempt a sale from registration directly. It removes the seller from the "underwriter" category, which then lets the Section 4(a)(1) "person other than an issuer, underwriter or dealer" exemption apply. The chain is: Rule 144 satisfied → not an underwriter → Section 4(a)(1) available → no registration required.

**Reference:** Securities Act §§ 4(a)(1), 5, 2(a)(11); Rule 144 Preliminary Notes

---

### 1.2 — Three Consequences of a Successful Rule 144 Sale (Preliminary Notes)

If a sale of securities complies with all applicable Rule 144 conditions:

- [ ] **(1)** Any affiliate or other person selling restricted securities is deemed NOT to be engaged in a distribution → not an underwriter for that transaction
- [ ] **(2)** Any person selling restricted or other securities **on behalf of** an affiliate is deemed NOT to be engaged in a distribution → not an underwriter for that transaction
- [ ] **(3)** The **purchaser** in the transaction receives securities that are **NOT restricted securities** (the "wash" effect — but only for non-affiliate Rule 144 sales; see Section 5.3 for tacking rules)

**Reference:** Rule 144 Preliminary Notes

---

### 1.3 — Non-Exclusive Safe Harbor & Anti-Evasion

- [ ] **Non-exclusive:** A person who does not meet all Rule 144 conditions may still claim any other available exemption (e.g., Section 4(a)(7), private resale doctrine, 4(a)(1½)). Failure of Rule 144 is not an automatic Section 5 violation
- [ ] **Anti-evasion principle:** Rule 144 is **not available** for any transaction or series of transactions that, although in technical compliance, is part of a **plan or scheme to evade the registration requirements** of the Act

> **Legal advisor note:** The anti-evasion principle is regularly invoked by the SEC in enforcement matters where a sequence of "technically compliant" Rule 144 sales is structured to function as an unregistered distribution. Pre-arranged sales, sales coordinated across multiple affiliated holders to evade volume caps, and sales timed against material non-public information all fall in this zone.

**Reference:** Rule 144 Preliminary Notes (anti-evasion paragraph)

## SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS (Rule 144(a))

*Rule 144 hinges on four definitions. Misunderstanding any of them collapses the analysis.*

---

### 2.1 — "Affiliate" (Rule 144(a)(1))

- [ ] **"Affiliate"** of an issuer is a person that **directly or indirectly through one or more intermediaries, controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with**, such issuer
- [ ] The test is **control**, not ownership percentage. Control means the power to direct management and policies
- [ ] Affiliate status looks through trusts, holding companies, and other intermediaries — a person cannot escape affiliate status by holding through a controlled entity
- [ ] Officers, directors, and managing members are presumptively affiliates by virtue of position
- [ ] 10%+ holders are commonly presumed potentially affiliates (rebuttable by showing no actual control influence)

> **Legal advisor note:** Rule 144 does not define "control." SEC guidance and case law treat control as a facts-and-circumstances determination. A 5% holder with a board seat and veto rights is an affiliate; a 25% passive holder with no governance rights may not be. Document the control analysis in writing.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(a)(1)

---

### 2.2 — "Person" — Attribution to Household and Controlled Entities (Rule 144(a)(2))

For purposes of determining whose account securities are being sold (relevant for volume aggregation, holding period, and affiliate status), "person" includes the seller PLUS:

- [ ] **(i) Household relatives and spouse** — any relative or spouse of such person, or any relative of such spouse, **any one of whom has the same home as such person**
- [ ] **(ii) Controlled trusts and estates** — any trust or estate in which the seller (or any household person under (i)) collectively owns **10% or more** of the beneficial interest, OR of which any such person serves as trustee, executor, or in any similar capacity
- [ ] **(iii) Controlled corporations and organizations** — any corporation or other organization (other than the issuer) in which the seller (or any household person under (i)) is the beneficial owner collectively of **10% or more of any class of equity securities OR 10% or more of the equity interest**

> **Legal advisor note:** This is the attribution rule that catches affiliates who try to split sales across family members, controlled holding companies, or family trusts. The 10% threshold is collective across the seller and all household members — not per individual. A Florida sponsor with a 4% interest plus his spouse's 7% interest in a holding company triggers the rule.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(a)(2)

---

### 2.3 — "Restricted Securities" (Rule 144(a)(3))

Eight categories of securities are "restricted securities" — meaning they are subject to Rule 144's holding period and other resale conditions:

- [ ] **(i)** Securities acquired directly or indirectly from the issuer, or from an affiliate of the issuer, in a transaction or chain of transactions **not involving any public offering** (private placements under Section 4(a)(2), Reg D Rule 506)
- [ ] **(ii)** Securities acquired from the issuer that are subject to the resale limitations of **Rule 502(d) (Regulation D)** or **Rule 701(c)** (compensatory issuances)
- [ ] **(iii)** Securities acquired in a transaction or chain of transactions meeting the requirements of **Rule 144A**
- [ ] **(iv)** Securities acquired from the issuer in a transaction subject to the conditions of **Regulation CE** (Rule 1001)
- [ ] **(v)** **Equity securities of domestic issuers acquired in a transaction or chain of transactions subject to the conditions of Rule 901 or Rule 903 under Regulation S** (the Rule 905 bridge into Rule 144)
- [ ] **(vi)** Securities acquired under Rule 801 to the same extent as the underlying rights-offering securities were restricted
- [ ] **(vii)** Securities acquired under Rule 802 to the same extent as the tendered/exchanged securities were restricted
- [ ] **(viii)** Securities acquired from the issuer in a transaction subject to a Section 4(5) exemption

> **Legal advisor note (Reg S linkage — critical):** Category (v) is the bridge from Reg S to Rule 144. Domestic-issuer equity acquired under Reg S Rules 901 or 903 is restricted under Rule 144 indefinitely. Rule 905 of Reg S specifically prevents Reg S secondary sales (Rules 901 or 904) from "washing" this restricted status — so the restricted label survives multiple offshore secondary trades. Only a successful Rule 144 sale to a non-affiliate (or registration / other exemption) removes the restricted label.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(a)(3)

---

### 2.4 — "Debt Securities" (Rule 144(a)(4))

For purposes of the debt-specific volume cap in Rule 144(e)(2):

- [ ] **(i)** Any security other than an equity security as defined in Rule 405
- [ ] **(ii)** Non-participatory preferred stock — non-convertible capital stock with preference in dividends and liquidation but no participation in residual earnings or assets
- [ ] **(iii)** Asset-backed securities as defined in Item 1101(b) of Regulation AB

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(a)(4)

---

## SECTION 3 — WHICH PATH APPLIES? (Rule 144(b))

*Rule 144 has TWO distinct paths — non-affiliate and affiliate. The path determines which conditions you must satisfy. Identify yours before proceeding to Sections 4–9.*

---

### 3.1 — Path Determination Decision Tree

Answer these in order:

- [ ] Are the securities being sold "restricted securities" as defined in Section 2.3? (If NO → Rule 144 holding period and current-info requirements do not apply, but if seller is an affiliate, the affiliate conditions still apply)
- [ ] Is the seller an affiliate of the issuer at the time of sale? (See Section 2.1)
- [ ] Was the seller an affiliate at any time during the **90 days immediately preceding** the sale?
- [ ] Is the seller selling securities **on behalf of** an affiliate (e.g., a broker or trustee selling for an affiliate's account)?

| Seller status | Path | Conditions to satisfy |
|---|---|---|
| Currently an affiliate | **Affiliate Path** (Rule 144(b)(2)) | ALL of Rule 144 — current info (c), holding period if restricted (d), volume (e), manner of sale (f), brokers (g), Form 144 (h) |
| Was an affiliate within the prior 90 days | **Affiliate Path** (Rule 144(b)(2)) | Same as above — the 3-month tail rule |
| Selling on behalf of an affiliate | **Affiliate Path** (Rule 144(b)(2)) | Same as above |
| Non-affiliate (and was not within prior 90 days), reporting issuer | **Non-Affiliate Path — Reporting** (Rule 144(b)(1)(i)) | Holding period (d) + current info (c)(1) for first 6–12 months; after 12 months: no conditions |
| Non-affiliate (and was not within prior 90 days), non-reporting issuer | **Non-Affiliate Path — Non-Reporting** (Rule 144(b)(1)(ii)) | Holding period (d) only |

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(b)

---

### 3.2 — Non-Affiliate Path (Rule 144(b)(1))

#### 3.2.1 — Reporting issuer (b)(1)(i)

If the issuer **is, and has been for at least 90 days immediately before the sale**, subject to Exchange Act § 13 or § 15(d) reporting:

- [ ] The seller is deemed not to be an underwriter under Section 2(a)(11) if conditions of **paragraph (c)(1) (current public information) AND paragraph (d) (holding period)** are met
- [ ] **After ONE YEAR has elapsed** since the later of the date the securities were acquired from the issuer or from an affiliate, **the current-public-information requirement (c)(1) NO LONGER APPLIES** to the non-affiliate seller — only the holding period (d) needs to be satisfied

#### 3.2.2 — Non-reporting issuer (b)(1)(ii)

If the issuer **is not, or has not been for at least 90 days immediately before the sale**, subject to Exchange Act § 13 or § 15(d) reporting:

- [ ] The seller is deemed not to be an underwriter if **only paragraph (d) (holding period) is met**
- [ ] **No current-public-information requirement** for non-affiliate sales of non-reporting-issuer securities (after the 1-year holding period clears, sales are essentially unconditional)

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(b)(1)

---

### 3.3 — Affiliate Path (Rule 144(b)(2))

Applicable to:

- [ ] Any affiliate of the issuer selling restricted securities
- [ ] Any person who was an affiliate at any time during the **90 days immediately before the sale** (the "3-month tail" — even after ceasing to be an affiliate, the affiliate conditions apply for 90 days post-exit)
- [ ] Any person selling restricted or non-restricted securities **for the account of an affiliate** (or a person who was an affiliate within the prior 90 days)

For the seller to be deemed not to be an underwriter, **ALL conditions of Rule 144 must be met**:

- [ ] Current public information (paragraph (c))
- [ ] Holding period for restricted securities (paragraph (d))
- [ ] Volume limitations (paragraph (e))
- [ ] Manner of sale (paragraph (f))
- [ ] Brokers' transactions requirements (paragraph (g))
- [ ] Form 144 notice (paragraph (h)) if applicable thresholds met

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(b)(2)

---

## SECTION 4 — CURRENT PUBLIC INFORMATION (Rule 144(c))

*Required for ALL affiliate sales and for non-affiliate sales during the first 12 months for reporting-issuer securities. Often the BINDING CONSTRAINT for sales of non-reporting-issuer securities by affiliates.*

---

### 4.1 — Reporting Issuers (Rule 144(c)(1))

The issuer must be, **and have been for at least 90 days immediately before the sale**, subject to Exchange Act § 13 or § 15(d) reporting AND must have:

- [ ] **(i)** Filed all required Exchange Act reports during the preceding 12 months (or such shorter period the issuer was required to file), **other than Form 8-K reports**; AND
- [ ] **(ii)** Submitted electronically every required Interactive Data File during the preceding 12 months (or shorter required period)

#### Reliance methods on issuer reporting status:

- [ ] **Method 1** — A statement in the issuer's most recent quarterly or annual report that:
  - (a) Issuer has filed all reports required under § 13 or § 15(d) during the preceding 12 months (other than 8-Ks), and has been subject to those filing requirements for the past 90 days; AND
  - (b) Issuer has submitted all required Interactive Data Files during the preceding 12 months
- [ ] **Method 2** — A written statement from the issuer confirming compliance with such reporting/submission requirements
- [ ] **NEITHER method may be relied on if the person knows or has reason to believe the issuer has not actually complied**

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(c)(1)

---

### 4.2 — Non-Reporting Issuers (Rule 144(c)(2))

If the issuer is NOT subject to Exchange Act § 13 or § 15(d) reporting, **adequate current public information is satisfied only if** the information specified in paragraphs (b)(5)(i)(A) through (N) **and** (b)(5)(i)(P) of Rule 15c2-11 (or, for insurance companies, the information specified in § 12(g)(2)(G)(i) of the Exchange Act) is **publicly available**.

- [ ] Rule 15c2-11 information statement publicly available, covering at minimum (per Rule 15c2-11(b)(5)(i)):
  - (A) Exact name of the issuer + any predecessors during the past 5 years
  - (B) Address of issuer's principal executive offices
  - (C) State of incorporation and jurisdiction of organization
  - (D) Exact title and class of the securities
  - (E) Par or stated value
  - (F) Number of shares or total amount of securities outstanding
  - (G) Name and address of the transfer agent
  - (H) Nature of issuer's business
  - (I) Nature of products or services offered
  - (J) Nature and extent of issuer's facilities
  - (K) Name of CEO and members of board of directors
  - (L) Issuer's most recent balance sheet, profit-and-loss, and retained earnings statements
  - (M) Similar financial information for prior fiscal year ends
  - (N) Whether the broker or dealer or any associated person is affiliated with the issuer
  - (P) Material changes since the most recent financial statements

> **Legal advisor note (CRITICAL for non-reporting issuers):** Maintaining current Rule 15c2-11 information is often the binding constraint for affiliate sales of non-reporting-issuer securities. If the issuer does not maintain publicly available updated financials and the other (A)–(N) and (P) items, **affiliates cannot use Rule 144 at all** — even after the holding period clears, even at volume well below the cap. The Rule 144 path is closed entirely. The issuer's commitment (or refusal) to maintain Rule 15c2-11 information is a strategic decision that determines whether affiliates ever have a U.S. resale path.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(c)(2); 17 CFR § 240.15c2-11(b)(5)(i)

---

## SECTION 5 — HOLDING PERIOD FOR RESTRICTED SECURITIES (Rule 144(d))

*Applies only if the securities sold are "restricted securities." Non-restricted securities sold by affiliates skip this section.*

---

### 5.1 — General Rule (Rule 144(d)(1))

- [ ] **Reporting issuer (subject to § 13 or § 15(d) reporting for at least 90 days immediately before the sale):** minimum **SIX MONTHS** must elapse between the later of (a) the date of acquisition from the issuer or an affiliate, and (b) any resale in reliance on Rule 144
- [ ] **Non-reporting issuer:** minimum **ONE YEAR** must elapse between the later of (a) the date of acquisition from the issuer or an affiliate, and (b) any resale in reliance on Rule 144
- [ ] **If acquired by purchase:** holding period does NOT begin until **full purchase price or other consideration is paid** by the acquirer

### 5.1a — Automatic Tacking for Non-Affiliate Holders (Rule 144(d)(1))

> [!key-insight] **The holding period clock applies to "any subsequent holder," not to each individual purchaser.**
> Rule 144(d)(1)(i) and (ii) both state that the minimum period must elapse "between the later of the date of the acquisition of the securities from the issuer, or from an affiliate of the issuer, and any resale of such securities in reliance on this section **for the account of either the acquiror or any subsequent holder of those securities**." The clock measures from when the securities FIRST LEFT the issuer (or affiliate) — it does NOT restart on subsequent transfers between non-affiliates.

- [ ] **Ordinary non-affiliate-to-non-affiliate secondary transfers tack automatically.** When Holder X (non-affiliate) acquires restricted securities from the issuer at T=0, and later sells to Holder Y (non-affiliate) at T+N months, Y's Rule 144 holding-period clock is measured from T=0 — NOT from T+N. Y inherits X's full holding period for Rule 144 purposes
- [ ] **This is the GENERAL rule for ordinary secondary trades.** Not one of the Rule 144(d)(3) "special situations" in Section 5.3 below. Without this automatic tacking, restricted securities could never practically trade in a secondary market — every secondary purchase would restart a fresh 6 or 12-month clock
- [ ] **When tacking does NOT apply (clock starts fresh on the new acquisition date):**
  - The new holder acquires **directly from the issuer** (primary acquisition — treated as a fresh start)
  - The new holder acquires **from an affiliate of the issuer** (purchase from an affiliate is treated like purchase from the issuer for Rule 144 holding-period purposes; the affiliate's prior holding period does NOT tack to the buyer)
  - One of the Rule 144(d)(3) special-situation restarts applies — Rule 145(a) transactions (Section 5.3(viii)), non-recourse pledges (Section 5.3(iv)), or certain amendment-driven conversions (Section 5.3(ii) exception)
- [ ] **Practical implication for the affiliate-free architecture:** Tacking only works as long as the chain of holders is uniformly non-affiliate. A holder who becomes an affiliate by operation of law disrupts the tacking chain for any tokens passing through their hands. This is one of the structural reasons to enforce affiliate-free token holding from the outset

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(d)(1)(i)–(ii); the "any subsequent holder" language is the operative phrase.
**Verbatim source URL:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-17/chapter-II/part-230/section-230.144 (locate § 230.144(d)(1)(i) for the 6-month rule and § 230.144(d)(1)(ii) for the 1-year rule — both contain identical "for the account of either the acquiror or any subsequent holder of those securities" language).

---

### 5.2 — Promissory Notes / Installment Contracts (Rule 144(d)(2))

A promissory note, other obligation to pay, or installment purchase contract is NOT deemed full payment unless ALL three conditions are met:

- [ ] **(i)** Provides for **full recourse** against the purchaser
- [ ] **(ii)** Is **secured by collateral** (other than the purchased securities themselves) with fair market value at least equal to the purchase price
- [ ] **(iii)** Has been **discharged by payment in full** prior to the sale of the securities

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(d)(2)

---

### 5.3 — Special Tacking and Restart Rules (Rule 144(d)(3))

> [!info] **These are SPECIAL situations layered on top of the general Rule 144(d)(1) automatic non-affiliate tacking covered in §5.1a above.** The (d)(3) subsections either:
> - (a) **extend tacking** to specific scenarios that would otherwise look like a fresh acquisition (stock dividends, conversions/exchanges between same-issuer securities, contingent issuance, recourse pledges, gifts, trusts, estates, holding-company formations, cashless option exercises), OR
> - (b) **restart the clock** in specific scenarios that would otherwise look like ordinary transfers (Rule 145(a) merger / share-exchange transactions, non-recourse pledges, certain amendment-driven conversions where consideration was paid).
>
> If a transaction does not fit one of the (d)(3) special situations, the general Rule 144(d)(1) rule from §5.1a applies — tacking for non-affiliate-to-non-affiliate, fresh start for issuer/affiliate acquisition.

Holding period determination in special-acquisition scenarios:

- [ ] **(i) Stock dividends, splits, recapitalizations** — securities acquired as a dividend, split, reverse split, or recapitalization deemed acquired at the same time as the underlying securities
- [ ] **(ii) Conversions and exchanges** — securities received from the issuer solely in exchange for other securities of the same issuer deemed acquired at the same time as the surrendered securities (even if the surrendered securities were not originally convertible). **Exception:** if the original securities did not provide for cashless conversion/exchange and the holder provided consideration to amend them to permit cashless conversion, tacking begins from the date of amendment
- [ ] **(iii) Contingent issuance** — securities issued as contingent payment for sale of a business or assets deemed acquired at the time of the original sale, IF the issuer was then committed to issue subject only to conditions other than further consideration. Employment / non-compete agreements do NOT count as additional consideration
- [ ] **(iv) Pledged securities** — securities bona-fide pledged by an affiliate, when sold by the pledgee after default, deemed acquired when acquired by the pledgor. **Exception:** non-recourse pledges → deemed acquired by the pledgee at the time of pledge (or by purchaser at time of purchase)
- [ ] **(v) Gifts** — securities received from an affiliate by gift deemed acquired by the donee when acquired by the donor
- [ ] **(vi) Trusts** — where a trust settlor is an affiliate, securities acquired by the trust from the settlor (or by trust beneficiaries from the trust) deemed acquired when acquired by the settlor
- [ ] **(vii) Estates** — where the deceased was an affiliate, securities held by the estate (or acquired from the estate by beneficiaries) deemed acquired when acquired by the deceased. **No holding period required** if the estate / beneficiary is not an affiliate (but current public information (c) and Form 144 (h) still apply if applicable)
- [ ] **(viii) Rule 145(a) transactions** — holding period for securities acquired in a Rule 145(a) transaction commences on the acquisition date in that transaction (no tacking from underlying securities, except per (d)(3)(ii) and (ix))
- [ ] **(ix) Holding company formations** — securities acquired in a holding-company-formation transaction tacked back to the predecessor securities, provided three conditions are met:
  - (A) Holding company's securities issued solely in exchange for predecessor securities in a reorganization
  - (B) Holders received same-class securities evidencing same proportional interest, with substantially the same rights
  - (C) Immediately following the transaction, holding company has no significant assets other than predecessor securities and existing subsidiaries, with substantially the same consolidated assets and liabilities
- [ ] **(x) Cashless exercise of options/warrants** — securities acquired solely upon cashless exercise of issuer-issued options or warrants deemed acquired when the option or warrant itself was acquired (even if cashless exercise was not originally provided by the terms). **Exception 1:** if cashless exercise was added via amendment and the holder paid consideration for the amendment, tacking begins from the amendment date. **Exception 2:** for options/warrants that have no investment risk (e.g., employee stock options), tacking begins at the time of exercise

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(d)(3)

## SECTION 6 — VOLUME LIMITATIONS — AFFILIATES ONLY (Rule 144(e))

*Applies ONLY to sales for the account of an affiliate (whether restricted or non-restricted securities). Non-affiliate sales are unrestricted in amount once the holding period clears.*

---

### 6.1 — Equity Securities Volume Cap (Rule 144(e)(1))

In any **three-month period**, the amount of securities sold for the account of an affiliate, together with all sales of the same class for that affiliate in the **preceding three months**, shall not exceed the **GREATEST** of:

- [ ] **(i)** **1% of the shares or other units of the class outstanding**, as shown by the most recent report or statement published by the issuer
- [ ] **(ii)** The **average weekly reported volume of trading** in such securities on all national securities exchanges and/or reported through the automated quotation system of a registered securities association during the **four calendar weeks preceding** the filing of Form 144 (or, if no Form 144 is required, the date of receipt of the order to execute by the broker or the date of direct execution with a market maker)
- [ ] **(iii)** The **average weekly volume of trading** in such securities reported pursuant to an effective transaction reporting plan or effective national market system plan (as defined in 17 CFR § 242.600) during the same four-week period

> **Practical note:** For securities that do not trade on a public market (private placements, illiquid Reg S tokens), prongs (ii) and (iii) effectively yield zero, so the **1% cap is the binding constraint**.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(e)(1)

---

### 6.2 — Debt Securities Volume Cap (Rule 144(e)(2))

For debt securities sold for an affiliate's account, the cap is the **GREATER** of:

- [ ] The limit in Rule 144(e)(1) (above); OR
- [ ] Together with all sales of the same tranche (or class for non-participatory preferred stock) for that affiliate in the **preceding three months**, **10% of the principal amount of the tranche** (or class for non-participatory preferred stock) attributable to the securities sold

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(e)(2)

---

### 6.3 — Determination of Amount and Aggregation Rules (Rule 144(e)(3))

#### Convertible securities

- [ ] **(i)** Where both convertible securities and the underlying class are sold, the convertible amount is treated as the underlying-class equivalent for aggregation purposes

#### Pledgees

- [ ] **(ii)** Securities sold for a pledgee's account (or for a purchaser of pledged securities) during any 3-month period within **6 months** (or **1 year for non-reporting issuer**) after default, aggregated with sales during the same 3-month period for the pledgor's account, shall not exceed the (e)(1) or (e)(2) cap
- [ ] Sales by a pledgee of borrower-pledged securities are NOT aggregated with sales by other pledgees of the same borrower, absent concerted action

#### Donees

- [ ] **(iii)** Sales for a donee's account during any 3-month period within **6 months** (or **1 year for non-reporting issuer**) after the donation, aggregated with sales during the same period for the donor's account, shall not exceed the cap

#### Trust settlors

- [ ] **(iv)** Where securities were acquired by a trust from the settlor, sales for the trust during any 3-month period within **6 months** (or **1 year for non-reporting issuer**) after acquisition by the trust, aggregated with sales during the same period for the settlor's account, shall not exceed the cap

#### Estates

- [ ] **(v)** Sales for an estate's account (or beneficiary's account) during any 3-month period, aggregated with sales during the same period for the deceased person's account prior to death, shall not exceed the cap. **Exception:** no limitation if the estate / beneficiary is not an affiliate of the issuer

#### Concerted action

- [ ] **(vi)** Where two or more affiliates or other persons agree to act in concert for the purpose of selling securities of an issuer, **all securities of the same class sold for the account of all such persons during any 3-month period shall be aggregated** for purposes of the volume cap

#### Excluded sales

The following sales need NOT be included in determining the Rule 144 volume cap:

- [ ] **(A)** Securities sold pursuant to an effective registration statement under the Act
- [ ] **(B)** Securities sold pursuant to Regulation A (Rules 251–263)
- [ ] **(C)** Securities sold in a transaction exempt under Section 4 of the Act and not involving a public offering
- [ ] **(D)** Securities sold offshore pursuant to **Regulation S** (Rules 901–905)

> **Note on (D):** This is a meaningful escape valve for affiliates. Offshore sales under Regulation S do not count against the Rule 144 volume cap. An affiliate seeking liquidity who can find offshore buyers under Rule 904 conditions can sell unlimited amounts without triggering the Rule 144 quarterly cap.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(e)(3)

---

## SECTION 7 — MANNER OF SALE — AFFILIATES ONLY (Rule 144(f))

*Applies to sales for the account of an affiliate. Determines HOW the sale may be effected. Restricts solicitation and payments.*

---

### 7.1 — Permitted Manners of Sale (Rule 144(f)(1))

The securities must be sold in **one of the following manners**:

- [ ] **(i) Brokers' transactions** within the meaning of Section 4(4) of the Act (see Section 8 below for broker conduct requirements)
- [ ] **(ii) Transactions directly with a market maker** (as defined in Exchange Act § 3(a)(38))
- [ ] **(iii) Riskless principal transactions**, provided:
  - **(A)** Offsetting trades executed at the same price (exclusive of explicitly disclosed markup/markdown, commission equivalent, or fee)
  - **(B)** Transaction is permitted to be reported as riskless under the rules of a self-regulatory organization (SRO)
  - **(C)** Requirements of paragraphs (g)(2), (g)(3), and (g)(4) are met (broker conduct — Section 8 below)

> **Riskless principal definition:** A principal transaction where, after having received from a customer an order to buy, a broker or dealer purchases the security as principal in the market to satisfy the order to buy; or after having received from a customer an order to sell, sells the security as principal to the market to satisfy the order to sell.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(f)(1)

---

### 7.2 — Prohibited Conduct (Rule 144(f)(2))

The affiliate selling the securities **shall NOT**:

- [ ] **(i)** Solicit or arrange for the solicitation of orders to buy the securities in anticipation of or in connection with the transaction
- [ ] **(ii)** Make any payment in connection with the offer or sale of the securities to any person **other than the broker or dealer who executes the order**

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(f)(2)

---

### 7.3 — Exceptions to Manner-of-Sale Requirement (Rule 144(f)(3))

The manner-of-sale requirements do NOT apply to:

- [ ] **(i)** Securities sold for the account of the estate of a deceased person or a beneficiary, **provided** the estate / beneficiary is not an affiliate of the issuer
- [ ] **(ii)** Debt securities

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(f)(3)

---

## SECTION 8 — BROKERS' TRANSACTIONS (Rule 144(g))

*Defines what counts as a "brokers' transaction" for Section 4(4) and Rule 144(f)(1)(i) purposes. The broker must conduct itself within tightly defined parameters.*

---

### 8.1 — Broker Conduct Requirements (Rule 144(g)(1)–(4))

A "brokers' transaction" under Section 4(4) for Rule 144 purposes is one in which the broker:

- [ ] **(1)** Does no more than **execute the order** as agent for the seller's account
- [ ] **(2)** Receives **no more than the usual and customary broker's commission**
- [ ] **(3) Neither solicits nor arranges for the solicitation** of customer orders to buy the securities in connection with the transaction. **Permitted exceptions:**
  - **(i)** Inquiries by the broker of **other brokers or dealers** who have indicated an interest in the securities within the preceding **60 days**
  - **(ii)** Inquiries by the broker of his **customers** who have indicated an **unsolicited bona fide interest** in the securities within the preceding **10 business days**
  - **(iii)** Broker publishes bid and ask quotations in an **inter-dealer quotation system** that are incident to maintaining a bona fide inter-dealer market for the broker's own account, AND the broker has published bona fide bid/ask quotations on at least 12 days within the preceding 30 calendar days with no more than 4 business days in succession without two-way quotations
  - **(iv)** Broker publishes bid and ask quotations in an **alternative trading system** (as defined in 17 CFR § 242.300), provided the broker has published bona fide bid/ask quotations on each of the last 12 business days
- [ ] Broker should retain written evidence of indications of bona fide unsolicited interest by customers at the time received
- [ ] **(4)** After **reasonable inquiry**, is not aware of circumstances indicating that the seller is an underwriter with respect to the securities, or that the transaction is part of a distribution of securities of the issuer. Broker is deemed aware of any facts in the Form 144 notice (if filed)

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(g)(1)–(4)

---

### 8.2 — Broker's Reasonable Inquiry Items (Rule 144(g)(4)(ii))

The reasonable inquiry should include but is not limited to inquiry as to:

- [ ] **(a)** The length of time the securities have been held by the seller (with physical inspection of the securities if practicable)
- [ ] **(b)** The nature of the transaction in which the securities were acquired by the seller
- [ ] **(c)** The amount of securities of the same class sold during the past 3 months by all persons whose sales are required to be aggregated under paragraph (e)
- [ ] **(d)** Whether the seller intends to sell additional securities of the same class through any other means
- [ ] **(e)** Whether the seller has solicited or made any arrangement for the solicitation of buy orders in connection with the proposed sale
- [ ] **(f)** Whether the seller has made any payment to any other person in connection with the proposed sale
- [ ] **(g)** The number of shares or other units of the class outstanding, or the relevant trading volume

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(g)(4)(ii)

---

## SECTION 9 — FORM 144 NOTICE OF PROPOSED SALE (Rule 144(h))

*Required for affiliate sales above threshold. Filing must precede or accompany the sale.*

---

### 9.1 — Filing Triggers and Mechanics

- [ ] **(1) Reporting issuers** — if the issuer is, and has been for at least 90 days immediately before the sale, subject to Exchange Act § 13 or § 15(d) reporting, AND the amount of securities to be sold in reliance on Rule 144 during any 3-month period exceeds **5,000 shares (or other units) OR has an aggregate sale price exceeding $50,000**, a notice on **Form 144** must be filed **electronically** with the SEC
- [ ] **(2) Non-reporting issuers** — same threshold (5,000 units or $50,000) triggers a Form 144 filing — **three copies** filed with the Commission
- [ ] **(3) Timing and signature** — Form 144 must be:
  - Signed by the person for whose account the securities are to be sold
  - Transmitted for filing **concurrently with** either:
    - The placing of the sale order with the broker, OR
    - The direct execution with a market maker
- [ ] The filing of Form 144 does NOT preclude SEC action with respect to the contemplated sale
- [ ] The person filing must have a **bona fide intention to sell** the securities referred to in the notice **within a reasonable time** after filing

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(h); Form 144 (17 CFR § 239.144)

---

## SECTION 10 — SHELL COMPANY CARVE-OUT (Rule 144(i))

*Rule 144 is UNAVAILABLE for resales of securities initially issued by shell companies — until specific restoration conditions are met.*

---

### 10.1 — Shell Company Unavailability (Rule 144(i)(1))

Rule 144 is NOT available for the resale of securities initially issued by:

- [ ] **(i)** An issuer (other than a business combination related shell company as defined in Rule 405, or an asset-backed issuer under Item 1101(b) of Regulation AB) that has:
  - **(A)** No or nominal operations; AND
  - **(B)** Either:
    - **(1)** No or nominal assets; OR
    - **(2)** Assets consisting solely of cash and cash equivalents; OR
    - **(3)** Assets consisting of any amount of cash and cash equivalents and nominal other assets
- [ ] **(ii)** An issuer that has been at any time previously an issuer described in paragraph (i)(1)(i) — i.e., the shell-status taint follows the entity

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(i)(1)

---

### 10.2 — Restoration After Ceasing to Be a Shell Company (Rule 144(i)(2)–(3))

Notwithstanding (i)(1), Rule 144 becomes available again if the issuer:

- [ ] Previously was a shell described in (i)(1)(i) but **has ceased to be a shell**; AND
- [ ] Is subject to Exchange Act § 13 or § 15(d) reporting; AND
- [ ] Has filed all required reports during the preceding 12 months (or shorter required period), **other than 8-Ks**; AND
- [ ] Has filed current "**Form 10 information**" with the Commission reflecting its non-shell status; AND
- [ ] **At least ONE YEAR has elapsed** since the date the issuer filed the Form 10 information

#### Definition of "Form 10 information"

- [ ] The information required by Form 10 (or Form 20-F for foreign private issuers) to register each class of securities under the Exchange Act
- [ ] May be provided in any SEC filing
- [ ] Deemed filed when the initial filing is made

> **Legal advisor note (de-SPAC and former shells):** This subsection is the operative rule for resale paths after a de-SPAC merger. Securities issued by a former shell company (including the post-merger surviving company that absorbed the SPAC) are subject to (i)(1)(ii) and cannot be resold under Rule 144 until the (i)(2) conditions are met — including the 1-year wait after Form 10 information is filed. This is materially different from the standard 6-month / 1-year holding period.

**Reference:** 17 CFR § 230.144(i)(2)–(3)

## SECTION 11 — RULE 144 AND OTHER REGULATORY OVERLAYS

*Rule 144 satisfaction is necessary but not always sufficient. Other federal and state law overlays apply.*

---

### 11.1 — State Securities Laws (Blue Sky)

- [ ] Rule 144 is a federal safe harbor only — it does NOT preempt state securities laws
- [ ] Some states have parallel resale exemptions for Rule 144 sales; others require separate notice filings or fees
- [ ] Confirm state-by-state requirements for the seller's residence AND the buyer's residence

### 11.2 — Anti-Fraud Provisions

- [ ] Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) / Rule 10b-5 of the Exchange Act apply independently of Rule 144
- [ ] A Rule 144 sale made while in possession of material non-public information may still constitute insider trading
- [ ] Affiliates should clear sales through the issuer's insider trading policy (10b5-1 plan or window-period framework) regardless of Rule 144 status

### 11.3 — Section 16 (for officers, directors, and 10% holders of reporting issuers)

- [ ] If the issuer has a class of equity securities registered under § 12 of the Exchange Act, officers / directors / 10% holders are subject to § 16(a) reporting (Forms 3, 4, 5) and § 16(b) short-swing profit recovery
- [ ] Rule 144 sales count for both § 16(a) reporting and § 16(b) matching purposes

### 11.4 — Sales by Persons Acting on Behalf of Affiliates

- [ ] Rule 144 applies to sales of restricted or non-restricted securities **for the account of** an affiliate (trustees, executors, brokers selling on commission, etc.)
- [ ] The seller (the person actually selling) is the "underwriter" being protected by Rule 144 — but the conditions are applied based on the **affiliate**'s status (holding period, volume, current info)
- [ ] All filings (Form 144) name the affiliate for whose account the sale is made

---

## SECTION 12 — COMMON FAILURE MODES

*Frequent ways Rule 144 reliance fails. Review before any sale.*

---

- [ ] **Treating Rule 144 as automatic** — Rule 144 conditions must be affirmatively satisfied at the time of each sale. Holding period clearing is not enough if current public information has lapsed
- [ ] **Forgetting the 90-day affiliate tail** — recent ex-affiliates (within 90 days) remain subject to the affiliate path
- [ ] **Aggregation oversight** — failure to aggregate sales across household members, controlled entities, concerted-action groups, donees, and trust settlors leads to inadvertent volume cap breaches
- [ ] **Tacking errors on conversion / amendment** — assuming the holding period tacks back when amendment consideration was paid (it does not)
- [ ] **Pre-arranged sales** — sales pre-arranged with a specific buyer at a specific price typically fail the manner-of-sale and brokers'-transaction requirements
- [ ] **Form 144 timing** — filing after the sale, or with an unrealistic "intention to sell," vitiates the filing
- [ ] **Shell-company taint missed** — securities of former shell companies cannot use Rule 144 until the (i)(2) conditions are met, regardless of how long the holder has owned the securities
- [ ] **Non-reporting issuer 15c2-11 information lapse** — affiliate sales completely blocked if the issuer's public information is not current
- [ ] **Pledge structure errors** — non-recourse pledges restart the holding period for the pledgee
- [ ] **Reg S secondary tacking confusion** — domestic-issuer equity acquired offshore under Reg S retains restricted status (Rule 905); Rule 144 holding period runs from acquisition by the offshore purchaser, not from the original issuer's offering
