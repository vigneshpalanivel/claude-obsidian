# Blog Article Templates — Trioangle AI Engineering & Custom Development

> Reusable writer briefs for all 7 pillars of the Service content strategy. Fill in the `[VARIABLES]` below for any pillar/vertical/persona combination to produce a complete article brief.
>
> Reference: [[ICP - Service]] · [[Content Strategy - Service]]

> [!IMPORTANT] Four Non-Negotiable Rules
> Every template must comply with all four rules before submission.
>
> **1. Service Line Priority — AI First, Custom Dev Second** — AI Engineering (Pillar 7) is the 🥇 primary service line; custom dev is 🥈 secondary. When AI is in scope (any way), CTA defaults to the **AI Audit Sprint funnel** (Free 1-hour AI Readiness Call → Paid 2-week AI Audit Sprint, $1,500–$4,000, fee credited). Discovery Sprint is the secondary CTA, used only for explicit non-AI custom-dev briefs.
>
> **2. Existing-Operator Filter — Mandatory** — Every article brief must implicitly target a reader running an **established platform with $1M+ ARR and 12+ months operational history** (or an established offline business with proven revenue). **Pre-traction Seed/Series A startups are out of scope** — route to Product ICP. If the article framing slides into "you just raised a Seed round" or "you're building an MVP," reroute or reframe.
>
> **3. AI-Era Positioning Rule** — Never position on "we build faster with AI." Position on domain expertise, certainty of delivery, and risk reduction. Service buyers in 2026 have seen too many AI demos that delivered nothing. They are buying a named team with deep vertical domain delivery history, not speed. "Faster with AI" invites "then why are you expensive?" and loses.
>
> **4. Trust & IP Positioning Rule** — Never defend trust with promises ("we're transparent," "we communicate well," "we're senior"). Defend it with verifiable structural guarantees: Day-1 GitHub access, contractual IP transfer, 48-hour silence → 5% contract credit, pre-contract engineer introduction, AI Audit Sprint / Discovery Sprint escape hatch. Every Decision-stage CTA block must reference at least one structural guarantee — never an adjective.

> [!IMPORTANT] AI Proof-Point Caveat — Mandatory in any AI-context article
> The "800+ platforms across 150+ countries since 2015" stat counts **platform deliveries, not AI feature deliveries**. The AI Engineering service line launched in 2026; AI delivery count is far smaller and tracked separately.
>
> **In Pillar 7 articles or any AI-context paragraph in Pillars 1/3/4/5:**
> - ❌ Never write *"we've built AI features in 800+ platforms"* — that conflates platform delivery with AI delivery and misrepresents the AI track record.
> - ✅ Use *"domain knowledge from 800+ platform engagements applied to the AI integration layer."*
>
> AI-specific metrics must be sourced from the actual AI delivery count (separately tracked) and always include A/B-test framing: *"[metric] improved by [X]% in A/B test against rule-based baseline, measured over [N] weeks in production."*

> [!NOTE] Persona Abbreviations + Tier
> - 🎯 **P5** — AI-Forward Platform Operator (live established platform, skeptical of AI vendors, domain-specific AI needs) — **direct primary** (active outbound priority; recurring-revenue base)
> - 🪶 **P1** — Scaling CTO (established platform business with active users, ex-FAANG, deeply technical) — **inbound only** (single-project shaped; no outbound motions)
> - 🪶 **P2** — Non-Technical Founder-Operator (founder of an *established operating business* with 12+ months revenue traction; the original technical co-founder left or never existed; **NOT a freshly-funded Seed-stage MVP-builder**) — **inbound only**
> - 🤝 **P3** — Enterprise Digital Buyer (Head of Digital, legacy modernization, RFP-driven) — **channel-partner enablement only** (no direct outbound until partner program is live)
> - 🤝 **P4** — MENA Relationship Buyer (UAE/KSA established operator, relationship-first, Vision 2030) — **channel-partner & warm-referral only**
>

## Global Writer Briefs — Apply to All Templates

### Pre-Brief Mandatory Checklist (every article, every cluster)

Before drafting, confirm all four:
1. **Existing-Operator Filter check** — Is the implied reader an established platform operator ($1M+ ARR, 12+ months operational history)? If no → reroute to Product ICP. If unclear → reframe.
2. **Service Line Priority check** — Is AI in scope (any way — vertical AI competitor pressure, AI feature topic, AI-mention paragraph)? If yes → CTA defaults to AI Audit Sprint funnel (Free Readiness Call → Paid Audit Sprint). If no → CTA defaults to Discovery Sprint or Technical Architecture Review.
3. **Persona Tier check** — Is the article targeted at 🎯 P5 (direct primary), 🪶 P1/P2 (inbound only — no outbound motions), or 🤝 P3/P4 (channel-partner enablement)? Tier dictates CTA structure and tone.
4. **AI Proof-Point Caveat check (AI-context articles only)** — No "we've built AI features in 800+ platforms" language anywhere. Use *"domain knowledge from 800+ platform engagements applied to the AI integration layer."*

### Proof & Credibility (every Decision / Consideration article)

- Every Decision and Consideration-stage article must include at least one `[PROOF_METRIC]` from the Part 10 Proof Signal Mapping table in the conclusion paragraph, immediately before the CTA.
- The "800+ platforms across 150+ countries since 2015" stat is self-reported pending internal audit ([[ICP - Service]] validation warning). Do not cite it in high-stakes RFP responses or enterprise proposals without verifying the underlying figure. For regular blog copy, use it with confidence but never as the *only* proof point — always pair with a specific named outcome.
- **AI-context articles:** Never cite 800+ as an AI delivery count. AI metrics must use A/B-test framing: *"[metric] improved by X% vs rule-based baseline, measured over N weeks in production."*
- Proof metrics must be specific: "$X saved," "Y% uptime," "N-week delivery," not "faster," "cheaper," "reliable."

### Structural Trust (every Pillar 2 + every Decision-stage article)

- Before the CTA, name at least one structural guarantee from the `[STRUCTURAL_GUARANTEE]` list. This is non-negotiable in Pillar 2 and Decision-stage content.
- Never use the words "transparent," "communicative," or "senior team" without immediately pairing them with a verifiable mechanism (GitHub access, contract clause, escrow, AI service isolation pattern).
- AI-context Decision articles: structural guarantee must include the AI-specific guarantee — *"AI features run as isolated services with automatic fallback to rule-based defaults; below-threshold model performance triggers automatic revert."*

### Objection Pre-Emption

- Identify the 1–2 most likely objections for the article's persona from the Part 10 Objection Handler Mapping table. Embed the response language naturally in the body — never as a standalone FAQ or Q&A block.
- For articles targeting post-failed-agency prospects (#79, vendor-fatigue context), lead with empathy, acknowledge the pain, and do **not** offer a paid-Sprint CTA — only a no-pitch architecture review.
- For articles where the prospect is comparing against AI SaaS (AWS Personalize, Vertex AI, Pinecone+OpenAI): embed honest build-vs-buy framing — *"sometimes the SaaS is the right answer, and we'll tell you so."* Differentiator is honesty, not capability claims.

### CTA Compliance Check

Before submitting any draft, verify:
1. The CTA matches the article's persona-tier per Part 3 CTA Strategy (wrong CTA on right article = wasted traffic).
2. **Founder-Operator awareness articles** (#45, #46, #47, #77) use the **free 20-min fit call** CTA, not a paid Sprint — qualification comes first (the call confirms the Existing-Operator Filter before any Sprint upgrade).
3. **Established-operator playbook articles** (#39, #40, #43) use the paid Sprint CTA (AI Audit Sprint when AI angle in scope, Discovery Sprint otherwise). Established-operator scale is implied by the article context.
4. **Enterprise articles** (Pillar 5) end with the **dual-CTA block** — Partner CTA (lead) + Buyer CTA (secondary, soft, partner-routed). Both 80% cost anchor and 16-week delivery anchor required before each CTA.
5. **MENA articles** (Pillar 6) use the relationship-first CTA — no forms, no response-SLA language, no pricing. Pillar 6 is channel-partner & warm-referral enablement; it does not drive direct conversion.
6. **AI-Forward articles** (Pillar 7) use the **two-step funnel** — Awareness articles route to free AI Readiness Call; Decision articles route directly to paid AI Audit Sprint. Never use "AI transformation," "strategy engagement," or "train a custom LLM."
7. `‡`-tagged articles are scheduled for *publishing* normally but **withheld from outbound (Channel 4 / cold email)** until the matching established-operator `[TRIGGER_EVENT]` is confirmed for the specific prospect. Old `†` Seed/Series A funding triggers are retired.
8. AI scope routing default: if the article touches AI in any way (Pillar 7, AI mention in Pillar 1/3/4/5, vertical AI use cases), default the primary CTA to the AI Audit Sprint funnel — even for P1/P2 inbound articles.

### AI-Era Compliance Check

- No headline or CTA positions Trioangle on "faster with AI" or "AI-powered development speed" — violates the ICP positioning rule.
- If the article mentions AI coding tools (Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code), acknowledge what they do well before documenting the domain-expertise gap. Dismissive framing loses P5 immediately.
- AI feature claims must cite A/B test framing: "[metric] improved by X% vs rule-based baseline, measured over N weeks in production." No vibe claims.
- Build-vs-buy AI articles (Cluster 7.4) must include a "use the SaaS when X" honest paragraph — Trioangle's differentiator is honesty about when the SaaS wins, which earns the right-fit deals where custom integration + vertical tuning is the actual moat.

### Channel-Partner Discipline (Pillars 5 & 6 only)

- Pillar 5 (Enterprise) articles end with dual CTA: Partner CTA (lead) + Buyer CTA (secondary, partner-routed soft ask). Pivot framing toward partner consultancies recommending Trioangle to their enterprise clients.
- Pillar 6 (MENA) articles drive Gulf-market topical authority for accelerator partners (Hub71/DIFC/Flat6Labs/STV) and warm-referral sources to recommend. They do not drive direct conversion. No transactional language anywhere.

1. **Add a row** to the Vertical Variable Table above with platform type, 2–3 domain edge cases, and reference clients.

3. **Produce T1** (Cluster 3.1 vertical platform architecture article) — supporting custom-dev SEO authority. Mandatory cross-link to the T14 AI article.
4. **Produce T15** if the vertical has obvious AI-stability / production-integration nuances (Cluster 7.3).
5. **Optional T11** if the vertical has enterprise-buyer relevance (Cluster 5.2 channel-partner enablement).
6. **Cross-link** the new T1 article to all relevant Cluster 3.2 core component articles per Part 6 Rule 6, and to the new T14 AI article per Part 6 Rule 9.

Minimum of 2 articles per new vertical (**T14 first + T1 second**). Scale up from there based on keyword volume and pipeline signal. **Do not start with T1 alone** — that defaults the new vertical into the secondary (custom-dev) motion and misses the primary (AI) motion.
