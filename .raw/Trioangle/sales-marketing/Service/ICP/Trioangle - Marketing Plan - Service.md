# 📣 Marketing Plan — Trioangle Service ICP (AI-First)

> [!NOTE] Executive Summary
> This is the marketing operating plan for [[Trioangle - ICP - Service]] — the Service ICP scoped to **established platform operators ($1M+ ARR, 12+ months operational)** who need to embed AI features into an already-running platform. Marketing's job in this plan is **not** to "build awareness for Trioangle" — it is to manufacture **qualified Persona-5 inbound and warm channel-partner referrals at the volume Track 2 needs to compound**: 4–6 active clients in Year 1 → 12–18 by Year 3, with each client buying 2–4 AI feature projects per year.
>
> **🥇 Primary marketing motion — AI Engineering demand generation aimed at Persona 5.** AI-language outbound, AI-search-optimized content, AI Engineer World's Fair / Latent Space community presence, and case-study production for AI features shipped in domain verticals. Lead magnet: free **AI Readiness Call**. Pre-engagement product the funnel converts to: paid **AI Audit Sprint** ($1,500–$4,000, credited).
>
> **🥈 Secondary marketing motion — channel-partner enablement (P3 Enterprise / P4 MENA) and inbound capture (P1 Scaling CTO / P2 Non-Technical Founder-Operator).** No outbound budget. Marketing produces enablement assets for channel partners; SEO + Clutch capture inbound P1/P2 demand opportunistically. Custom-dev marketing is a **support function for AI deals already in motion**, not an independent acquisition motion.
>
> **What this plan deliberately does NOT do:** generic awareness PR · brand-anchor "trust us, we're senior" content · paid Seed/Series A startup-targeting ads (out of ICP) · "AI transformation" deck production · retainer-shaped marketing-services pricing models. Each is justified inline.

> [!IMPORTANT] Service Line Priority — AI First, Custom Dev Second
> Per [[Trioangle - ICP - Service]] §Service Line Priority, AI Engineering is **primary** and Custom Web/Mobile Dev is **secondary**. This plan inherits that priority absolutely:
> - Every campaign brief leads with the AI angle unless the inbound prospect explicitly arrived with a non-AI rebuild scope.
> - Every paid dollar defaults to a P5/AI campaign before a custom-dev campaign — see Budget Allocation.
> - Every piece of content links primarily to the AI Audit Sprint CTA; Discovery Sprint CTA is the secondary surface, used only on custom-dev pillar pages.
> - Every marketing KPI rolls up AI-sourced pipeline first; custom-dev pipeline is reported separately and is not a primary objective.
>
> **The reason for the asymmetry:** custom dev is commoditized in 2026 (vibe-coding tools, nearshore agencies, AI coding assistants). Domain-specific AI engineering applied to verticals Trioangle has already shipped platforms in since 2015 is the only software-services wedge where the delivery history compounds into a durable moat. Marketing budget that doesn't sit on that wedge is leaking margin.

> [!IMPORTANT] Persona Tiering — Direct vs Channel-Partner vs Inbound-Only
> Per [[Trioangle - ICP - Service]] §Persona Tiering and [[Trioangle Growth Plan]] Track 2 activation, the five Service personas are NOT pursued equally by marketing:
>
> | Persona | Marketing Treatment | Outbound Budget | Inbound Capture |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | **P5 — AI-Forward Platform Operator** | 🎯 **Direct primary** | Yes — Channel 4 outbound, paid LinkedIn, AI-search SEO | Pillar 7 + AI Readiness Call |
> | **P3 — Enterprise Digital Buyer** | 🤝 **Channel-partner enablement only** | No direct outbound. Marketing produces enablement assets *for the partner*. | Clutch / G2 / RFP-response collateral |
> | **P4 — MENA Relationship Buyer** | 🤝 **Channel-partner & warm-referral only** | No direct outbound. Marketing supplies Arabic/MENA-localized one-pagers + reference cards. | In-person referral; LinkedIn presence is supportive |
> | **P1 — Scaling CTO** | 🪶 **Inbound only** | No outbound spend | SEO long-tail + Clutch + LinkedIn organic |
> | **P2 — Non-Technical Founder-Operator** | 🪶 **Inbound only** | No outbound spend | SEO long-tail + Clutch + founder peer referrals |
>
> **Reading rule:** if a marketing tactic in this plan does not eventually drive (a) qualified P5 inbound or outbound replies, (b) channel partner pipeline (P3/P4), or (c) inbound conversion of P1/P2, it is out of scope and should be cut at the next planning cycle. No "general brand" activity passes that filter.

## 🎯 Marketing Mission & Operating Principles

### Mission
Make **Persona 5 inbound + qualified channel-partner referrals** the cheapest, fastest-converting source of pipeline for Trioangle's AI Engineering service line — and, secondarily, recover Persona 1 / Persona 2 inbound at zero outbound cost via SEO + review-platform compounding.

### Operating principles

1. **AI-first, every artifact.** Pillar messages, paid ads, lead magnets, event sponsorships, and case studies all default to the AI angle. Custom-dev marketing surfaces only behind specific custom-dev pillar pages and inbound flows. See §Positioning & Messaging.
2. **No abstract brand campaigns.** Every campaign has a defined persona, a defined CTA (AI Readiness Call, AI Audit Sprint, Discovery Sprint, channel-partner intro), and a defined attribution path. No "awareness" line items in the budget.
3. **Buy domain proof, not awareness.** Marketing budget over-indexes on **case-study production, vertical-specific deep dives, and named client references** — the assets that buyers in this ICP actually convert on per [[Trioangle - ICP - Service]] §Diversified Social Proof. Generic capabilities decks are not produced.
4. **Marketing exists to feed Track 2 cadence, not vanity metrics.** Per [[Trioangle Growth Plan]], Track 2 needs 4–6 active clients in Year 1 buying 2–4 projects/year each. Marketing reports against active-client volume + project-cadence, not impressions, MQLs, or CTR. See §KPIs.
5. **Output-based pricing extends to marketing operations.** Per memory: Trioangle does not run retainers. Marketing vendor relationships (PR, paid agency, design) are scoped as deliverable-based engagements with named outputs and fixed prices — not monthly retainers. No exceptions.
6. **Honest disqualification beats pressured conversion.** Marketing copy and CTAs should screen *out* unqualified inbound (pre-traction startups, sub-$10k budgets, "we'll build with Copilot" posture) — not compress them into the funnel. Disqualifying language is a feature, not a bug. See §Positioning Rule on AI Era.
7. **Never defend trust with promises; defend it with structural guarantees.** Per [[Trioangle - ICP - Service]] §Trust & IP Positioning Rule, every external-facing marketing artifact references contractual mechanisms (Day-1 GitHub access, IP transfer clause, 48-hour comm SLA with credit, pre-contract engineer intro, Discovery Sprint escape hatch) — never "we pride ourselves on transparent communication."

### Banned language across all marketing surfaces

These phrases are forbidden in any external Service ICP marketing artifact — they trigger the exact skepticism Persona 5 (and burned P1/P2) is hardened against:

- "AI transformation" / "AI strategy session" / "AI roadmap consulting"
- "We can train a custom LLM for your use case" (overengineered; irrelevant for most platform AI work)
- "Demo of our AI platform" (we don't sell a platform)
- "Faster development with AI" / "AI-powered development" (commoditized; invites the "then why are you expensive?" loss)
- "We pride ourselves on transparent communication" / "high-quality code" / "senior engineers" (every losing vendor said the same — replace with the structural guarantee)
- "Powered by AI" decoration on existing features
- "We've built AI features in 800+ platforms" (conflates platform delivery count with AI delivery count — see [[Trioangle - ICP - Service]] §Proof-Point Caveat)
- Any exact "800+" count until the internal delivery audit is confirmed — see 800+ Pre-Publish Gate in §Positioning above
- "Scrappy startup team that moves fast" (loses P3 enterprise; channel partners reject it)

### Disqualification copy (used on landing pages and ads)

Counterintuitive but high-conversion: marketing copy that names who is **not** a fit increases close rate by raising perceived bar and pre-screening tire-kickers. Standard disqualification banner used on AI Audit Sprint and Service landing pages:

> *"This service is for established platform operators with $1M+ ARR and 12+ months of production traction. If you're pre-launch or pre-revenue, our [clone script catalog] is the right starting point — not this. If your timeline is 'AI live in 3 weeks for $5k,' we will not be a fit."*

The Product ICP link is explicit. Routing the wrong-fit prospect to the right product is correct, and is also lower-pain than letting them enter the AI funnel and churn.

## 🪜 Marketing Funnel Architecture

The Service marketing funnel maps to the ICP's two-step pre-engagement product structure (free AI Readiness Call → paid AI Audit Sprint → signed AI feature build). Marketing owns Stages 1–3; Sales owns Stages 4–5.

| Stage | Buyer State | Marketing Asset | Conversion Target | Marketing Owns? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Reach** | Unaware / aware-of-problem | SEO Pillar 7, AI-search snippets, LinkedIn thought-leadership posts, podcast appearances, conference talks | Visit / impression | ✅ |
| **2. Engage** | Researching AI options for their vertical | Pillar 7 deep-dives, vertical AI feature playbooks, AI Audit Sprint landing page, AI Readiness Call lead magnet, newsletter | Email or LinkedIn capture, AI Readiness Call booking | ✅ |
| **3. Qualify** | Considering paid AI Audit Sprint or Discovery Sprint | Sprint landing page, sample audit deliverable, named-client reference packets, pre-call qualification form | Sprint booked | ✅ Marketing initiates → Sales closes |
| **4. Convert** | Sprint delivered, scoping full project | Sales-led; marketing supplies enablement assets (proposal templates, case studies, ROI calculator) | Signed feature build | Sales |
| **5. Expand** | First feature shipped; quarterly roadmap review | Customer-marketing assets: case-study-as-deliverable, executive briefing decks, AI roadmap one-pagers | Follow-on project (2–4/year cadence) | Sales + Customer Marketing |

### Conversion benchmarks (priors to instrument and revise)

These are **starting-assumption benchmarks**, not validated numbers. Re-set after one full quarter of attribution:

| Stage Transition | Benchmark | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Reach → Engage | 1.5–3% (organic SEO), 0.5–1.5% (paid LinkedIn) | Standard B2B technical-buyer engagement rates |
| Engage → AI Readiness Call booked | 8–15% of Engaged | Free, low-commitment offer; technical landing page conversion priors |
| AI Readiness Call → AI Audit Sprint booked | 25–40% | Audit is paid; selection bias is high — but the call is also the qualification gate |
| AI Audit Sprint → Signed feature build | 50–70% | Sprint mechanics are designed to close. Buyers self-select by paying |
| First build → Second build (12-month window) | 60–75% (P5 target) | Per [[Trioangle Growth Plan]] §Repeat Business Mechanics, P5 buys 2–4 projects/year — but only if the first ships clean and the quarterly review cadence is maintained |

> **Read the funnel against revenue math.** To hit the Year 1 target of 4–6 active Track 2 clients per [[Trioangle Growth Plan]], the funnel needs ~10 AI Readiness Calls per quarter at the upper end of these benchmarks. That is the marketing volume target — not "1,000 leads/month" or impression targets.

Channel partners with existing P3/P4 relationships are the only viable activation path; marketing's job is to make those partners productive.

### Quarterly reallocation rule

After each quarter's attribution review:
- Cut the lowest-performing two line items by 50%.
- Reallocate to the highest-performing two by 25% each.
- Hold the rest constant.
- Cap any single line item at 35% of total spend (avoid over-concentration).

This is a deliberate "rolling reallocation" — not "set the annual budget at year-start and stick to it." Marketing fit is being discovered, not executed against a known formula.

## 📌 Summary — What Marketing Is and Is Not Doing

**Marketing IS doing:**
- Building Persona-5 inbound + outbound at the volume Track 2 needs (4–6 active clients Y1 → 12–18 Y3, 2–4 projects/year each).
- Producing the Proof Point Library (case studies + NPS + benchmarks) that makes the messaging framework convert.
- Running Channel 4 trigger-based outbound for P5.
- Producing channel-partner enablement assets for P3 (Enterprise) and P4 (MENA).
- Capturing P1 / P2 opportunistically through SEO + Clutch + founder peer-network.
- Compounding Pillar 7 + AI-search SEO as the long-term inbound moat.
- Reporting against active-client + AI Audit Sprint volume — not impressions, MQLs, or share-of-voice.

**Marketing is NOT doing:**
- Generic brand awareness campaigns.
- Direct outbound to P3 (Enterprise) or P4 (MENA) — those are channel-partner motions only.
- Targeting pre-traction Seed/Series A startups (out of ICP — they belong in the Product ICP funnel).
- Marketing custom dev as an independent acquisition motion — custom dev surfaces inside AI deals or via inbound only.
- Selling Trioangle as "faster developers because of AI" — that loses the AI-era positioning rule.
- Operating retainer-shaped marketing pricing or "AI subscription" positioning.
- Adding tools, agencies, or headcount that don't trace to Track 2 active-client outcomes.

When in doubt, the test is: **does this marketing activity produce qualified P5 inbound, channel-partner pipeline, or P1/P2 inbound conversion at a CAC that supports the Growth Plan revenue math?** If not, it doesn't ship.
