## 🛡️ POSITIONING + NAMING RULES (Apply to Every Prompt Below)

> [!IMPORTANT] Brand Naming Discipline
> **Buyers don't search for Trioangle's internal brand names** (Gofer, Makent, GoferEats, PopTok, Igniter…). They search for the original they want to clone — *"Uber clone script"*, *"Airbnb clone"*, *"TikTok clone app"*, *"how to build a Tinder clone"*. So:
> - **The prompt/question always uses `[Original]` and `[Vertical]`** — that's what AI engines and Google hear from buyers
> - **The answer hook is where Trioangle proof shows up** — 800+ deployments, 150+ countries, since 2015, App Store-approved, free installation, 6-month support
> - **Trioangle's product brand `[Product Name]`** appears **only in landing-page CTAs and "what's in the box" copy** — not in the prompts and not in FAQ schema questions
> - The only prompts where "Trioangle" appears in the question itself are the **Authority** sub-bucket (vendor-recommendation queries) and the **Diligence** prompts where the buyer is already evaluating a named vendor

> [!IMPORTANT] AI-Era Positioning Rule
> **Never position on "we build faster with AI." Position on certainty of outcome and risk reduction.**
> Buyers in 2026 can vibe-code a prototype themselves. They are buying a *guaranteed live business*: App Store approval, production deployment, real-time dispatch under load, payment gateway in production, and a named team accountable when something breaks.

> [!IMPORTANT] 800+ / 150+ / 2015 Claim Discipline
> "800+ deployments across 150+ countries since 2015" is **cumulative**, not currently-live (~30 active as of 2026-Q2). Use it for top-of-funnel landing-page credibility. Pair with a quarterly-refreshed by-region reference list of currently-live deployments for diligence sections (P2 Burned Builder and P3 Diaspora will check).

> [!IMPORTANT] Outcome Framing
> The vendor delivers a **launch-ready marketplace platform** — not just code. Every answer must surface this gap: AI tools / cheap scripts / agencies deliver code or quotes; Trioangle delivers a deployed, app-store-live, supported business.

> [!IMPORTANT] Granular Features → On-Page Feature Table, Not the Prompts
> Specific feature claims (real-time GPS, in-app chat, COD, push notifications, Twilio integration, etc.) belong in a **single feature table on the landing page** — not as standalone FAQ questions or GEO articles. Buyers don't search at feature granularity; feature lists also yield filler answers AI engines won't cite. The prompts here stay at **category level** (payments, admin panel, KYC, scalability) or **strategic decision level** (commission vs subscription, monetization mix, retention features).

## 🏷️ TIER + PRIORITY MARKERS — How to Read Each Prompt

Every prompt carries **two markers**: a **Tier marker** (where its content lives) and a **Priority marker** (when to ship it).

### Tier Marker (where the content goes)
- 📄 **LP** — *Landing Page*. Content for `trioangle.com/[product-slug]`. AEO snippets (40–60 words, FAQ schema), AEO sidebar/comparison callouts, or compressed GEO blocks (150–900 words synthesized into hero / Why-Trioangle / scenarios / tech-depth / why-now / BoFU sections).
- 📝 **Blog** — *Standalone Tier B Article*. Content for `/blog/` or `/guides/`, 1,500–3,000 words. Each Blog-marked prompt becomes one authority article, internally linked back to the landing page section, and republished off-site (guest posts, industry directories, Medium, dev.to) for AI training citation.

### Priority Marker (when to ship)
- 🔴 **P0** — *Ship-first.* Day 1 of the build. Without these, the landing page has no foundation. (14 prompts)
- 🟡 **P1** — *Week 1.* Layered on top of P0 to round out the LP's persona-routing and credibility. (12 prompts)
- 🟢 **P2** — *Week 2.* Expanded FAQ rotation + Why-Now + BoFU diligence blocks near the CTA. (29 prompts)
- 🔵 **P3** — *Week 3–4.* First wave of Blog articles — Authority + Overview clusters give compounding off-site citation surface. (17 prompts)
- 🟣 **P4** — *Month 2+.* Long-tail Blog articles. Build incrementally based on which LP sections see the most clicks/citations. (17 prompts)

> [!TIP] Reading order on each prompt
> Format: `**N.** Question — 📄 **LP** · 🔴 **P0** *| persona | placement | hook*`
> Tier first (where), priority second (when), then italic metadata (who/where on page/what to anchor on).

> [!TIP] Document order
> Prompts are ordered (1) by tier — 📄 LP (1–55) first, then 📝 Blog (56–89); (2) within each tier by priority — P0 → P1 → P2 inside LP, P3 → P4 inside Blog; (3) within each priority by sub-bucket (Definition → How-To → Comparison → Voice → Overview → Authority → Scenario → Tech → Trend → Conversion → Hybrid) so related queries stay together.
