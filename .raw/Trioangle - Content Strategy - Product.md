## 1. Strategic Overview

### Business Context
Trioangle Technologies sells readymade clone scripts (Uber, Airbnb, UberEats, Gojek, Handyman, etc.) to **non-technical startup founders** who need to launch fast at 90% lower cost than custom development. The primary content goal is to **capture high-intent organic search traffic**, build topical authority, and nurture leads through the research-heavy buying cycle (2–10 weeks).

### Target Audience (from [[ICP - Product]])
| Persona | Description | Content Preference | Decision Speed |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Visionary Velocity Founder** | Solo founder, 25-35, spots local gaps | YouTube demos, comparison blogs | Fast (<20 days) |
| **Burned Builder** | Failed past attempt, 30-45, highly skeptical | Reviews, testimonials, support proof | Slow (30-60 days) |
| **Diaspora Entrepreneur** | Lives abroad, builds for home country, 28-40 | Case studies, community recs | Moderate (20-40 days) |
| **Small Business Digitalizer** | Existing offline business, 35-55, losing share | ROI calculators, admin walkthroughs | Slow (30-60 days) |
| **Super-App Ecosystem Builder** | Validated one vertical, wants multi-service expansion, 28-45 | Live demos of all service verticals, admin panel walkthroughs | Slow (30-60 days) |
| **AI Builder Who Hit the Wall** | Technical solopreneur 22-38, vibe-coded a prototype with Cursor/Lovable/Replit, blocked at production (App Store rejection, GPS/dispatch failing at scale, payment gateway production gap) | Production-gap explainers, deployment walkthroughs, side-by-side "AI prototype vs live business" comparisons, Reddit/IndieHackers/Stack Overflow threads | Fast (<14 days) — already deep in sunk cost |

### Content Mission Statement

> [!NOTE] Mission
> *Help non-technical founders launch profitable platform businesses in their local markets — faster, cheaper, and with less risk — by providing the most actionable, trustworthy, and comprehensive content in the clone script space.*

> [!IMPORTANT] AI-Era Positioning Rule (from [[ICP - Product]])
> **Never position on "we build faster with AI." Position on certainty of outcome and risk reduction.** Buyers in 2026 can vibe-code a prototype themselves — they are not buying speed, they are buying a guaranteed live business: App Store approval, production deployment, real-time dispatch under load, and a named team accountable when something breaks. Every content piece must reflect this — headlines, CTAs, comparison framings, and proof points. "Faster with AI" invites "then why are you expensive?" and loses. "Certainty of launch" does not.

## 2. Content Verticals (Industry Categories)

Content is organized into **10 content silos** across 9 product verticals. Vertical 8 is split into two separate silos — **8a: Creator Economy / Short Video** and **8b: Dating Apps** — because they target different personas, keywords, and buyer triggers despite sharing the same product category. Each silo has its own search universe and buyer intent.

### Vertical 1: 🚕 **Ride-Hailing & Mobility**
- **Products**: Uber Clone (Gofer), GoferTow (Towing), Taxi Apps, Bike Taxi, Carpooling
- **Target Markets**: **Priority:** North America, UK, EU, Middle East (highest purchasing power + largest diaspora founder population); **Secondary:** Africa, Southeast Asia, Latin America, India Tier-2/3 cities
- **Primary Keywords**: "uber clone script", "taxi app development cost", "how to start a ride-hailing business", "taxi booking app"
- **Search Volume Tier**: HIGH (highest demand vertical)

### Vertical 2: 🏠 **Vacation Rental & Property**
- **Products**: Airbnb Clone (Makent), Boat Rental, Car Rental, Party Rental
- **Target Markets**: Tourism-heavy emerging economies, diaspora investors
- **Primary Keywords**: "airbnb clone app", "vacation rental software", "how to start a rental marketplace", "property booking script"
- **Search Volume Tier**: HIGH

### Vertical 3: 🍔 **Food & Grocery Delivery**
- **Products**: UberEats Clone, Grocery Delivery, Restaurant Ordering
- **Target Markets**: Urban emerging markets, Tier-2 cities globally
- **Primary Keywords**: "ubereats clone script", "food delivery app development", "how to build a food delivery app", "online food ordering system"
- **Search Volume Tier**: HIGH

### Vertical 4: 📱 **Multi-Service Super Apps**
- **Products**: Gojek Clone (GoferJek), All-in-One On-Demand Apps
- **Target Markets**: Southeast Asia, Africa, Middle East
- **Primary Keywords**: "gojek clone app", "multi-service app development", "super app for startups", "on-demand multi-service platform"
- **Search Volume Tier**: MEDIUM

### Vertical 5: 🔧 **Home & Handyman Services**
- **Products**: Handyman Clone (GoferHandy), Service Marketplace
- **Target Markets**: Urban markets globally, especially US/UK/Gulf for diaspora
- **Primary Keywords**: "handyman app clone", "home service marketplace", "on-demand service app", "taskrabbit clone"
- **Search Volume Tier**: MEDIUM

### Vertical 6: 📋 **Classifieds & Local Marketplace**
- **Products**: OLX Clone (PassUp), Letgo Clone, Classifieds Script
- **Target Markets**: Emerging economies where informal buying/selling is dominant (Africa, South Asia, Latin America, MENA)
- **Primary Keywords**: "olx clone script", "classifieds app development", "letgo clone", "buy sell app like craigslist", "local marketplace app"
- **Search Volume Tier**: LOW PRIORITY / BACKLOG
- **ICP Note**: Persona 1 (Visionary Velocity) and Persona 3 (Diaspora Entrepreneur) commonly target home markets with no dominant classifieds platform

### Vertical 7: 🛍️ **eCommerce Marketplace**
- **Products**: Amazon Clone, Multi-Vendor eCommerce Script
- **Target Markets**: Emerging economies building regional Amazon-alternatives; diaspora entrepreneurs building niche commerce hubs
- **Primary Keywords**: "amazon clone script", "multi-vendor marketplace script", "build ecommerce marketplace", "amazon clone app development"
- **Search Volume Tier**: MEDIUM
- **ICP Note**: Higher-budget buyers; often co-founders with existing retail relationships

### Vertical 8a: 🎬 **Creator Economy, Short Video & Social Apps**
- **Products**: TikTok Clone (PopTok), Instagram Clone, Reels/Short-Video App Script
- **Target Markets**: Emerging market content entrepreneurs, regional social platform builders, markets where TikTok has restrictions or no dominant local alternative
- **Primary Keywords**: "tiktok clone script", "tiktok clone app development", "short video app like tiktok", "instagram clone app"
- **Search Volume Tier**: HIGH (active traction — prioritise)
- **ICP Note**: Younger founders (22-32), tech-savvy, motivated by creator economy trends — strong inbound, treat as high priority.

### Vertical 8b: 💘 **Dating Apps**
- **Products**: Dating App Clone (Igniter)
- **Target Markets**: Founders targeting local/regional markets where Tinder/Bumble have low penetration or poor cultural fit
- **Primary Keywords**: "tinder clone script", "dating app development cost", "how to build a dating app like tinder", "dating app clone script"
- **Search Volume Tier**: MEDIUM-HIGH
- **ICP Note**: Founders (25-40) targeting markets where global dating apps have low reach or cultural mismatch; often niche-community or regional matchmaking platforms. *(Treated as a separate content silo — see Dating Cluster Map.)*

### Vertical 9: 📚 **E-Learning Platforms**
- **Products**: Udemy Clone, Online Course Platform Script, eLearning Marketplace
- **Target Markets**: Edtech founders, corporate training startups, regional online education platforms in emerging markets
- **Primary Keywords**: "udemy clone script", "elearning platform development", "online learning app clone", "online course platform script", "build udemy like website"
- **Search Volume Tier**: LOW PRIORITY / BACKLOG
- **ICP Note**: Higher-consideration buyers; often have existing course content or instructor network before purchasing

### Secondary Markets — Distinct Distribution Playbook

Priority markets (NA, UK, EU, UAE, KSA, Qatar) get the full paid search + LinkedIn + YouTube stack documented above. **Secondary markets (SE Asia, Africa, LATAM, India) convert through a structurally different motion** and must not inherit the priority-market playbook. From [[ICP - Product]]:

| Distribution lever | Secondary-market treatment |
|---|---|
| **Dominant channel** | WhatsApp/Telegram founder groups, Facebook diaspora groups, local meetups, referral from live deployments — paid search burns budget on low-intent traffic here |
| **Payment framing in content** | Demo must show **cash on delivery** for end-user flows; list **local gateway support** explicitly (Paystack, Flutterwave, Razorpay, PhonePe, MercadoPago, Xendit, GCash); mention **split/milestone billing** options (Wise, local bank, USDT) for the script purchase itself |
| **Language** | Bilingual content where demand is real; **local-language demo videos** signal credibility before price is ever discussed; RTL support called out for Middle East |
| **Trust signals** | **Local founder testimonials in the buyer's exact region** (Nigerian testimonial lands in Lagos; Indian testimonial lands in Bangalore — region matching is the signal, not volume); WhatsApp contact in CTAs, not web forms; same-timezone response commitments |
| **Sales motion** | CTAs route to WhatsApp intro → demo → proposal; skip the web form entirely |
| **Do NOT** | Push Clutch/G2 as primary proof in secondary-market content — these buyers weight peer referral 10x higher than review sites they do not use |

**Strategic principle:** Secondary markets are where Trioangle's 150+ country deployment history is most defensible. Content built for these markets compounds a moat no Western competitor can match — priority markets fund growth; secondary markets compound the moat. Budget editorial time accordingly.
